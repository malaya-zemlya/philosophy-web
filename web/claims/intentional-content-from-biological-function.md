What an inner state is *about* is settled by what the systems that make and use it were selected
for doing: a state means "predator overhead" if registering predators-overhead is the condition
under which the mechanisms consuming that state historically did their evolutionary work. Aboutness
is thus a *biological* category — a matter of proper function, like the heart's function of pumping
blood — rather than a matter of resemblance, convention, or interpretation (Millikan,
[1984](../sources/millikan-1984.md); a related teleological account in Dretske,
[1995](../sources/dretske-1995.md)).

**Scope.** The claim concerns *original* intentionality
([intrinsic-derived-intentionality](../concepts/intrinsic-derived-intentionality.md)): it says where unborrowed aboutness comes from, and
so answers the [problem of intentionality](../questions/intentionality.md) in the naturalist's favour. It
does not say every meaningful item gets content this way — words and maps still borrow theirs from
users. It is denied by those who hold original intentionality to be primitive, by those who ground
it in consciousness instead ([intentionality-grounded-in-phenomenality](intentionality-grounded-in-phenomenality.md)), and by
[interpretivists](../positions/intentional-stance.md), for whom no observer-independent fact fixes
content at all.

### In plain terms

Why does a bee's waggle dance "say" where the nectar is? Not because anyone reads it — because the
dance-making and dance-following machinery was kept in the species precisely for getting bees to
nectar. This claim says all mental aboutness works like that: a brain state is *about* food or
danger because detecting food or danger is the job natural selection gave the machinery that
produces and uses that state. Meaning, on this view, is as biological as digestion.
