The functional architecture associated with conscious access — a small, capacity-limited workspace
of reportable, flexibly usable representations atop a mass of automatic processing — is not an
accident of biological implementation but a solution that learning systems converge on when facing
the right computational pressures. The evidence is its unplanned emergence: language models were
never architecturally endowed with a workspace, yet one arises from next-token-prediction training
alone ([llm-global-workspace](llm-global-workspace.md), [gurnee-2026-workspace](../sources/gurnee-2026-workspace.md)). The likely pressure is
economy: a common representational format lets an intermediate result be written once and read by
many downstream processes, which any system that must chain reasoning steps and apply general
operations in arbitrary contexts stands to benefit from.

### Scope

The claim concerns the *functional organization* of access, not consciousness itself: convergence
on workspace architecture across substrates is compatible with consciousness nonetheless requiring
a particular substrate. It bears on [substrate-independence](../questions/substrate-independence.md) as evidence rather than as
an answer — it shows that at least the access-side *organization* of the mental is multiply
realized in fact, not merely realizable in principle. It would be denied by anyone who reads the
LLM workspace as an artefact of training on human-generated text — an imprint of our own cognitive
organization mirrored back — rather than an independent rediscovery of the design.

Distinct from [perceptron-realizable-in-any-substrate](perceptron-realizable-in-any-substrate.md) because that claim asserts the
multiple realizability of a fixed, given design; this one asserts something stronger and different —
that unrelated learning processes *arrive at* the design independently.

### In plain terms

Brains keep only a small spotlight of ideas ready for talking and reasoning, while everything else
runs on autopilot. AI language models, trained merely to predict text, turn out to have developed
the same spotlight arrangement on their own — nobody designed it in. The claim: this arrangement is
just what efficient thinking machines end up with, whatever they are made of. Left open is the
deeper question of whether being *conscious*, as opposed to being organized this way, also carries
over between materials.
