Even if the laws of nature and the state of the world long before your birth jointly fix everything
you will ever do, you can still act freely — because freedom, properly understood, is a matter of
*how* an action comes about, not of whether the future was open. An action is free when it flows
from the agent's own deliberation, values, and reasons-responsiveness, unimpeded by compulsion,
coercion, or manipulation; determinism threatens none of that.

This is the defining commitment of [compatibilism](../positions/compatibilism.md). It asserts a
compatibility, not the truth of determinism, and it takes no stand on whether any agent is in fact
free. It is denied by both [libertarians](../positions/libertarianism.md) and
[hard determinists](../positions/hard-determinism.md), who agree with each other that freedom requires
what determinism excludes ([alternatives-genuinely-open](alternatives-genuinely-open.md)) and disagree only about whether
we have it.
