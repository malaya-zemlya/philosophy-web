If consciousness is constituted by functional organisation, then [functionalism](../concepts/functionalism.md)
owes an observer-independent invariant that singles out the *one* correct decomposition of a system.
The reasoning is short: consciousness is determinate — there is a fact about what one is experiencing
([phenomenal determinacy](phenomenal-determinacy.md)) — whereas functional decomposition is not,
since a system can be carved into functional parts in more than one admissible way
([the relativity of functional decomposition](functional-decomposition-relative.md)). A
determinate phenomenal fact cannot be grounded in a description-relative one, so functionalism about
consciousness must name some invariant that fixes the carving independently of any observer.

This is a conditional *burden*, not an outright refutation. It leaves open that the invariant exists;
it asserts only that functionalism about consciousness is incomplete until one is named. The leading
candidate is the system's objective counterfactual causal structure.

It is denied by a functionalist who holds that the invariant is already in hand — the organisational
grain, that is, the system's objective counterfactual causal structure
([role-grain-is-organisational](role-grain-is-organisational.md), [grain](../concepts/grain.md)) — so that no new commitment is owed.

This is distinct from the demand of the [role-grain dilemma](../arguments/role-grain-dilemma.md) (a grain
coarse enough yet fine enough): that asks *where* to set the resolution of a role; this asks *what*
fixes the carving at all.

### In plain terms

Here's a bill handed to the "mind = function" view. Your conscious experience right now is perfectly
definite — there's a fact about what you're feeling. But (the worry goes) there's no single definite
way to carve a system into functional parts. If your consciousness were *made of* one such carving,
it would be as wobbly as the carving — yet it isn't. So the view owes us an account of what fixes the
*one* real organization, independent of how an observer slices things. It's a demand, not a knockout:
maybe such a fixer exists (the usual candidate is real cause-and-effect structure) — but until it's
named, the view is unfinished.
