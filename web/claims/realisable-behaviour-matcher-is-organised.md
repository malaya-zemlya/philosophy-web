Any system that could actually reproduce a mind's behaviour does so through rich internal causal
organisation — it computes, compresses, and generalises — rather than by flat stored lookup. The
reason is twofold: a flat [lookup table](../concepts/lookup-table-mind.md) is physically unrealisable
([there is nowhere to store that many rows](lookup-table-physically-unrealisable.md)), and any
representation smaller than roughly $v^n$ rows must *compute* an input's response rather than
retrieve it ([the table's size is exponential](lookup-table-size-exponential.md)). "Mere storage
with no organisation" is therefore not a kind that exists at mind-matching scale.

### Scope

The claim is about *physically realisable* systems. It does not refute the conditional
[that pure stored lookup would lack mind](lookup-table-lacks-mind.md) ("if a system runs by pure
stored lookup then it has no mind"); it denies that the antecedent is ever realisably satisfied at
mind scale. Its target is therefore [the separability of behaviour from
organisation](lookup-table-possible.md) on its *realisable* reading, not Block's bare conceptual possibility.

A concrete consequence: real large language models are exactly such organised computers — on the
order of $10^{12}$ parameters, not $10^{9600}$ rows — so they fall on the organised side of
[grain](../concepts/grain.md), not in the lookup-table class, which is what organisational-role
functionalism needs against the blockhead worry.

The view that resists the claim is Block's ([block](../sources/block.md)): he holds that the addressing and
retrieval organisation of a table is mere "plumbing," not mind-relevant organisation. The reply is
that at around $10^{9600}$ entries there is nothing to retrieve *from* — it cannot be stored — so
the system must compute, and the plumbing/computation distinction collapses at scale.

Distinct from [the unrealisability claim](lookup-table-physically-unrealisable.md): that says
the flat table cannot exist; this says what the realisable alternative positively *is* — an
organised computer — converting the physical fact into a point about kind.

### In plain terms

A flat answer-table big enough to fake a mind can't physically exist, and anything that stores
*less* than the full list has to *work out* its answers instead of fetching them — and working them
out is real processing, organized inner machinery. So any machine that could actually reproduce a
mind's behavior is an organized computer, never a dumb lookup. (Real AI models are exactly this: far
too small to be lookup tables, so they must be computing.) It doesn't deny that a *pure* lookup
table would lack a mind — it denies that such a thing could ever really be built at mind scale.
