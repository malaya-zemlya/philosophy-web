What introspection delivers is not the experience itself but a *representation of* it — a
higher-order model or report the brain constructs, of the same broad epistemic kind as any outward
report: selective, theory-laden, and capable of being wrong, even wholesale. This is the
*representation* answer to the
[acquaintance question](../questions/acquaintance-datum-or-representation.md). Its home programmes are
Dennett's heterophenomenology, which treats first-person deliverances as a subject's *theory* of
their inner life, authoritative about how things seem but not about how they are
([1991](../sources/dennett-1991.md)); Frankish's illusionism about the introspected feel
([2016](../sources/frankish-2016.md)); and Graziano's attention schema, on which the brain's simplified
self-model "paints in" the glow of awareness ([2013](../sources/graziano-2013.md)).

**Scope.** The claim concerns the *channel*: it says first-person access carries no special
evidential cargo exempting it from the discount rules that govern testimony and instruments. It
does not by itself deny that experiences exist or that phenomenality is real — that stronger,
further step is [phenomenality-is-illusion](phenomenality-is-illusion.md), and one can hold this claim without it (a
fallible window may still open onto something real). Distinct from
[inside-vantage-access-limited](inside-vantage-access-limited.md), which limits how *much* the inside view reveals; this
claim re-classifies what the inside view *is*. It is denied by
[introspective-acquaintance-is-datum](introspective-acquaintance-is-datum.md) and the acquaintance theorists behind it.

### In plain terms

When you "look inward" at your own experience, what you get may be less like seeing and more like
reading a summary your brain wrote about itself — quick, simplified, and sometimes just wrong.
Psychology backs this up: people confidently explain their own choices with reasons experiments
show weren't the real ones ([introspective-confabulation](../arguments/introspective-confabulation.md)). If the inner witness is
really an inner *press office*, then "I directly feel it, so it must be exactly as it seems" loses
its special authority — your reports about your mind deserve the same friendly skepticism as
anyone's reports about anything. Note what this doesn't say: it doesn't claim your feelings aren't
real — only that your access to them goes through a middleman.
