Whether a person at one time is the same person as a person at another is a basic fact about the
world — perfect, all-or-nothing identity of a persisting subject — that no amount of bodily or
psychological continuity *constitutes* or *amounts to*. Continuities are at best evidence of
identity, never what identity is. Thomas Reid put the classical case: personal identity "is
perfect identity: wherever it is real, it admits of no degrees," and cannot consist in memory,
since memory may be lost while the person remains ([1785](../sources/reid-1785.md)). The view is often
paired with a substantial self — a soul or Cartesian ego — but requires only that identity be
primitive; this is what Parfit calls the *further fact* his reductionism denies
([1984](../sources/parfit-1984.md)).

**Scope.** The claim is negative and structural: it says what identity is *not* (an analyzable
compound of continuities) and what follows — identity never holds partially, so
[personal-identity-admits-of-degree](personal-identity-admits-of-degree.md) is false, and every puzzle case (fission, spectra,
teleporters) has a definite yes-or-no answer, however unknowable. It does not by itself say what
the persisting subject is made of. It is denied by both reductionist criteria —
[identity-is-psychological-continuity](identity-is-psychological-continuity.md) and [identity-is-biological-continuity](identity-is-biological-continuity.md) —
and its standard liability is epistemic: if identity floats free of every observable continuity,
nothing in principle could tell us whether the person before us is the same one as yesterday.

### In plain terms

Maybe being "the same person" can't be broken down into anything simpler — not matching memories,
not the same living body — because it's a bedrock fact, like a soul's continuing, that either
wholly holds or wholly fails. On this view, all the philosopher's puzzle cases (teleporters,
split brains, gradual rewiring) have exact answers: either it's really you on the other side or it
isn't, even if no test could ever tell us which. The price of this tidiness: sameness of person
becomes invisible — since every observable sign could stay the same while the "further fact"
changed, or vice versa, you could never actually know that today's friend is yesterday's.
