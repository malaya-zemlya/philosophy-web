The barrier between first-person experience and third-person observation is *reciprocal*: it stands
equally between any two subjects, so it cannot single machines out as a special case. First-person
acquaintance — the direct, lived access a subject has to its own inner states
([first-person-access](../concepts/first-person-access.md)) — is non-transferable in *both* directions at once. A machine would
have access to its own substrate and none to mine, by exactly the measure I have access to mine and
none to its. The divide is between first and third person as such, holding between any two subjects
whatever, not between human and machine.

This is a structural claim about the *reciprocity* of access, not about what anything in fact feels.
It does not assert that the machine has experience; it asserts a conditional symmetry: to whatever
extent a subject has a first-person standpoint, every other subject is excluded from it equally. Its
dialectical job is narrow but pointed — to deny that any access asymmetry capable of singling
machines out exists.

The natural objection targets the claim's second arm. If a machine has no first-person standpoint at
all, then nothing on its side is reciprocally inaccessible, and the symmetry collapses: one side is
empty rather than merely hidden. This is distinct from [no-acquaintance-with-machine](no-acquaintance-with-machine.md), which
records only the human-side deficit; the present claim adds the reciprocal, fully general structure
that neutralises the *asymmetry*. The point that consciousness is mutually unprovable between minds —
that the wall runs symmetrically, neither party able to demonstrate an inner life to the other — is
due to Stanisław Lem ([lem](../sources/lem.md)).

### In plain terms

You can never directly feel anyone else's experience — and that cuts both ways. If a machine has any
inner life at all, it's just as shut out of yours as you are of its. The wall isn't "humans on one
side, machines on the other"; it's "me versus everyone else," and it stands equally between any two
minds. So that wall can't be used to single machines out as the doubtful case.
