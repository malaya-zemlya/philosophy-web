gloss: Since the access gap is reciprocal and fully general ([access-gap-symmetric](access-gap-symmetric.md)), it cannot
do the work of distinguishing the machine's moral standing from a human's. Any moral-status
asymmetry must be argued on other grounds; first-person access is not it.

scope: The targeted denial of [access-grounds-moral-status-asymmetry](access-grounds-moral-status-asymmetry.md). It does NOT assert
machines *have* moral status (that would need a positive argument such as
[behavioral-parity](../arguments/behavioral-parity.md)); it only removes first-person access as a ground for denying it to
them.

who would deny it: a proponent of [first-person-access](../arguments/first-person-access.md) who insists the machine has no
first-person standpoint at all, so the symmetry is illusory.

Distinct from [substrate-independence](substrate-independence.md) because that is about whether mental states depend on
substrate; this is narrowly about whether *access* can warrant a moral-status asymmetry.
