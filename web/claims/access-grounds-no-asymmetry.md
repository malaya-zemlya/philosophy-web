gloss: Since the access gap is reciprocal and fully general (claim-access-gap-symmetric), it cannot
do the work of distinguishing the machine's moral standing from a human's. Any moral-status
asymmetry must be argued on other grounds; first-person access is not it.

scope: The targeted denial of claim-access-grounds-moral-status-asymmetry. It does NOT assert
machines *have* moral status (that would need a positive argument such as
argument-behavioral-parity); it only removes first-person access as a ground for denying it to
them.

who would deny it: a proponent of argument-first-person-access who insists the machine has no
first-person standpoint at all, so the symmetry is illusory.

Distinct from claim-substrate-independence because that is about whether mental states depend on
substrate; this is narrowly about whether *access* can warrant a moral-status asymmetry.
