If each of a nation's people implements the role of a neuron (or functional node), passing signals
on the same conditions, the population jointly instantiates the very state-to-state transition
structure a brain instantiates — the functional organisation, not just the gross input–output
profile (Block, ["Troubles with Functionalism"](../sources/block-1978.md)).

This is a possibility claim about *multiple realisation of organisation* in an unusual medium. It
is deliberately stronger than [lookup-table-possible](lookup-table-possible.md): here the internal organisation is
present, not absent. By itself it takes no stand on whether the system is conscious — that is the
separate question raised by [chinese-nation-lacks-qualia](chinese-nation-lacks-qualia.md).

It would be denied by those who argue the organisation is not truly realised — for example because
of timing or speed mismatches, or because radio-linked people cannot sustain the dense, real-time
counterfactual dependencies the role requires (a realisation worry rather than a qualia worry).

Distinct from [lookup-table-possible](lookup-table-possible.md) because that system reproduces only behaviour by
storage and lacks organisation, whereas this one reproduces the organisation. Distinct from
[role-grain-is-organisational](role-grain-is-organisational.md), which says organisation is the individuating grain — a
thesis this claim sets up a counterexample-candidate against, rather than asserting.

### In plain terms

This says a giant crowd of people, each playing the part of one brain cell and passing signals by
the same rules, could together reproduce not just a brain's outward behavior but its full inner
pattern of cause-and-effect. It's a stronger setup than the cheat-sheet machine
([lookup-table mind](../concepts/lookup-table-mind.md)): here the inner organization is genuinely
present, not faked by a stored answer list. On its own it takes no stand on whether such a crowd
would actually be conscious — that's a separate question.
