Even granting that the [Chinese nation](../concepts/chinese-nation.md) realises the full functional
organisation, the considered intuition behind Block's absent-qualia pump ([block-1978](../sources/block-1978.md)) is
that there is *nothing it is like* to be the nation — no unified phenomenal subject over and above
the busy citizens.

This is a claim about *phenomenal* consciousness specifically, not about intentional or cognitive
states (the nation may well count as information-processing). It is the contested premise of the
absent-qualia objection — contested because it rests on an intuition the *systems reply* rejects.

It is denied by defenders of the systems reply (Lycan, Dennett), who hold that the nation as a
whole is a subject even though no member is, and by functionalists generally who deny that
absent-qualia intuitions carry evidential weight (compare the deflation of such intuitions in
[deflation by phenomenal concepts](../arguments/gap-deflated-by-phenomenal-concepts.md)).

Distinct from [lookup-table-lacks-mind](lookup-table-lacks-mind.md) because that denies mind to a system *lacking*
organisation, whereas this denies *qualia* to a system that *has* the organisation — the harder,
contested case. Distinct too from [the phenomenal-residue thesis](phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md), the
general claim that role under-determines the phenomenal; this is the specific intuited datum
offered as evidence for it.

### In plain terms

Picture an entire nation wired up to act out the exact inner workings of a single brain. The claim
here is just the stubborn intuition that, even so, there's *nobody home* — no unified someone
hovering above the busy crowd actually feeling anything. It's specifically about *felt experience*,
not about whether the nation processes information (it might do plenty). And it's the disputed link
in the whole objection: some insist the nation-as-a-whole really could be a subject even if no
single person in it is.
