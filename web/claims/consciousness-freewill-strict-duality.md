Consciousness and free will are related as a construction and its categorical dual: conscious
integration is product-like ([conscious-content-is-product-like](conscious-content-is-product-like.md)), free selection is
coproduct-like ([action-content-is-coproduct-like](action-content-is-coproduct-like.md)), and the mapping between the two
domains is arrow-reversal itself ([categorical-duality](../concepts/categorical-duality.md)) — not merely an analogy but a
duality in the mathematical sense, from which transfer of results should in principle follow.

This is the *strict* reading of the [consciousness-freewill-duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md), stated separately
from the hedged [mirror thesis](consciousness-freewill-mirror.md) precisely because the two
have different vulnerabilities. Distinct from [consciousness-freewill-mirror](consciousness-freewill-mirror.md) because the
mirror thesis asserts only a systematic correspondence of dialectics, which survives even if no
genuine universal property exists on either pole; the strict thesis asserts the duality itself, and
so is the target of [no-universal-property](../arguments/no-universal-property.md), [actual-possible-seam](../arguments/actual-possible-seam.md), and
[ambient-consciousness-collapse](../arguments/ambient-consciousness-collapse.md). Its defenders owe a completed universal property —
something that factors uniquely through conscious integration and co-uniquely through choice — and
none is currently on the table.
