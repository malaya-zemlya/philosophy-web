A language model can deliberately summon, hold, and compute with contents of its
[J-space](../concepts/j-space.md) independently of what it is outwardly saying. Instructed to hold a
concept in mind or to perform a mental calculation, the model activates the corresponding J-space
vectors even while its surface output concerns something else; information not normally represented
there can be pulled in when a task demands it ([gurnee-2026-workspace](../sources/gurnee-2026-workspace.md)). This mirrors the
top-down controllability of consciously accessible contents in humans — a thought can be summoned,
held, and dismissed at will.

It would be denied by a sceptic who reads the activations as passive echoes of the instruction text
rather than a controlled internal state; against this, the modulation also appears under implicit
task demands, without any overt instruction naming the concept.
