It *seems* to us that we have intrinsic, ineffable, private qualia, but we do not. What exists is
introspective machinery that *represents* our states as having phenomenal properties — so the
appearance of phenomenality is an introspective illusion. The real explanatory task then becomes the
"illusion problem": explaining why experience *seems* phenomenal, a task that *replaces* the
[hard problem](../concepts/hard-problem.md) rather than solving it. The thesis is the heart of illusionism
(Keith Frankish, [frankish-2016](../sources/frankish-2016.md); and Daniel Dennett's eliminativism about qualia,
[dennett-1988](../sources/dennett-1988.md)).

The claim denies the *explanandum* of the hard problem — phenomenal properties themselves — not the
existence of experience-talk, introspective reports, or access consciousness. It is a thoroughly
physicalist view: nothing non-physical is posited, and the misleading appearance is taken to be
fully mechanistically explicable. It is denied by realists about phenomenal consciousness —
dualists, panpsychists, Russellian monists, and anyone asserting
[a phenomenal residue](phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md) that functional role leaves out. Distinct from
[phenomenal-residue](phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md): this claim is its direct negation at the prior level — it denies there
is any phenomenal character there *at all* for functional role to leave out.

### In plain terms

The claim that the "inner feel" of experience isn't really there — it only seems to be. Your brain
runs a kind of self-monitoring that reports your states as having a special glow or feel (the
redness of red, the rawness of pain), but the report is misleading: there's no such glow, just the
very convincing impression of one. The puzzle of consciousness then changes shape — instead of "how
does matter produce feeling?", the question becomes "why does the brain so insistently tell itself it
has feelings it doesn't have?" This is the exact opposite of the view that experience is something
extra science can't capture ([that there's a felt residue left over](phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md)).
