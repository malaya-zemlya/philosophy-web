gloss: the basic stuff of the world is *neutral* — in itself neither mental nor physical — and the
mental and the physical are two different *arrangements* or groupings of these same neutral elements
(William James's "pure experience"; Mach; Russell's neutral monism). Mind and matter are derivative
patterns, not fundamental kinds.

scope: a monism about the fundamental base plus a deflation of the mental/physical divide to a
difference in arrangement. It does not, in its classic form, say the neutral elements are
proto-experiential — that extra step is what yields Russellian monism.

who would deny it: dualists (two fundamental kinds); physicalists (the base is physical); idealists
(the base is mental); and Russellian monists, who hold the intrinsic natures are (proto)phenomenal
rather than genuinely neutral.

Distinct from [russellian-monism](../positions/russellian-monism.md)'s base ([consciousness-categorical-not-dispositional](consciousness-categorical-not-dispositional.md)):
neutral monism keeps the base *neutral*; Russellian monism makes that intrinsic base the very thing
consciousness is. It is the historical parent of the Russellian view.
