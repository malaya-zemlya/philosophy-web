gloss: the cosmos itself is a single fundamental conscious subject, and individual minds are
*derivative* — aspects or dissociations *of* the universal consciousness rather than building blocks of
it (cosmopsychism: Goff, Shani, Nagasawa; a priority-monist cousin of panpsychism). Grounding runs
top-down, from the whole to its parts.

scope: combines priority monism (the cosmos is the one fundamental concrete object) with experiential
fundamentality. Its characteristic problem is the *de*combination problem: how does the universal
subject give rise to many distinct, bounded subjects like us?

who would deny it: (constitutive micro-)panpsychists (the fundamental subjects are micro, not cosmic);
physicalists and dualists; and those who reject priority monism.

Distinct from [consciousness-fundamental-ubiquitous](consciousness-fundamental-ubiquitous.md) (constitutive panpsychism builds macro-
minds *up* from micro-experiences; cosmopsychism derives them *down* from the whole — combination vs
decombination).
