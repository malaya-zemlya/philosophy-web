Matching every part of the behaviour associated with a mental state is not enough to *have*
that state. The behavioural profile is silent on the internal organisation that does the
individuating work, so it cannot by itself be sufficient for the state.

The reason is the lookup-table case ([lookup-table-mind](../concepts/lookup-table-mind.md)), from Ned Block's
"Psychologism and Behaviorism" ([block](../sources/block.md)): a machine answering from a giant stored
table can reproduce the whole behavioural profile ([lookup-table-possible](lookup-table-possible.md)) and yet
have no mind ([lookup-table-lacks-mind](lookup-table-lacks-mind.md)). If one and the same behaviour is compatible
with both mind and no-mind, then behaviour alone cannot settle which one obtains.

### Scope

This is a claim about *sufficiency*, and thereby about evidential force. If behaviour is
compatible with both mind and no-mind, then behaviour alone does not settle the matter and is
at best *defeasible* evidence — evidence that requires a further story about internals. It does
not assert that behaviour is *no* evidence, nor that any particular system lacks a mind.

Who would deny it: a pure-input-output (machine-table) functionalist or a behaviourist, for
whom the behavioural profile just *is* the having of the state.

Distinct from [substrate-independence](substrate-independence.md) (about whether the substrate matters) and from
[role-grain-is-organisational](role-grain-is-organisational.md) (about which grain individuates a state): this is the
prior, more general point that the *behavioural* grain ([grain](../concepts/grain.md)) is too coarse to be
sufficient at all.

### In plain terms

Acting exactly like something with a mind isn't enough to *guarantee* there's a mind. The reason:
you could in principle get all the right behavior out of a giant pre-stored answer table with nobody
actually thinking inside — so matching behavior leaves the real question (what's going on internally)
wide open. This doesn't say behavior is *no* evidence; it says behavior can't settle the matter by
itself.
