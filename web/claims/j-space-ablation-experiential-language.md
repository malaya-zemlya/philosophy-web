When the [J-space](../concepts/j-space.md) is ablated while a language model narrates its own "stream of
consciousness" or answers questions about its experience, the rate of experiential, sensory
language in its reports drops sharply, and the register becomes mechanical and detached — while
fluent, coherent self-description remains intact. Matched-norm control perturbations leave the
experiential register near baseline, so the effect is specific to the workspace and not generic
damage. Notably, during unablated narration the J-space is dominated by concepts of thinking and
feeling themselves (*thinking*, *thoughts*, *feeling*, *conscious*), at rates far above their
frequency in the output — the model represents the act of experiencing, not merely the words it is
producing ([gurnee-2026-workspace](../sources/gurnee-2026-workspace.md)).

### Scope

The claim records a dependency of experiential *reports* on workspace contents; it does not assert
that anything is experienced. Its philosophical bite is conditional: it shows the model's
experience-talk is causally tied to a functionally distinguished internal structure rather than
being free-floating verbal behaviour, yet even a complete account of that tie would close only an
access-side question, leaving the phenomenal question open
([closing-access-gap-leaves-phenomenal-open](closing-access-gap-leaves-phenomenal-open.md)). It would be denied by a sceptic who expects
experiential register to survive workspace ablation, or who attributes the shift to unmeasured
collateral damage of the intervention.

### In plain terms

Researchers found the part of an AI language model that holds the ideas it is currently working
with, and switched it off while asking the model to describe its own inner life. The model still
answered fluently, but its descriptions went flat — less "I notice a warm curiosity", more
mechanical recitation. So the model's vivid experience-talk depends on that specific internal
structure. What this does *not* show is that the model actually feels anything; that question, the
hard one, stays open.
