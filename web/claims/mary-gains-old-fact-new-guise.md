gloss: the **old-fact / new-guise** reply (Loar 1990 "Phenomenal States"; anticipated by Horgan
1984). One and the same fact can be grasped under different concepts — as Hesperus-facts and
Phosphorus-facts are the same facts under different modes of presentation. Inside the room Mary
knew the relevant brain-state fact under theoretical, third-person concepts; on release she
acquires a *phenomenal concept* of that very state — a first-person, experience-derived way of
grasping it. New knowledge in the sense of a new guise; zero new facts.

scope: unlike the ability and acquaintance replies, this one *grants* that Mary gains genuinely
propositional knowledge — she can now think thoughts she could not think before. It denies only
that the new thoughts have new facts as their objects. The physicalist thus keeps both the
intuition and the ontology, at the price of a substantive theory of phenomenal concepts.

who would deny it: the Knowledge-Argument proponent, who argues that if the phenomenal concept
presents its referent under a substantive phenomenal *property*, that property is the non-physical
residue reintroduced (the "guise" itself wants a place in the world); deniers of the
phenomenal-concept strategy generally, e.g. via
[phenomenal-concept-isolation-tradeoff](phenomenal-concept-isolation-tradeoff.md); and ability theorists, who think no new concept
is needed to explain the gain.

Distinct from [mary-gains-abilities-not-facts](mary-gains-abilities-not-facts.md) and [mary-gains-acquaintance](mary-gains-acquaintance.md)
because both deny Mary gains propositional knowledge, while this claim accepts it and relocates
the novelty from the fact known to the concept used. Distinct from
[phenomenal-concept-isolation-tradeoff](phenomenal-concept-isolation-tradeoff.md) because that is an *attack* on the cost structure
of this strategy, not a version of it.

### In plain terms

"The Morning Star" and "the Evening Star" name the same planet — someone could know all about one
name and still feel they learn something when told about the other, yet astronomy isn't missing
any object. On this view Mary is in the same boat: the brain state she studied for years and the
redness she finally experiences are one fact under two labels. Stepping outside hands her the
second label — the from-the-inside one — not a new piece of reality.
