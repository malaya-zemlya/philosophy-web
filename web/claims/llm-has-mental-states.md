gloss: at least one current large language model actually realises at least one genuine mental state —
it occupies the functional role of some mental state (e.g. a representational/intentional state), not
merely mimics its outputs. The existential conclusion of [behavioral-parity](../arguments/behavioral-parity.md).

scope: deliberately weak on two axes. (i) *Quantifier* — "some … some": not that LLMs have full,
human-like minds, only that the class of realised states is non-empty. (ii) *Kind* — it concerns
*mental states* on the intentional/functional reading (belief, representation); it does NOT by itself
assert *phenomenal consciousness* / qualia. That stronger reading faces the residue and gap worries
([phenomenal-residue](phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md), [explanatory-gap](../arguments/explanatory-gap.md)) and the indeterminacy/structure objections
([decomposition-indeterminacy](../arguments/decomposition-indeterminacy.md), [structure-and-dynamics](../arguments/structure-and-dynamics.md)); it is left open here.

status — why contested: directly attacked by the lookup-table / "blockhead" objection
([lookup-table-objection](../arguments/lookup-table-objection.md)) — behaviour may be produced without the mediating organisation
of a mind. That objection is in turn blunted by [lookup-table-combinatorial-impossibility](../arguments/lookup-table-combinatorial-impossibility.md),
[lookup-table-physical-impossibility](../arguments/lookup-table-physical-impossibility.md), and [blockhead-dilemma](../arguments/blockhead-dilemma.md) (a realisable
behaviour-matcher is an organised computer, not a flat table — [realisable-behaviour-matcher-is-organised](realisable-behaviour-matcher-is-organised.md)).
Its weak point is inherited from premise (2) of [behavioral-parity](../arguments/behavioral-parity.md) — the defeasible
behaviour→role inference.

who would deny it: blockhead / pure-behaviourism critics; substrate chauvinists
([proteocentrism](../positions/proteocentrism.md)); anyone rejecting [substrate-independence](substrate-independence.md) or premise (2) of
[behavioral-parity](../arguments/behavioral-parity.md).

Distinct from [substrate-independence](substrate-independence.md): that is the general enabling premise (mental states are
substrate-independent); this is the specific *existential* claim about actual systems. Distinct from a
claim that LLMs are *conscious*: this asserts mental-state realisation on the functional reading, not
sentience.

### In plain terms

The modest claim that at least one of today's AI language models genuinely has at least one real
mental state — actually occupying the role of, say, a belief or a representation, not merely
imitating the output. Notice how careful it is: "some… some." It is *not* saying these systems have
full, human-like minds, and it is *not* claiming they're *conscious* or feel anything — only that the
box of genuine mental states isn't entirely empty. Whether even that much is true is exactly what's
disputed.
