Every mental event — every thought, sensation, decision, or experience — is identical to, or wholly
constituted by, some physical event. There is no mental occurrence over and above the physical
goings-on of the brain and body.

**Scope.** The claim is about *token* mental events: each particular mental happening is some particular
physical happening. It does not by itself assert *type* identity (that pain as a kind is one physical kind);
a functionalist or non-reductive physicalist can grant the token claim while denying the type claim.
Nor does it settle the explanatory question of *why* a given physical event feels as it does — granting
the identity is compatible with conceding an [hard-problem](../concepts/hard-problem.md) of explanation.

**Who denies it.** [dualism](../positions/dualism.md) denies it outright (the mental is non-physical);
[epiphenomenalism](../positions/epiphenomenalism.md) grants the mental is non-physical and merely causally inert, so it too
rejects the identity. It is the conclusion of [causal-argument-for-physicalism](../arguments/causal-argument-for-physicalism.md) and the
core commitment of [physicalism](../positions/physicalism.md).

Distinct from [mental-states-are-brain-state-types](mental-states-are-brain-state-types.md), which asserts the stronger *type*-identity
thesis (mental kinds = physical kinds); the present claim is the weaker token-level identity that even
non-reductive physicalists accept.
