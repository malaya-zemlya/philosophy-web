Categorical duality relates a category to its opposite: a theorem about limits in $C$ becomes a
theorem about colimits in $C^{op}$. But the free-will problems do not live in an opposite category
— they live in the same time-forward causal world as the consciousness problems. Arrow-reversal
between the two domains therefore supplies a *dictionary* — a systematic generator of candidate
correspondences — and no more: each entry must be verified by hand, and a verified entry's warrant
comes from its own content, not from the duality that suggested it.

The claim sets the framework's epistemic ceiling. It does not disparage the dictionary — a
generator whose outputs repeatedly land on independently existing positions is tracking something
([consciousness-freewill-mirror](consciousness-freewill-mirror.md)) — it denies only that the categorical apparatus, as it
stands, licenses *automatic* transfer. What would upgrade dictionary to theorem is a completed
universal property on each pole and a duality involution on the ambient category; the argument that
neither is available is [no-universal-property](../arguments/no-universal-property.md). Who would deny it: a defender of
[consciousness-freewill-strict-duality](consciousness-freewill-strict-duality.md) who holds that the mathematics already carries
transfer-force.
