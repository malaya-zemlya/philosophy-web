gloss: Unlike a behavioural sign, living biological organisation is expensive and not made to
order; at present it cannot be mass-counterfeited the way begging or weeping can. So biology is, for
now, an unforgeable mark.

scope: A contingent, time-indexed empirical claim ("currently", "to that standard"). It does NOT
assert biology is necessary or sufficient for consciousness, nor that it could never be
counterfeited — only that it presently resists the cheap, to-order forgery that defeats behavioural
signs. This contingency is exactly why the argument it serves is prudential, not metaphysical.

who would deny it: those who think biological signatures are or will soon be cheaply simulable, or
who deny that "unforgeability" is the right property to track (the conclusion's own concession,
"unforgeability is not flesh," presses this).

Distinct from position-proteocentrism because that asserts carbon is *metaphysically necessary* for
consciousness; this asserts only that biology is *currently hard to fake*.
