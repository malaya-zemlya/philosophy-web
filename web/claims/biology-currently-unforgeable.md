Living biological organisation is, for the moment, something that cannot be cheaply counterfeited.
Unlike a behavioural sign — begging, weeping, a convincing show of distress — a functioning
organism is expensive and not made to order, so at present it cannot be mass-produced to fake
suffering. Biology is therefore, for now, an *unforgeable* mark.

This is a *contingent, time-indexed* empirical claim, flagged by its "currently" and its "to that
standard." It does not assert that biology is necessary or sufficient for consciousness, nor that
it could never be counterfeited — only that it presently resists the cheap, to-order forgery that
defeats behavioural signs, the criterion-gaming dynamic of [criterion-gaming](../concepts/criterion-gaming.md). That
contingency is exactly why the argument it serves is prudential rather than metaphysical.

It would be denied by anyone who thinks biological signatures are, or will soon be, cheaply
simulable, and by anyone who denies that "unforgeability" is the right property to track in the
first place — a doubt the conclusion drawn from this very claim, that unforgeability is not flesh,
presses directly.

Distinct from [proteocentrism](../positions/proteocentrism.md), which asserts that carbon is *metaphysically necessary*
for consciousness; this asserts only that biology is *currently hard to fake*.

### In plain terms

Right now you can fake the *behavior* of suffering cheaply — anyone can put on a convincing show —
but you can't cheaply fake actual living biology; it's costly and can't be mass-produced to order.
So, for the time being, being biological is a hard-to-counterfeit mark. Note the "for now": this is
a claim about today's technology, not a claim that biology is magic or required for a mind.
