A mental state can be the cause of a piece of behaviour in the difference-making sense — had the
mental state been otherwise, the behaviour would have been otherwise, and interventions on the
mental state are systematically followed by changes in the behaviour — even granting the
[causal closure](../concepts/causal-closure.md) of the physical. On the *interventionist* account of
causation (Woodward, [2003](../sources/woodward-2003.md)), that difference-making relation is all that
causation is; and where the behaviour is *realization-insensitive* — it would have followed the
mental state whichever of its many physical realizers had occurred — the mental property, not the
particular neural realizer, is the cause at the right grain (List & Menzies,
[2009](../sources/list-menzies-2009.md)). So the mental can do genuine causal work without being
identical to any physical kind.

**Scope.** The claim asserts causal efficacy for higher-level mental causes; it does not assert
that mental properties are irreducible (though it is the natural companion of non-reductive
physicalism), nor that mental causes ever compete with physical ones. It is denied by proponents of
the [exclusion argument](../arguments/causal-exclusion.md), for whom sufficiency at the physical level
pre-empts any distinct higher-level cause, and it answers the
[mental-causation question](../questions/mental-causation.md) without the identity move of
[mental-is-physical](mental-is-physical.md). Distinct from [counterfactual-structure-objective](counterfactual-structure-objective.md), which
asserts that difference-making structure is objective; this claim applies that framework to vindicate
specifically *mental* causes.

### In plain terms

What makes something a cause? A natural answer: wiggle it and the effect wiggles. Turn the thermostat
and the room temperature changes — so the thermostat setting is a cause, even though the "real work"
is molecules bumping around. This claim says decisions and desires are causes in exactly that sense:
change the decision and the arm doesn't rise, whatever the neurons underneath happen to be doing.
Being the cause is about making the difference at the right level of description, not about being
the microscopic machinery — so the mind doesn't have to *be* brain chemistry to genuinely run things.
