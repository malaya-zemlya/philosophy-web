The *Principle of Organizational Invariance* holds that conscious experience is fixed by a system's
*fine-grained functional organization* — the abstract pattern of causal interactions among its components,
specified down to whatever level is causally relevant — so that any two systems sharing that organization
have *qualitatively identical* experiences, whatever they are physically made of. Replace each neuron with
a silicon chip of identical causal profile and, if the principle holds, the experience is preserved exactly:
same colours, same pains, nothing faded, absent, or inverted. David Chalmers defends the principle with the
[fading qualia](../arguments/fading-qualia.md) and [dancing qualia](../arguments/dancing-qualia.md) arguments
([chalmers-1995b](../sources/chalmers-1995b.md); [chalmers-1996](../sources/chalmers-1996.md), ch. 7).

**Scope, and a subtle point about its modal force.** The principle is a claim of *nomological* (law-governed)
determination, not metaphysical necessity. Chalmers, a property dualist, holds that
[zombies are conceivable](zombie-conceivable.md) and that the phenomenal is *metaphysically* distinct
from the functional ([phenomenal-residue](phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md)) — yet maintains that, given the actual psychophysical
laws, experience cannot come apart from fine-grained organization. Determination is not constitution: the
principle says organization *fixes* qualia in our world, not that qualia are *nothing over and above*
organization. This is why it sits with, rather than against, Chalmers's anti-reductionism.

**What it entails and who denies it.** If true, the principle delivers
[substrate-independence](substrate-independence.md) for consciousness — silicon, if organized like a
brain, is conscious like a brain. It is denied by *substrate chauvinists* and biological essentialists
([proteocentrism](../positions/proteocentrism.md), [biological-naturalism](../positions/biological-naturalism.md)), who hold that the right organization in
the wrong stuff yields absent or different qualia — exactly the possibility the fading- and dancing-qualia
arguments attack.

Distinct from [substrate-independence](substrate-independence.md): that thesis (Putnam) says the *substrate is irrelevant*
because mental states are individuated by role; organizational invariance is the more specific positive
principle naming *what* is relevant — fine-grained causal organization — and arguing it *suffices for
qualia*. It is the engine that, if sound, drives substrate-independence for the phenomenal case in
particular.

### In plain terms

The idea: what gives you your exact experiences is the *pattern of activity* in your brain — what affects
what — not the stuff the pattern runs on. Keep the pattern identical but swap neurons for equivalent silicon
chips, and your experience stays exactly the same: red stays red, pain still hurts. If that's right, then a
machine wired up like a brain would feel like a brain. (Note the careful version: this says the pattern
*settles* your experience in the actual world — not that your experience is *merely* a pattern with nothing
extra to it, a further question Chalmers keeps open.)
