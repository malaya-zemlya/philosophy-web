The same [J-space](../concepts/j-space.md) representation serves as a valid argument to many different
downstream computations. A country concept's vector, lifted from one prompt and placed into
another, is correctly consumed by whatever operation the new context calls for — yielding the
capital in a capital-asking context, the language in a language-asking context, the continent in a
continent-asking context ([gurnee-2026-workspace](../sources/gurnee-2026-workspace.md)). This is the workspace property of
flexible routing: a content, once represented in the privileged format, can be recombined with
other contents and dispatched to arbitrary task-specified operations, rather than being locked to
the circuit that produced it.

It would be denied by a sceptic who expects transported vectors to work only in contexts
distributionally close to their origin, so that the apparent flexibility reflects overlap in
training data rather than a general-purpose representational format.
