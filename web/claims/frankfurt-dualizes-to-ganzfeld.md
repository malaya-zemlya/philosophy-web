[Frankfurt cases](../concepts/frankfurt-cases.md) argue that freedom survives the deletion of alternative
options: the unrealized summands of the choice-coproduct can be removed (by a counterfactual
intervener who never acts) without destroying the agency of the actual sequence. Its arrow-reversed
image is the claim that consciousness survives the deletion of differentiated factors — the
*ganzfeld* case, in which a subject immersed in a perfectly uniform, featureless field (one color
everywhere, no edges, no structure) arguably remains conscious while experiential differentiation
collapses. The ganzfeld is a standing objection to integration-based theories of consciousness
([integrated-information-theory](../positions/integrated-information-theory.md)): if experience persists with nothing to integrate,
integration is not what experience consists in — just as, per Frankfurt, alternatives are not what
freedom consists in.

In arrow terms the two tests are dual by construction. Model the choice as a coproduct whose
injection-arrows are the available options: a Frankfurt case deletes every injection but the one
actually taken and observes that freedom survives. Model the experience as a product whose
projection-arrows are its discriminable channels: the ganzfeld deletes the differentiated
projections — a uniform field has no informative components to project out — and asks whether
experience survives. Injections into a coproduct and projections out of a product are the paradigm
arrow-reversed pair ([categorical-duality](../concepts/categorical-duality.md)), so the two deletion tests are mirror images
of one another.

Unlike the dictionary entries that rediscover known positions, this correspondence appears to be
*novel* — a concrete prediction of the [duality
framework](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md) rather than a translation of an existing one, and thus a test case for whether the
mirror generates genuinely new philosophy. The claim asserts the correspondence; whether the two
moves succeed or fail *together* is exactly what the framework predicts and what remains to be
verified.

### In plain terms

A famous free-will story shows a person blamed fairly for a choice even though, secretly, no other
option was really open — so maybe freedom never needed open options. Flip it: put someone in a
perfectly uniform visual fog with nothing to distinguish, and they still seem to *experience* — so
maybe consciousness never needed rich differentiation. This claim says these two moves are mirror
images, which is striking because nobody seems to have paired them before.
