When a language model is asked what it is thinking about, the concepts it names are the ones its
[J-space](../concepts/j-space.md) currently represents. The link is causal, not merely correlational:
swapping one active J-space vector for another changes the model's verbal answer to match the
swapped-in concept ([gurnee-2026-workspace](../sources/gurnee-2026-workspace.md)). Verbal report is the first of the five
workspace properties, and the one the Jacobian lens was built to detect; the philosophical work is
done by its co-occurrence with the other four.

It would be denied by a sceptic who holds that the model's reports track trained verbal
dispositions rather than the identified representations, or that the swap experiments show only
that the lens vectors influence output, not that they are what the report is *about*.

Distinct from [llm-functional-introspection](llm-functional-introspection.md) because that claim asserts a limited,
unreliable capacity to detect artificial perturbations of internal state under concept-injection
probing; this claim asserts the systematic reportability of an independently identified
representational subspace during ordinary processing.
