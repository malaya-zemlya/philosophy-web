When a subject attends to a current experience — a present ache, the look of the colours in front
of them — what introspection delivers is the experience *itself*, met directly, not a constructed
report about it that could be wrong wholesale. This is the *datum* answer to the
[acquaintance question](../questions/acquaintance-datum-or-representation.md): knowledge by acquaintance
in Russell's sense ([1911](../sources/russell-1911.md)) is a relation to the thing, unmediated by
description, and so prior to the evidential discount rules that govern reports. Kindred
formulations are Searle's first-person ontology ([1992](../sources/searle-1992.md)) and Chalmers's direct
phenomenal concepts ([2003](../sources/chalmers-2003b.md)).

**Scope.** The claim concerns the epistemic *status* of introspective contact with current
experience — that it is observation-like, a given, rather than one more report channel. It does not
assert infallibility about the experience's causes, categories, or comparisons (one can misjudge
whether today's ache is worse than yesterday's), and it takes no stand on other minds or machines.
Distinct from [privileged-first-person-access](privileged-first-person-access.md), which asserts only that direct,
non-inferential self-access *exists* while deliberately taking no stand on its nature; this claim
takes the stand — the access is a genuine datum, not a representation. It is denied by
[introspection-is-fallible-representation](introspection-is-fallible-representation.md) and the illusionist and heterophenomenological
programmes behind it.

### In plain terms

You're aware of your own pain right now. Is that awareness like *touching* the pain itself, or like
reading a memo your brain wrote about the pain? This claim says: touching. The experience isn't
something you learn about through an inner news channel that might be spun or garbled — it's simply
*had*, and having it is the most direct contact with anything you'll ever get. You can still be
wrong about the details around it (what caused it, whether it's worse than yesterday's), but not
about the sheer felt presence of it. That is why, on this view, first-person experience counts as
rock-bottom evidence that no outside test can overrule.
