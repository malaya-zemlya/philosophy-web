The [binding problem](../concepts/binding-problem.md) is, in categorical terms, the thesis that
experience is *not* actually a product: the unified experience is a cone over the quale channels
whose mediating map into the product fails to be an isomorphism — the whole carries structure the
tuple of parts does not. Integrated information theory quantifies exactly this: Φ measures the
failure of a system's cause-effect structure to factorize
([consciousness-is-integrated-information](consciousness-is-integrated-information.md)), so consciousness on that view *is* the
obstruction to product structure. Binding read as coherence-enforcement — streams constrained to
agree on shared structure — is limit-flavored, a pullback rather than a mere aggregation, which
keeps the phenomenon on the product side of the [duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md)
even as it qualifies the crude assignment.

Distinct from [consciousness-is-integrated-information](consciousness-is-integrated-information.md) because that claim identifies
consciousness with a quantity of integration; this claim is a structural reading of what such
integration *is* — the deviation of experience from a genuine product of its channels — and stands
whether or not IIT's identification succeeds. It qualifies
[conscious-content-is-product-like](conscious-content-is-product-like.md) from within: experience is product-*shaped* (a
conjunctive tuple of channels) while systematically exceeding the product (the channels are not
independent). Its output-side dual, on the framework's reading, is the dispute over whether the
action space is a genuine coproduct ([alternatives-genuinely-open](alternatives-genuinely-open.md)) — and the deletion
tests for the two structures pair as [frankfurt-dualizes-to-ganzfeld](frankfurt-dualizes-to-ganzfeld.md).

### In plain terms

A spreadsheet row is just its cells: know each cell and you know the row. Experience looks at first
like such a row — sight, sound, touch, side by side — but it isn't: the parts of one experienced
moment belong to each other in a way separate readings can't capture, and you can't reconstruct the
moment from independent channel reports. One prominent theory of consciousness even *measures*
consciousness as the amount by which a system fails to split into independent parts. This claim
says that failure-to-split is the right mathematical description of the mind's famous "binding" of
features into one experience.
