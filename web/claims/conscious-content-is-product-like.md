A moment of experience realizes all of its components at once: the visual, auditory, and
proprioceptive channels are simultaneously present in a single field, the way an element of a
mathematical *product* is a tuple realizing every one of its coordinates
([categorical-duality](../concepts/categorical-duality.md)). Experience combines its [qualia](../concepts/qualia.md) channels
conjunctively — this sight *and* this sound *and* this bodily feel — never by settling on one to
the exclusion of the rest.

The claim concerns the combinatorial *shape* of conscious content, not its substrate or its
explanation. It is one of the two structural premises installing consciousness and free choice on
dual poles ([consciousness-freewill-duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md)), opposite
[action-content-is-coproduct-like](action-content-is-coproduct-like.md). It is denied, in its crude form, by the observation
that attention makes experience partially exclusive ([graded-polarity](../arguments/graded-polarity.md)), and it is
qualified from within: unified experience arguably *fails* to factorize into independent channels,
making consciousness the obstruction to full product structure rather than its instance
([binding-is-obstruction-to-product](binding-is-obstruction-to-product.md)).
