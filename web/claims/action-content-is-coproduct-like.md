A choice's options combine as alternatives, not as co-present parts: to act is to realize exactly
one of them, the way an element of a mathematical *coproduct* inhabits exactly one summand
([categorical-duality](../concepts/categorical-duality.md)). Incompatible motor programs cannot co-execute — one cannot stand
and sit — so action is *committing* where experience is *conjoining*. Where perception says "all of
these at once," action says "this one, not the others."

The claim concerns the combinatorial shape of a choice's content and is neutral on whether the
alternatives are metaphysically open ([alternatives-genuinely-open](alternatives-genuinely-open.md)) or merely
represented candidate plans — that further question is precisely where the
[actual/possible seam](../arguments/actual-possible-seam.md) opens. It is the second structural premise
of the [consciousness–free-will duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md), opposite
[conscious-content-is-product-like](conscious-content-is-product-like.md), and is denied in its crude form by the observation
that behavior is partially conjunctive — one walks while talking — so the motor system takes
products of compatible actions and coproducts only of incompatible ones
([graded-polarity](../arguments/graded-polarity.md)).
