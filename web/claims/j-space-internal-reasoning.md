When a language model chains inferential steps — answering a multi-hop question, composing a plan —
the intermediate results appear in its [J-space](../concepts/j-space.md) without ever being written into
the output. In a two-hop query whose unspoken middle term is, say, *spider*, that concept surfaces
in the J-space readout between question and answer; and the role is load-bearing, since swapping
the intermediate's vector for another concept's redirects the final answer accordingly
([gurnee-2026-workspace](../sources/gurnee-2026-workspace.md)). The J-space thus functions as the medium of the model's
deliberate reasoning, as consciously accessible contents are held to be the medium of effortful,
step-by-step thought in humans.

It would be denied by a sceptic who holds that the decoded intermediates are epiphenomenal
correlates of computation done elsewhere; the swap results are the direct answer to that reading.
