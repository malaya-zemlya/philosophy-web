Arrow-reversing the [philosophical zombie](../concepts/philosophical-zombie.md) — a physical duplicate
with nothing it is like to be it — yields the *volitional zombie*: a system behaviorally identical
to an agent, in which no real choosing occurs. That figure is the intuition pump behind
incompatibilism, and the correspondence extends to the replies: the compatibilist's "the functional
duplicate lacks nothing" is structurally the deflationist reply to zombies. Each debate then splits
the same way — a further-fact camp positing an ingredient no functional story captures (raw feel on
one side; libertarian origination on the other) and a deflationary camp on which the apparent gap
is an artifact of self-representation. Chalmers-realist is to illusionist as libertarian is to
compatibilist.

The reversal here touches none of the arrows. Reading the mind as a diagram of dependence-arrows
among states ([what the arrows are](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md)), the zombie keeps
the entire diagram — stimulus to discrimination to report — and deletes only the alleged
non-functional ingredient at the *receiving* apex: what the arriving is like. Its mirror image
keeps the same diagram, arrows reversed, and deletes the alleged extra at the *issuing* apex: the
act of origination over and above the transition from deliberation-states to action. The
deflationist reply, on both sides, is that once the arrows are fixed there was nothing at the apex
left to delete.

Subsidiary pairings follow the same fold: [illusionism](../positions/illusionism.md) about phenomenality
pairs with Wegner's illusion of conscious will (Wegner, [2002](../sources/wegner-2002.md);
[phenomenality-is-illusion](phenomenality-is-illusion.md)); input-side postdictive phenomena — color phi, backward
masking — pair with Libet-style findings on the output side (Libet, [1985](../sources/libet-1985.md));
and Block's phenomenal/access distinction (Block, [1995](../sources/block-1995.md)) pairs with Fischer
and Ravizza's regulative/guidance control (Fischer & Ravizza, [1998](../sources/fischer-ravizza-1998.md)),
with the two deflationary collapses mirroring each other. The claim asserts these structural
correspondences, not any verdict inside them; the parity *commitment* they suggest is stated
separately as [deflationism-entails-compatibilism](deflationism-entails-compatibilism.md).

### In plain terms

Philosophers imagine a "zombie": a creature that acts exactly like you but has no inner experience.
Flip the picture from perception to action and you get a machine that *behaves* exactly like a
person deciding, but in which no real deciding happens — which is just how skeptics about free will
have always painted us. This claim says the two nightmares, and the standard ways of answering
them, are mirror images of each other.
