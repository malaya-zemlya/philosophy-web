The [combination problem](../arguments/combination-problem.md) asks how micro-subjects could compose a
macro-subject; its arrow-reversed image asks how micro-*tendencies* compose a unified macro-agent.
The dual of [panpsychism](../concepts/panpsychism.md) is a pan-agentialism — proto-agency everywhere,
composing upward — and both programs stall at the same juncture: aggregation of the primitive does
not obviously yield the unified higher thing. A special case makes the parallel exact: micro
*indeterminacies* do not sum to macro-agency — the standard objection to quantum libertarianism,
that amplified chance is randomness, not authorship — just as micro-experiences do not sum to a
subject ([micro-subjects-do-not-sum](micro-subjects-do-not-sum.md)).

The arrows here are arrows of *constitution* rather than causation — many micro-items composing
one macro-item — and at that scale both problems are fan-ins. What the mirror reverses is not the
composition arrows themselves but the pole of mind the composite occupies: a composed experiencer
at the terminus of perception, a composed agent at the source of action, each composition problem
inheriting its shape from the duality of the things composed.

The claim asserts the correspondence of the two composition problems, not a verdict on either
program. It is a dictionary entry of the
[consciousness–free-will duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md), counted as evidence by
[consciousness-freewill-duality](../arguments/consciousness-freewill-duality.md) because the free-will side's composition problem
already exists in that literature under its own name (the luck objection to event-causal
libertarianism is its sharpest edge).

### In plain terms

One theory says consciousness is everywhere in tiny amounts, and big minds are built from little
ones — but no one can say how a pile of tiny experiences adds up to *you*. Flip it to the action
side: suppose tiny pushes of randomness or proto-choice are everywhere — no one can say how those
add up to a person genuinely *deciding*. In particular, sprinkling quantum chance into the brain
gives you dice, not a chooser, exactly as a heap of micro-feelings gives you a crowd, not a self
(that older point is [micro-subjects-do-not-sum](micro-subjects-do-not-sum.md)).
