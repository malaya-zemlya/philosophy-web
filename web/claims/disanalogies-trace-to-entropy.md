Automatic transfer between the two domains would require a duality involution — roughly, a dagger
structure making the ambient category equivalent to its opposite. Fundamental physics is
CPT-symmetric, so such a structure arguably exists microphysically; what breaks it is
thermodynamic. If that is the *only* thing that breaks it, then every genuine disanalogy between
the consciousness problems and the free-will problems should trace back to the arrow of time — to
entropy — rather than to anything metaphysically deeper. The asymmetry is visible in the
mathematics of any Set-like, copy-and-discard causal setting: distributivity holds, $A \times (B +
C) \cong (A \times B) + (A \times C)$, but its dual fails — information fans out for free,
decisions cannot be un-made ([categorical-duality](../concepts/categorical-duality.md)).

The claim is the [duality framework's](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md) boldest and most
falsifiable-flavored commitment: find one disanalogy between the two problem-families that does
*not* reduce to temporal asymmetry, and it fails. It is also its least defended — the inference
from CPT symmetry of the fundamental dynamics to a dagger structure on the relevant category of
*representational states* is a leap across levels that the framework does not close. Who would deny
it: anyone who locates the actual/possible seam ([actual-possible-seam](../arguments/actual-possible-seam.md)) or the ambient
status of consciousness ([ambient-consciousness-collapse](../arguments/ambient-consciousness-collapse.md)) at a depth below
thermodynamics.

### In plain terms

The two problem-families mirror each other imperfectly, and this claim makes a bet about the
imperfections: every place the mirror warps, the culprit is time's one-way flow — the same fact
that lets you copy a file but not un-send an email — and never anything more mysterious. Physics at
the bottom runs the same forwards and backwards; only entropy picks a direction. If someone finds a
mismatch between the consciousness puzzles and the free-will puzzles that has nothing to do with
time's direction, the bet is lost.
