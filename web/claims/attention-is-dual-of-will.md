When the [consciousness–free-will duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md) is resolved to
the level of maps, the dual of the will is not consciousness as a whole but *attention*. Selecting
one stream among co-present ones is projection out of a product; committing to one action among
alternatives is injection into a coproduct; and these two operations are exact categorical duals
([categorical-duality](../concepts/categorical-duality.md)). Attention is selection on the input side, volition is selection
on the output side.

The correspondence has independent support in the philosophy of attention: on Wayne Wu's account,
attention just *is* selection for action (Wu, [2011](../sources/wu-2011.md)) — the two selectors are
already theorized as one mechanism seen from its two ends. The claim refines the coarse duality
(consciousness dual to will) into a finer one, and it supplies the mirror's answer to the
observation that attention makes experience partially exclusive: exclusivity enters experience
exactly where the input-side *selector* operates, as the duality predicts
([graded-polarity](../arguments/graded-polarity.md)).
