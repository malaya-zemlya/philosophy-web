An *anti-zombie* is a being microphysically and functionally identical to a conscious person
whose very physical constitution *necessitates* its phenomenal consciousness — a creature for
which physicalism holds. The claim is that such a being is *ideally conceivable*: the
description "a full physical duplicate of me, and conscious in virtue of that physical nature"
survives idealised rational reflection with no contradiction revealed ([frankish-2007](../sources/frankish-2007.md)).

The claim is about conceivability, not possibility; like its mirror image
[zombie-conceivable](zombie-conceivable.md) it becomes modally potent only through a
conceivability-to-possibility bridge. Its dialectical point is *parity*: the anti-zombie seems
exactly as conceivable as the zombie, so whatever bridge carries the zombie to metaphysical
possibility carries the anti-zombie there too — and an anti-zombie's possibility entails that
physicalism is possibly true, hence (being a thesis about necessitation) actually true. Someone
who accepts zombie conceivability must therefore either find an asymmetry that discredits the
anti-zombie conception or accept that the symmetric intuitions cancel. Denying this claim
typically means holding that reflection on a full physical duplicate leaves the presence of
consciousness *open* in a way it does not leave its absence open — the asymmetry the anti-zombie
theorist says has not been earned.
