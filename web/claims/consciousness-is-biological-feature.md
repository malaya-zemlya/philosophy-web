gloss: consciousness is a real, higher-level biological feature *caused by* lower-level neurobiological
processes and *realised in* the brain — as digestion is caused by and realised in the stomach. It is
ontologically irreducible (first-personal) yet causally explained by, and nothing "over and above",
the brain's specific causal powers (Searle's "biological naturalism", 1992).

scope: physicalist but anti-reductionist and anti-computationalist. Because it is the brain's concrete
*causal powers* that produce consciousness — not the abstract program it runs — merely implementing
the right syntax is insufficient ([syntax-insufficient-for-semantics](syntax-insufficient-for-semantics.md), the moral of
[chinese-room](../concepts/chinese-room.md)).

who would deny it: strong-AI functionalists ([functionalism](../concepts/functionalism.md), [substrate-independence](substrate-independence.md)),
who hold the right causal organisation suffices regardless of substrate.

Distinct from [mental-states-are-brain-state-types](mental-states-are-brain-state-types.md) (a reductive type-identity; biological
naturalism is non-reductive) and from proteocentrism: the demand is the right *causal powers*, which
need not be carbon-specific — though Searle holds only suitably brain-like causal powers are known to
do it.
