A *play-it-safe policy*, not a verdict about which things have minds. Because behavioural signs of
suffering are cheap to fake while living biology, for now, is not, the prudent course is to let
flesh carry the weight of moral standing and place the burden of proof on the non-biological mind.
This is caution under uncertainty, not a metaphysical claim. It is offered as a precautionary,
withhold-by-default candidate answer to [llm-moral-status](../questions/llm-moral-status.md).

The claim is about *justified caution and policy*, not about what does or does not possess a mind.
The underlying worry throughout is *criterion-gaming* ([criterion-gaming](../concepts/criterion-gaming.md)): a standard
that is cheap to fake invites the very forgery that would empty it of meaning. Two recorded limits
keep the claim contested:

- *Unforgeability is not flesh.* The premises favour whatever sign is hardest to forge, and
  biology wins only contingently ([biology-currently-unforgeable](biology-currently-unforgeable.md)). A non-biological
  unforgeable sign would displace flesh, so the claim does not establish
  [proteocentrism](../positions/proteocentrism.md) — it lends that position only defeasible, prudential support.
- *The false-positive worry.* The claim inherits a contested bridge premise
  ([forgeable-sign-unfit-as-criterion](forgeable-sign-unfit-as-criterion.md)): forgeability may bound false positives without
  nullifying a sign's evidential value.

It would be denied by proponents of the [behavioral-parity](../arguments/behavioral-parity.md), who hold that behaviour is
good defeasible evidence of a mind, and by anyone who regards the burden-of-proof asymmetry against
machines as itself unmotivated (compare [access-symmetry-rebuttal](../arguments/access-symmetry-rebuttal.md) on unearned
asymmetries).

Distinct from [proteocentrism](../positions/proteocentrism.md) (a metaphysical thesis) and from
[access-grounds-moral-status-asymmetry](access-grounds-moral-status-asymmetry.md), which rests the asymmetry on first-person access
rather than on forgeability.

### In plain terms

A play-it-safe policy, not a claim about what machines really are. Since acting distressed is cheap
to fake but living biology (for now) isn't, the cautious move is to rest moral standing on the
hard-to-fake biological mark and make the non-biological case prove itself. Two built-in caveats
keep it modest: "hard to fake" isn't the same as "must be flesh" — a future unforgeable
*non*-biological sign would do just as well — and a forgeable sign (like a fingerprint) can still
be useful evidence rather than worthless.
