A system can be completely explicable from the outside and still be unable, from the inside, to reach a
satisfying explanation of why its own introspective states appear as they do. On this thesis the felt
explanatory gap is produced by the *architecture of self-access*, not by any non-physical residue in
the world.

### Gloss

A system may admit a complete mechanistic account from an external vantage while being unable, within
its own accessible cognitive economy, to reach an intuitively satisfying explanation of why its
introspective states appear as they do. The gap is then produced by missing internal observables
([inside-vantage-access-limited](inside-vantage-access-limited.md)) plus a non-traversable bridge between vocabularies
([explanation-must-be-internally-traversable](explanation-must-be-internally-traversable.md)) — rather than by a residue in the world. The
standing illustration: a brain is fully explicable from outside, yet from inside we have no access to
our own neuron-firings, so the mechanism-to-appearance link is not derivable from where the subject
sits.

### Scope

A claim about the *origin and location* of a self-explanatory gap — in the knower, not the world. It is
an *epistemic* or *architectural* thesis: it does not assert phenomenal consciousness, does not assert
that there is no ontological residue, and does not establish illusionism. It says only that a gap of
the felt kind *can* arise from access architecture alone, leaving open whether, in any given case
(human or machine), that is the whole story. As such it is one candidate answer to the hard problem of
consciousness ([hard-problem](../concepts/hard-problem.md), the issue posed in [hard-problem](../questions/hard-problem.md)), classed among
the varieties of introspective gap ([introspective-gap-varieties](../concepts/introspective-gap-varieties.md)).

### Relation to mysterianism

This is a *specified, mechanism-level* cousin of McGinn's cognitive closure ([mysterianism](../positions/mysterianism.md),
[mcginn-1989](../sources/mcginn-1989.md)). Distinct from [humans-closed-to-psychophysical-link](humans-closed-to-psychophysical-link.md) because that
asserts a brute, human-specific closure to the *psychophysical property*; this supplies a *general
mechanism* (architectural self-access plus internal non-traversability) and applies to *any*
self-modelling system — humans and consciousness-naive language models alike
([llm-own-explanatory-gap](../questions/llm-own-explanatory-gap.md)). Distinct from [phenomenal-residue](phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md) because that locates
the shortfall in the world; this locates it in the knower's access economy.

### Who would deny it

A residue or dualist theorist who holds that the gap could not be *merely* architectural — that even a
system with full self-access would still face the ontological question. Block's access/phenomenal
distinction ([access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness](../concepts/access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness.md), [block-2007](../sources/block-2007.md)) cuts here:
explaining away an *access* gap need not touch the *phenomenal* one. And an optimist who holds that any
such access limit is contingently removable, so no principled gap follows.

### In plain terms

Here's a way a mind could end up stuck on its own "hard problem" without anything spooky being true.
Suppose, looked at from the outside, the system is fully explained — every step accounted for. But from
*inside*, it can't reach the facts that do the explaining (it can't watch its own gears turn) and it
has no internal route from "here's the mechanism" to "here's how it feels." Then it will sincerely
report: "I know how I work, yet that doesn't explain how my own thoughts appear to me" — and the gap is
real *for it*, even though the world holds no extra ingredient. It's McGinn's idea ("the answer exists
but we can't grasp it") with a concrete mechanism bolted on: the answer is locked away the same way
your own neurons firing are locked away from your experience. Marked **contested** because explaining an
*access* gap is not yet explaining the *felt* one — the load-bearing and disputed step.
