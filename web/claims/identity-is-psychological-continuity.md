What makes the person reading this sentence the same person as the child in an old photograph is
*psychology*: memory of earlier experiences, persistence of character, intentions later carried
out — direct connections of these kinds, chained together so that each day's person is strongly
connected to the last even where no single connection spans the decades. The criterion descends
from Locke, who located personal identity in continuity of consciousness rather than in soul or
body ([1689](../sources/locke-1689.md)); its modern, neo-Lockean form replaces Locke's simple memory
requirement with *overlapping chains* of connectedness — thereby answering Reid's old objection
that an officer might remember the boy while the general remembers only the officer
([1785](../sources/reid-1785.md)) — and standardly adds that the continuity must have the right kind of
cause (Parfit, [1984](../sources/parfit-1984.md)).

**Scope.** The claim gives a criterion of *numerical* identity over time — the
[diachronic question](../questions/personal-identity.md) — not an account of what a person is at a
moment. On it, a person would go where their psychology goes: into a new body in the
Brown/Brownson transplant case ([transplant-intuition](../arguments/transplant-intuition.md)), and, if the cause-clause is
generous, through teleportation or uploading. It is denied by
[animalists](../positions/animalism.md) ([identity-is-biological-continuity](identity-is-biological-continuity.md)), for whom we are
organisms with biological persistence conditions, and by
[further-fact theorists](identity-is-further-fact.md), for whom no continuity constitutes
identity. Fission cases press it from within ([fission](../arguments/fission.md)): psychological continuity can
hold one-to-many, and identity cannot.

### In plain terms

What carries *you* through time? This answer says: the running thread of your mental life — today
you remember yesterday, yesterday remembered the day before, and so on back to childhood, with your
personality and projects flowing along the same chain. You don't need any one memory to reach all
the way back; links of overlapping links will do, like a rope no single fibre of which runs its
whole length. The punchy consequence: if your whole mental life were moved into a new body, you'd
go with the mind, not stay with the old body.
