A belief is justified not by descending a one-way chain of reasons to some foundation, but by *fitting* —
cohering, mutually supporting, hanging together — within the believer's whole system of beliefs.
Justification, on this view, is a holistic property of the web, not a relation that flows from privileged
starting points to everything else. This is the coherentist's response to
[the Münchhausen trilemma](../concepts/munchhausen-trilemma.md): it embraces the *circularity* horn while denying
that the circle is vicious, because the relevant relation is system-wide coherence rather than a small
question-begging loop.

**Scope.** The claim concerns the *structure* of justification (holistic, not linear) and need not assert
any particular measure of coherence. It is compatible with strong holism (Neurath's boat, Quine's web of
belief, on which we rebuild the ship plank by plank at sea, with no dry dock) without committing to a
specific algorithm for what coheres with what.

**Who would deny it.** *Foundationalists* deny it, insisting on [basic
beliefs](some-beliefs-are-basic.md) that justify without themselves being justified by others — and pressing the *isolation
objection*: a maximally coherent system could still be a fiction, detached from the world. *Infinitists*
deny it from the opposite direction, rejecting any closed circle in favour of an open, non-repeating chain
([justification-is-infinite-chain](justification-is-infinite-chain.md)).

Distinct from [some-beliefs-are-basic](some-beliefs-are-basic.md), which it directly contradicts, and from
[justification-is-infinite-chain](justification-is-infinite-chain.md), the rival anti-foundationalist option that keeps the chain open
rather than closing it into a web.

### In plain terms

Coherentism's picture: beliefs hold each other up, like the timbers of a raft, rather than resting on a
single keel. A belief is justified when it fits snugly with everything else you believe — no foundation
required, because the support runs in all directions at once. Critics worry a perfectly *consistent* story
could still be a daydream with no contact with reality; that's the standing objection this view has to
answer.
