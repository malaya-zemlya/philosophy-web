The developed statement of **ultimate responsibility** (UR) and *self-forming actions*, and the
leading event-causal libertarian attempt to make [sourcehood](../concepts/sourcehood.md) intelligible without
[agent-causation](../concepts/agent-causation.md). Kane argues that an agent can be the ultimate source of her will even
when she could not have done otherwise, provided the character and motives her action flows from
were themselves formed by earlier undetermined choices — grounding the *historical* reading of the
source condition and its reply to the [frankfurt-cases](../concepts/frankfurt-cases.md).

Reference: Kane, R. (1996). *The significance of free will*. Oxford University Press.
