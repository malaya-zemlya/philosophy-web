relevance (content): Seager reconstructs and defends the *intrinsic-nature argument* for panpsychism —
the Russellian line, sharpened by Galen Strawson, that physics describes only the relational-structural
pattern of matter and so leaves its intrinsic nature blank, that this structure must have an intrinsic
ground, and that the one intrinsic nature we are acquainted with is consciousness. Grounds the argument
[intrinsic-nature](../arguments/intrinsic-nature.md) and its load-bearing premise [relations-grounded-in-intrinsic](../claims/relations-grounded-in-intrinsic.md), and
supplies the framing for [intrinsic-structural-distinction](../concepts/intrinsic-structural-distinction.md). Part 2 of the paper develops the
chief counter — *relationalism* / ontic structural realism and M. H. A. Newman's problem — which targets
that same premise.

Reference:
- Seager, W. (2006). The 'intrinsic nature' argument for panpsychism. *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, *13*(10–11), 129–145.
