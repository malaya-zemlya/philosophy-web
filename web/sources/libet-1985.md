The classic readiness-potential experiments: measurable brain preparation for a "spontaneous"
voluntary movement precedes the reported time of the conscious decision by several hundred
milliseconds, suggesting the conscious will is informed of, rather than the initiator of, the
action. Cited in [zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie](../claims/zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie.md) as the output-side partner of
input-side postdictive phenomena (color phi, backward masking) in the duality dictionary.

Reference: Libet, B. (1985). Unconscious cerebral initiative and the role of conscious will in
voluntary action. *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, *8*(4), 529–566.
https://doi.org/10.1017/S0140525X00044903
