relevance (content): grounds the counter-consequence pressed against the forgeable-rights-sign
argument — that resting moral standing on currently-unforgeable biology risks wrongly denying
standing to a genuinely sentient artificial system, a grave moral error rather than a safe
default. Cited by [two-sided-precaution](../arguments/two-sided-precaution.md) and its premise
[precaution-is-two-sided](../claims/precaution-is-two-sided.md). Schwitzgebel and Garza argue
that AIs of debatable moral status should be treated with a "principle of the ethical treatment
of AIs" that takes under-attribution as seriously as over-attribution.

Reference:
- Schwitzgebel, E., & Garza, M. (2015). A defense of the rights of artificial intelligences.
  *Midwest Studies in Philosophy*, *39*(1), 98–119. https://doi.org/10.1111/misp.12032
