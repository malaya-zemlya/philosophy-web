relevance (content): Jerry Fodor endorses a reductive *dispositionalist* account of meaning and
content — that what one means is constituted by one's dispositions to apply the term — at least for
logical vocabulary. This is the "straight solution" to the rule-following paradox that the sceptic
([kripke-1982](kripke-1982.md)) sets up and rejects. Fodor's version leans on idealization (optimal
conditions, unbounded memory) to answer Kripke's finitude and error objections. Grounds the
dispositionalist reply [dispositionalist-reply](../arguments/dispositionalist-reply.md) and its thesis
[meaning-constituted-by-dispositions](../claims/meaning-constituted-by-dispositions.md).

Reference:
- Fodor, J. A. (1990). *A theory of content and other essays*. MIT Press.
