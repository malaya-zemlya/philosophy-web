relevance (content): the founding paper of the mind-brain *identity theory*. Place argues that the
thesis "consciousness is a process in the brain" is a reasonable scientific hypothesis, not a
logical truth — an *a posteriori* ("is") identity on the model of "lightning is an electric
discharge," not a definitional one. He diagnoses the "phenomenological fallacy" that makes such an
identification seem impossible. Grounds [mental-states-are-brain-state-types](../claims/mental-states-are-brain-state-types.md) and
[type-identity](../positions/type-identity.md).

Reference:
- Place, U. T. (1956). Is consciousness a brain process? *British Journal of Psychology, 47*(1),
  44–50. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.2044-8295.1956.tb00560.x
