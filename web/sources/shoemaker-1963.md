relevance (content): Sydney Shoemaker's book introducing the *Brown/Brownson* brain-transplant
case: Brown's brain is placed in Robinson's body, and the resulting person ("Brownson") has Brown's
memories, personality, and sense of self. The near-universal verdict that Brownson *is* Brown is
the canonical intuition for psychological over biological criteria of personal identity. Grounds
[transplant-intuition](../arguments/transplant-intuition.md).

Reference:
- Shoemaker, S. (1963). *Self-knowledge and self-identity*. Cornell University Press.
