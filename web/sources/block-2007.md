relevance (content): the target article in which Ned Block argues that *phenomenal consciousness
overflows access* — we phenomenally experience more than gets cognitively accessed, broadcast, and
reported — so global availability is at best the wrong target for explaining felt experience.
Grounds the overflow objection cited against global workspace theory in
[global-workspace](../positions/global-workspace.md) and [consciousness-is-global-broadcast](../claims/consciousness-is-global-broadcast.md). Distinct from
Block's lookup-table paper ([block](block.md), 1981) and his Chinese Nation paper
([block-1978](block-1978.md), 1978).

Reference:
- Block, N. (2007). Consciousness, accessibility, and the mesh between psychology and neuroscience.
  *Behavioral and Brain Sciences, 30*(5–6), 481–499. https://doi.org/10.1017/S0140525X07002786
