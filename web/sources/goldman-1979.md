relevance (content): Alvin Goldman's statement of *process reliabilism*, the leading form of epistemic
externalism: a belief is justified just in case it is produced (and sustained) by a reliable,
truth-conducive cognitive process, whether or not the believer can cite reasons for it. Grounds
[epistemic-externalism](../positions/epistemic-externalism.md) and the externalist core of [knowledge-without-noncircular-proof](../claims/knowledge-without-noncircular-proof.md).

Reference:
- Goldman, A. I. (1979). What is justified belief? In G. S. Pappas (Ed.), *Justification and knowledge* (pp. 1–23). D. Reidel.
