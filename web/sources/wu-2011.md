Grounds the selection-for-action account of attention: attending to something is selecting it as
the input that guides what one does, which makes attention the input-side twin of choosing. This is
the independent support cited for [attention-is-dual-of-will](../claims/attention-is-dual-of-will.md) — the correspondence between
attention (input-side selection) and volition (output-side selection) in the
[consciousness-freewill-duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md).

Reference: Wu, W. (2011). Attention as selection for action. In C. Mole, D. Smithies, & W. Wu
(Eds.), *Attention: Philosophical and psychological essays* (pp. 97–116). Oxford University Press.
