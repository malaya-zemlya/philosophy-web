relevance (content): the historical origin of the knowledge-by-acquaintance / knowledge-by-description
distinction. Russell separates direct cognitive acquaintance with a thing from knowledge of truths
described about it. This distinction is the ancestor of the acquaintance category invoked by the
acquaintance hypothesis ([acquaintance-hypothesis](../arguments/acquaintance-hypothesis.md)).

Reference:
- Russell, B. (1911). Knowledge by acquaintance and knowledge by description. *Proceedings of the
  Aristotelian Society*, *11*, 108–128. https://doi.org/10.1093/aristotelian/11.1.108
