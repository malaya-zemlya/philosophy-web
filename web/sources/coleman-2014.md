relevance (content): Coleman's sharpening of the *combination problem* into its hardest form — the
"subject-summing" problem. Distinct micro-subjects, each with their own point of view and their own
phenomenal field, cannot be summed into a further macro-subject: the would-be composite subject's
experience is not entailed by, and would exclude, the experiences of its parts. Grounds the central
objection in [combination-problem](../arguments/combination-problem.md) and the objections section of
[panpsychism](../concepts/panpsychism.md).

Reference:
- Coleman, S. (2014). The real combination problem: Panpsychism, micro-subjects, and emergence. *Erkenntnis*, *79*(1), 19–44. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10670-013-9431-x
