relevance (content): the empirical anchor of the LLM global-workspace cluster. Using a new
interpretability technique, the *Jacobian lens*, the paper identifies a privileged subspace of
verbalizable representations in production language models (the [j-space](../concepts/j-space.md)) and shows
experimentally that it bears five functional properties associated with conscious access: verbal
report ([j-space-verbal-report](../claims/j-space-verbal-report.md)), directed modulation
([j-space-directed-modulation](../claims/j-space-directed-modulation.md)), internal reasoning ([j-space-internal-reasoning](../claims/j-space-internal-reasoning.md)),
flexible generalization ([j-space-flexible-generalization](../claims/j-space-flexible-generalization.md)), and selectivity
([j-space-selectivity](../claims/j-space-selectivity.md)). From these it concludes that language models maintain a functional
analog of a global workspace ([llm-global-workspace](../claims/llm-global-workspace.md), via
[llm-workspace-from-functional-signature](../arguments/llm-workspace-from-functional-signature.md)). Secondary findings imported here:
J-space ablation strips experiential language from self-reports
([j-space-ablation-experiential-language](../claims/j-space-ablation-experiential-language.md)); the workspace is present in base models before
post-training and privileges no point of view ([workspace-precedes-self](../claims/workspace-precedes-self.md)); and the authors
read the structure's emergence as convergent rather than biology-bound
([workspace-convergent-architecture](../claims/workspace-convergent-architecture.md)). The direct successor to
[lindsey-introspection-2025](lindsey-introspection-2025.md), from the same research group.

framing to preserve when citing:
- The paper is **explicitly functional**: "access consciousness is a purely functional notion; the
  relationship that it has with subjective experience (sometimes called phenomenal consciousness)
  is widely debated. In this paper, we take no position on this issue." Never cite it for a
  phenomenal-consciousness claim.
- It **disclaims the full architecture** of global workspace theory: no separable, encapsulated
  input processors; the broadcast it documents occurs within a single feedforward pass rather than
  through recurrent loops; and it is unclear whether competition for J-space entry mirrors the
  brain's ignition dynamics.
- The authors note their results are **not relevant** to theories that tie consciousness to
  physical causal structure or biological substrate, since the experiments concern computational
  mechanisms only.
