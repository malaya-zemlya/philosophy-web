relevance (content): empirical evidence that sequence-trained models build structured *internal world
models*, not just surface statistics. Testing seven language models trained only on Othello move sequences,
the authors find the models not only play legal Othello but *induce the board layout* internally with up to
99% accuracy, and converge on remarkably similar board features across architectures — strengthening the
"world model" reading of what next-token training produces. Grounds
[llm-text-yields-human-aligned-representations](../claims/llm-text-yields-human-aligned-representations.md) and the
[LLM-representations reply](../arguments/llm-representations-reply.md) on the *world-model* (as opposed to
perceptual-alignment) front.

Reference:
- Yuan, Y., & Søgaard, A. (2025). *Revisiting the Othello world model hypothesis* (arXiv:2503.04421). arXiv. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2503.04421
