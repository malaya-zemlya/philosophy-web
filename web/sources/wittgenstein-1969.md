relevance (content): the source of the idea of a [hinge proposition](../concepts/hinge-proposition.md). Writing
against G. E. Moore's anti-skeptical "proof," Wittgenstein argues that propositions such as "there are
physical objects," "the world has existed for a long time," and "here is a hand" are not conclusions we
have evidence for but the implicit *hinges* on which all inquiry and doubt turn — exempt from doubt because
doubting them would dismantle the practice of doubting itself. Grounds [hinge-proposition](../concepts/hinge-proposition.md),
[hinge-epistemology](../positions/hinge-epistemology.md), and the peculiar status of [external-world-exists](../claims/external-world-exists.md).

Reference:
- Wittgenstein, L. (1969). *On certainty* (G. E. M. Anscombe & G. H. von Wright, Eds.; D. Paul & G. E. M. Anscombe, Trans.). Blackwell. (Original work composed 1950–1951)
