relevance (content): the *indicator-properties* methodology — deriving functional "indicator
properties" from scientific theories of consciousness (global workspace theory, higher-order
theories, recurrent processing theory, attention schema theory, and others) and assessing AI
systems against them, on the working assumption of computational functionalism. This is the
methodological backdrop for [access-functional-signature](../claims/access-functional-signature.md) and for the empirical program of
[gurnee-2026-workspace](gurnee-2026-workspace.md), whose experiments can be read as direct tests of several such
indicators in production language models.

Reference:
- Butlin, P., Long, R., Elmoznino, E., Bengio, Y., Birch, J., Constant, A., Deane, G., Fleming, S. M., Frith, C., Ji, X., Kanai, R., Klein, C., Lindsay, G., Michel, M., Mudrik, L., Peters, M. A. K., Schwitzgebel, E., Simon, J., & VanRullen, R. (2023). *Consciousness in artificial intelligence: Insights from the science of consciousness*. arXiv. https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08708

framing to preserve when citing:
- The report does not claim any current AI system is conscious; it argues that no obvious technical
  barrier prevents near-term systems from satisfying many indicators, and that assessment should be
  theory-derived rather than behaviour-based.
