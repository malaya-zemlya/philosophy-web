relevance (content): Chalmers' dedicated technical statement of **two-dimensional semantics** in its
*epistemic* form — the framework that splits a term's meaning into a **primary (epistemic) intension**
and a **secondary (subjunctive) intension**. Grounds [primary-intension](../concepts/primary-intension.md) and
[secondary-intension](../concepts/secondary-intension.md), and underwrites the modal apparatus deployed in
[modal-rationalism](../arguments/modal-rationalism.md), [aposteriori-necessities-are-misdescribed-possibilities](../claims/aposteriori-necessities-are-misdescribed-possibilities.md),
and [phenomenal-intensions-coincide](../claims/phenomenal-intensions-coincide.md). The terminology originates in
*The Conscious Mind* ([chalmers-1996](chalmers-1996.md), ch. 2); this paper is the refined defence.

Reference:
- Chalmers, D. J. (2002). On sense and intension. *Philosophical Perspectives*, *16*, 135–182.
  (The two-dimensional framework as an account of the *epistemic* dimension of meaning: primary
  intensions defined over worlds considered as actual, secondary intensions over worlds considered as
  counterfactual; the bearing on the necessary a posteriori and on the conceivability–possibility link.)
