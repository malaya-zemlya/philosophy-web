relevance (content): Donald Davidson's statement of *anomalous monism* — every mental event is a
physical event (token identity), yet there are no strict laws couched in mental vocabulary, so the
mental is real, causally efficacious, and irreducible at once. The paper also made "supervenience"
a standard term in philosophy of mind. Grounds [anomalous-monism](../positions/anomalous-monism.md) and
[no-strict-psychophysical-laws](../claims/no-strict-psychophysical-laws.md).

Reference:
- Davidson, D. (1970). Mental events. In L. Foster & J. W. Swanson (Eds.), *Experience and theory*
  (pp. 79–101). University of Massachusetts Press.
