relevance (content): Dennett's reply to critics of *Consciousness Explained*, containing the
sharpest statement of his positive account of consciousness — *cerebral celebrity*, later
"fame in the brain": "Consciousness is cerebral celebrity — nothing more and nothing less. Those
contents are conscious that persevere, that monopolize resources long enough to achieve certain
typical and 'symptomatic' effects — on memory, on the control of behavior, and especially on
verbal report." This grounds [consciousness-is-cerebral-celebrity](../claims/consciousness-is-cerebral-celebrity.md), the deflationary,
verbalization-weighted rival to the broadcast formulation of
[consciousness-is-global-broadcast](../claims/consciousness-is-global-broadcast.md), and the philosophical anticipation of the
verbalizability-defined workspace of [gurnee-2026-workspace](gurnee-2026-workspace.md). The underlying model
(Multiple Drafts, probing, heterophenomenology) is developed in [dennett-1991](dennett-1991.md).

Reference:
- Dennett, D. C. (1993). The message is: There is no medium. *Philosophy and Phenomenological Research*, *53*(4), 919–931. https://doi.org/10.2307/2108259
