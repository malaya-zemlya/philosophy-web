relevance (content): the modern *genetic argument* for panpsychism. Strawson argues that a thorough
physicalist who is also a *realist* about experience cannot accept that experience emerges, "brutely"
and without intelligible connection, from the wholly non-experiential — so experience (or its
proto-form) must already be present in the fundamental physical constituents. Grounds the case-for
section of [panpsychism](../concepts/panpsychism.md) and the claim
[consciousness-fundamental-ubiquitous](../claims/consciousness-fundamental-ubiquitous.md).

Reference:
- Strawson, G. (2006). Realistic monism: Why physicalism entails panpsychism. *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, *13*(10–11), 3–31.
