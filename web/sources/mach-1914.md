relevance (content): a historical root of neutral monism. Mach analyses the world into neutral
*elements* (colours, sounds, pressures) that count as physical when taken in their mutual
dependencies and as psychological when taken in their dependence on a living body — mind and
matter as groupings of one neutral stuff. Grounds [neutral-monism](../positions/neutral-monism.md) and
[reality-fundamental-stuff-neutral](../claims/reality-fundamental-stuff-neutral.md).

Reference:
- Mach, E. (1914). *The analysis of sensations, and the relation of the physical to the psychical*
  (C. M. Williams, Trans.; rev. and supplemented from the 5th German ed. by S. Waterlow). Open
  Court. (Original work published 1886)
