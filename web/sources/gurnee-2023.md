relevance (content): evidence that LLMs build representations of *real-world* structure, not just synthetic
games. Probing across datasets of places and events, the authors find *linear* representations of space and
time at multiple scales, and identify individual neurons that encode spatial and temporal coordinates —
which they describe as the "basic ingredients of a world model." Extends the world-model evidence behind
[llm-text-yields-human-aligned-representations](../claims/llm-text-yields-human-aligned-representations.md) from game boards to the structure of physical
reality, and supports the [llm-representations-reply](../arguments/llm-representations-reply.md).

Reference:
- Gurnee, W., & Tegmark, M. (2024). Language models represent space and time. In *Proceedings of the 12th International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR 2024)*. https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2310.02207
