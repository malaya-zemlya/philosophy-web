relevance (content): the paper that introduced the *multiple-realizability* argument against the
type-identity theory and launched functionalism. Putnam argues that a single mental state (e.g.
pain) can be realised in physically very different systems — mammals, molluscs, conceivably
silicon — so it cannot be *identical* to any one physical type; better to individuate mental states
by functional/causal role. Later reprinted as "The Nature of Mental States." Grounds the
multiple-realizability objection cited by [type-identity](../positions/type-identity.md) and
[mental-states-are-brain-state-types](../claims/mental-states-are-brain-state-types.md). Distinct from [putnam-1963](putnam-1963.md) (*Brains and
Behavior*), which targets behaviourism rather than identity theory.

Reference:
- Putnam, H. (1967). Psychological predicates. In W. H. Capitan & D. D. Merrill (Eds.), *Art, mind,
  and religion* (pp. 37–48). University of Pittsburgh Press. (Reprinted as "The nature of mental
  states" in Putnam, H. (1975). *Mind, language and reality: Philosophical papers, Vol. 2*
  (pp. 429–440). Cambridge University Press.)
