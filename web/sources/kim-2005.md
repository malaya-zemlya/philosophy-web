relevance (content): the canonical development of the **causal exclusion (supervenience) argument**,
which sharpens the role of the **causal closure of the physical** and the **exclusion / no-over­determination**
principle. Grounds [causal-closure](../concepts/causal-closure.md), [no-systematic-overdetermination](../claims/no-systematic-overdetermination.md), and the
dilemma physicalism presses on the dualist — reduce the mental or accept [epiphenomenalism](../positions/epiphenomenalism.md).
Kim states the exclusion principle as: no single event has more than one sufficient cause at a given
time unless it is a genuine case of overdetermination.

Reference:
- Kim, J. (2005). *Physicalism, or something near enough*. Princeton University Press.
