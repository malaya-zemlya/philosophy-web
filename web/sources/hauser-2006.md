Argues that human beings possess an innate *moral faculty* — a "universal moral grammar," modeled on
the notion of an innate language faculty — that delivers rapid, largely unconscious judgments of
right and wrong prior to and independent of conscious reasoning. This grounds the instinct-first half
of [responsibility-partly-instinctual](../claims/responsibility-partly-instinctual.md): our verdicts about who is to
blame issue in significant part from evolved intuition rather than deliberation. (Hauser's
subsequent research-misconduct findings concern his animal-cognition work, not the moral-psychology
synthesis of this book, which reports a broad literature.)

Reference: Hauser, M. D. (2006). *Moral minds: How nature designed our universal sense of right and wrong.* Ecco/HarperCollins.
