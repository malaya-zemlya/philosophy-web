relevance (content): Nagel argues that in commissurotomy ("split-brain") patients there is no whole
number of minds that correctly counts the subjects present — the unity of consciousness breaks down by
degrees, and the question "how many subjects?" has no determinate answer. The classic source for the
*indeterminacy* and gradedness of subject-count under varying interhemispheric integration. Grounds
[subject-unity-graded-by-integration](../claims/subject-unity-graded-by-integration.md) and the split-brain case in
[subjects-actually-merge-and-divide](../claims/subjects-actually-merge-and-divide.md).

Reference:
- Nagel, T. (1971). Brain bisection and the unity of consciousness. *Synthese*, *22*(3–4), 396–413. https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00413435
