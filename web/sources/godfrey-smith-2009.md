relevance (content): examines the triviality arguments against functionalism and presses that even
counterfactual/dispositional constraints on implementation can be met by gerrymandered mappings, so
the constraint does not by itself secure a non-trivial notion of computation. Cited as a denier of
[implementation-requires-counterfactual-structure](../claims/implementation-requires-counterfactual-structure.md).

Reference:
- Godfrey-Smith, P. (2009). Triviality arguments against functionalism. *Philosophical Studies,
  145*(2), 273–295. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11098-008-9231-3
