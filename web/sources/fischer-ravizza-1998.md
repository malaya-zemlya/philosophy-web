Distinguishes **regulative control** (the power to do otherwise) from **guidance control**
(reasons-responsive ownership of the actual sequence), and argues moral responsibility requires
only the latter. Cited in [zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie](../claims/zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie.md) as the free-will
distinction paired with Block's phenomenal/access distinction in the duality dictionary, and in
[compatibilism](../positions/compatibilism.md) as a leading contemporary form of the view.

Reference: Fischer, J. M., & Ravizza, M. (1998). *Responsibility and control: A theory of moral
responsibility*. Cambridge University Press.
