relevance (content): grounds the two-sided reading of precaution about sentience — that a
precautionary policy must weigh the cost of wrongly excluding a sentient mind alongside the cost
of wrongly including a non-sentient one, rather than defaulting to the mark that is merely hardest
to fake. Cited by [two-sided-precaution](../arguments/two-sided-precaution.md),
[unforgeability-proxy](../arguments/unforgeability-proxy.md), and the premise
[precaution-is-two-sided](../claims/precaution-is-two-sided.md). Birch develops a framework of
"sentience candidates" and proportionate precautions in which both error directions carry moral
weight.

Reference:
- Birch, J. (2024). *The edge of sentience: Risk and precaution in humans, other animals, and AI*.
  Oxford University Press. https://doi.org/10.1093/9780191966729.001.0001
