relevance (content): the origin of epiphenomenalism about consciousness and of the "steam-whistle"
metaphor — conscious feeling as a by-product of brain activity that does no causal work. Grounds
[epiphenomenalism](../positions/epiphenomenalism.md) and [mental-states-causally-inert](../claims/mental-states-causally-inert.md).

Reference:
- Huxley, T. H. (1874). On the hypothesis that animals are automata, and its history. *The
  Fortnightly Review, 16*(95), 555–580. (Reprinted in *Collected essays: Vol. 1. Method and
  results*, pp. 199–250, Macmillan, 1893)
