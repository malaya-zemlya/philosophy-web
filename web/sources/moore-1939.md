relevance (content): G. E. Moore's famous "proof" — holding up a hand and declaring "here is one hand,"
then another, "here is another," and concluding that external things exist. The proof is valid and its
premises arguably known, yet it is the textbook case of an argument that fails to *move* the skeptic,
because warrant for "here is a hand" is not available independently of the external-world conclusion it is
meant to establish. Cited as the foil for [external-world-unprovable-noncircular](../claims/external-world-unprovable-noncircular.md) and as the target
Wittgenstein wrote [On Certainty](wittgenstein-1969.md) against.

Reference:
- Moore, G. E. (1939). Proof of an external world. *Proceedings of the British Academy*, *25*, 273–300.
