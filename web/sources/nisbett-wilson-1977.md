relevance (content): the classic experimental survey of *confabulation* in self-report: subjects
confidently cite causes of their own choices and judgments that demonstrably were not operative
(and miss ones that were), suggesting that reports on one's own mental processes are generated from
a priori causal theories rather than by direct inner observation. Standard empirical support for
treating introspection as constructed representation; grounds
[introspective-confabulation](../arguments/introspective-confabulation.md).

Reference:
- Nisbett, R. E., & Wilson, T. D. (1977). Telling more than we can know: Verbal reports on mental
  processes. *Psychological Review*, *84*(3), 231–259.
