relevance (content): the original Gödelian anti-mechanism argument — that because a human can see
the truth of a Gödel sentence unprovable in any given consistent formal system, the mind cannot be a
machine. Revived and extended by Penrose ([penrose-1989](penrose-1989.md), [penrose-1994](penrose-1994.md));
grounds the Gödelian premise in [consciousness-noncomputable](../claims/consciousness-noncomputable.md) and
[noncomputable-consciousness](../positions/noncomputable-consciousness.md).

Reference:
- Lucas, J. R. (1961). Minds, machines and Gödel. *Philosophy, 36*(137), 112–127.
  https://doi.org/10.1017/S0031819100057983
