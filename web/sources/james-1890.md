relevance (content): in the chapter "The Mind-Stuff Theory," James presses what later became the
*combination problem*: a hundred feelings, however arranged, do not by themselves sum into one
hundred-and-first feeling that contains them — each unit of experience is its own self-contained
whole, and there is no aggregating felt states the way one aggregates physical "mind-dust." The
historical source for [combination-problem](../arguments/combination-problem.md) and the standing
objection to constitutive [panpsychism](../concepts/panpsychism.md).

Reference:
- James, W. (1890). *The principles of psychology* (Vol. 1). Henry Holt. (See ch. 6, "The mind-stuff theory.")
