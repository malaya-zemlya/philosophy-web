relevance (content): the source of the "stochastic parrot" characterisation — that a large
language model is a system for haphazardly stitching together sequences of linguistic form
according to probabilistic information about how they combine, without reference to meaning.
Grounds the specific rival explanation the trained-mimicry objection names: the behavioural sign
is antecedently well explained by high-fidelity distributional imitation induced by the
next-token training objective. Cited by [trained-mimicry-rival](../arguments/trained-mimicry-rival.md)
and its premise [llm-mimicry-rival](../claims/llm-mimicry-rival.md).

Reference:
- Bender, E. M., Gebru, T., McMillan-Major, A., & Shmitchell, S. (2021). On the dangers of
  stochastic parrots: Can language models be too big? In *Proceedings of the 2021 ACM Conference
  on Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency* (pp. 610–623). Association for Computing
  Machinery. https://doi.org/10.1145/3442188.3445922
