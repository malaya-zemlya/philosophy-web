relevance (physics premise): the paper that quantifies the maximum number of elementary logical
operations the observable universe can have performed, and the maximum information it can register
— on the order of $10^{120}$ bits / $10^{120}$ operations — from the Bekenstein/holographic and
relativistic bounds on energy and information density. It grounds the finite-storage premise used
in this web ([observable-universe-finite-storage](../claims/observable-universe-finite-storage.md)) and, through it, the cosmic-size reply
to the lookup-table objection ([lookup-table-physical-impossibility](../arguments/lookup-table-physical-impossibility.md)).

Reference:
- Lloyd, S. (2002). Computational capacity of the universe. *Physical Review Letters, 88*(23),
  Article 237901. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.88.237901
