relevance (content): the source of the **two-gods case** and of the doctrine that self-locating
(*de se*) belief is irreducible to *de dicto* belief about the world — a being who knew the whole
"world-book" could still fail to know which thing in it is itself. Supplies premise (1) of the
[indexical-reply](../arguments/indexical-reply.md) to the knowledge argument: self-locating truths are not deducible from
any complete objective description, and that silence is no defect.

Reference:
- Lewis, D. (1979). Attitudes de dicto and de se. *The Philosophical Review*, *88*(4), 513–543.
  (Reprinted in D. Lewis, *Philosophical papers, Volume I*, pp. 133–159, Oxford University Press,
  1983.)
