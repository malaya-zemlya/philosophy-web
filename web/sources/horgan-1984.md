relevance (content): an early anticipation of the *old-fact / new-guise* reply to the knowledge
argument, arguing that Mary's new knowledge is a new mode of presentation of physical information she
already possessed rather than knowledge of a new fact. Cited as the precursor to
[mary-gains-old-fact-new-guise](../claims/mary-gains-old-fact-new-guise.md) / [old-fact-new-guise](../arguments/old-fact-new-guise.md).

Also grounds the *scope* objection to the knowledge argument: Horgan presses that Mary's
new knowledge is at most non-*deducible* from the physical description, and that a truth's
non-deducibility from physical premises need not mark a non-physical fact — so the argument
bears only on an a-priori/deducibility physicalism and leaves a-posteriori (token-identity)
physicalism untouched. Cited in that role by [mary-scope-reply](../arguments/mary-scope-reply.md).

Reference:
- Horgan, T. (1984). Jackson on physical information and qualia. *The Philosophical Quarterly*,
  *34*(135), 147–152. https://doi.org/10.2307/2219508
