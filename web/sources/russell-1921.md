relevance (content): Russell's fullest book-length statement of *neutral monism* — reality is
composed of one basic kind of stuff, neither mental nor physical, of which mind and matter are
more complex arrangements. Grounds [neutral-monism](../positions/neutral-monism.md) and
[reality-fundamental-stuff-neutral](../claims/reality-fundamental-stuff-neutral.md). Distinct from [russell-1927](russell-1927.md) (*The Analysis
of Matter*), which grounds the categorical/intrinsic-nature line behind Russellian monism.

Reference:
- Russell, B. (1921). *The analysis of mind*. George Allen & Unwin.
