relevance (content): the founding text of hylomorphism about the mind — the soul defined as the
*form* (first actuality) of a living body that has life potentially within it. Grounds
[hylomorphism](../positions/hylomorphism.md) and [soul-is-form-of-body](../claims/soul-is-form-of-body.md).

Reference:
- Aristotle. (1984). On the soul (J. A. Smith, Trans.). In J. Barnes (Ed.), *The complete works of
  Aristotle: The revised Oxford translation* (Vol. 1, pp. 641–692). Princeton University Press.
  (Original work composed ca. 350 BCE)
