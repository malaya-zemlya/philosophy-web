relevance (content): the paper in which Chalmers develops *direct phenomenal concepts* — concepts
that pick out a phenomenal quality by direct acquaintance with it, the quality partly constituting
the concept — to defend the special epistemic standing of phenomenal belief against deflationary
readings. Cited in [acquaintance-datum-or-representation](../questions/acquaintance-datum-or-representation.md) as a representative of the
datum / acquaintance answer. Distinct from [chalmers-2003](chalmers-2003.md) (*Consciousness and Its Place in
Nature*), a different 2003 paper on the structural-dynamical limits of physics.

Reference:
- Chalmers, D. J. (2003). The content and epistemology of phenomenal belief. In Q. Smith & A. Jokic
  (Eds.), *Consciousness: New philosophical perspectives* (pp. 220–272). Oxford University Press.
