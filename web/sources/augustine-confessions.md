Book VIII contains the canonical phenomenology of the divided will: Augustine, on the verge of
conversion, commands himself to will wholly and finds "two wills" in conflict — "it was I who
willed, I who was unwilling; it was I, I myself." Cited in
[unity-dualizes-to-unity-of-agency](../claims/unity-dualizes-to-unity-of-agency.md) as a classic case of agency failing to be one, the
output-side mirror of failures in the unity of consciousness.

Reference: Augustine. (1991). *Confessions* (H. Chadwick, Trans.). Oxford University Press.
(Original work published ca. 397–400 CE)
