relevance (content): Christian List and Peter Menzies's difference-making reply to causal
exclusion: on an interventionist account of causation, a higher-level mental property — rather than
its lower-level realizer — can be the cause of an effect when the effect is insensitive to which
realizer occurs, so the exclusion principle fails in exactly the cases the non-reductive
physicalist cares about. Grounds [mental-causes-are-difference-makers](../claims/mental-causes-are-difference-makers.md).

Reference:
- List, C., & Menzies, P. (2009). Nonreductive physicalism and the limits of the exclusion
  principle. *The Journal of Philosophy*, *106*(9), 475–502.
