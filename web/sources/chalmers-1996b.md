relevance (content): the canonical reply to the triviality theorems of Putnam ([putnam-1988](putnam-1988.md))
and Searle. Chalmers argues that genuine implementation requires the right *counterfactual*
state-transition structure — dispositions holding across the whole transition table, not merely a
mapping fitted to the states that actually occurred — which rules out the gerrymandered mappings that
make triviality run. The result secures *non-triviality* but is explicit that it does not yield a
*unique* implementation. Grounds [implementation-requires-counterfactual-structure](../claims/implementation-requires-counterfactual-structure.md),
[counterfactual-structure-underdetermines-decomposition](../claims/counterfactual-structure-underdetermines-decomposition.md), and
[causal-structure-supplies-invariant](../arguments/causal-structure-supplies-invariant.md). Distinct from [chalmers-1996](chalmers-1996.md) (*The
Conscious Mind*).

Reference:
- Chalmers, D. J. (1996). Does a rock implement every finite-state automaton? *Synthese, 108*(3),
  309–333. https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00413692
