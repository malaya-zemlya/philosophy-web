relevance (content): the primary clinical paper identifying the genetic basis of the pain (and
anxiety) insensitivity of the patient since widely known as **Jo Cameron**. A 66-year-old woman
presented requiring no analgesia after normally painful hand surgery, with a lifelong history of
painless injuries and burns; sequencing revealed a microdeletion in the pseudogene *FAAH-OUT*
together with a hypomorphic SNP in *FAAH*, yielding elevated anandamide (an endocannabinoid) and
near-absent pain. Grounds [jo-cameron](../concepts/jo-cameron.md) — the real-world case the super-spartan cluster
points to, distinguished from [pain-asymbolia](../concepts/pain-asymbolia.md) in that here the pain sensation itself is
largely absent rather than merely stripped of its affect.

Reference:
- Habib, A. M., Okorokov, A. L., Hill, M. N., Bras, J. T., Lee, M.-C., Li, S., Gossage, S. J.,
  van Drimmelen, M., Morena, M., Houlden, H., Ramirez, J. D., Bennett, D. L. H., Srivastava, D.,
  & Cox, J. J. (2019). Microdeletion in a FAAH pseudogene identified in a patient with high
  anandamide concentrations and pain insensitivity. *British Journal of Anaesthesia*, *123*(2),
  e249–e253. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bja.2019.02.019
