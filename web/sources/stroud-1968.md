relevance (content): Barry Stroud's classic critique of [transcendental
arguments](../concepts/transcendental-argument.md) against the skeptic. He argues that, on their own, such arguments establish at most that we
must *believe* or *conceive* the world to be a certain way in order to think or speak at all — not that it
*is* that way; bridging "must presuppose" to "is true" requires a verificationist premise the skeptic can
refuse. Grounds the weak point of [retorsion-against-skepticism](../arguments/retorsion-against-skepticism.md) and the critical assessment in
[transcendental-argument](../concepts/transcendental-argument.md).

Reference:
- Stroud, B. (1968). Transcendental arguments. *The Journal of Philosophy*, *65*(9), 241–256.
