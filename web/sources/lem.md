relevance (content): Lem develops the point that a being cannot prove its consciousness to another,
and — crucially — that this unprovability is *mutual*: it runs symmetrically between human and
machine, neither party being able to demonstrate an inner life to the other. This is the source of
the symmetry move used against [first-person-access](../arguments/first-person-access.md) (see [access-gap-symmetric](../claims/access-gap-symmetric.md),
[access-symmetry-rebuttal](../arguments/access-symmetry-rebuttal.md)).

key passages: **work and page references to be confirmed.** Candidate loci in Lem's corpus include
*Summa Technologiae* (1964) and *Golem XIV* (1981); the precise text should be pinned before this
node is cited in a treatise. Recorded now for provenance per the import; flagged as needing a
citation check (cf. /ars-citation-check).

*form* (Cleanthes/Philo/Demea). The imported argument reached the web via a prior-run pastiche
dialogue (*О правах непротеиновых существ*), but its genuine intellectual provenance for the
symmetry point is Lem, recorded here.
