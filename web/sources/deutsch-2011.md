relevance (content): Deutsch's fuller statement of his explanation-centred epistemology — that a *good
explanation* is one that is **hard to vary** while still accounting for what it explains, and that
knowledge grows by conjecture and criticism rather than by derivation from foundations. Grounds
[good-explanation-is-hard-to-vary](../claims/good-explanation-is-hard-to-vary.md), reinforces [all-knowledge-is-conjectural](../claims/all-knowledge-is-conjectural.md) and
[fallibilism](../positions/fallibilism.md), and supplies the criterion used in [deutsch-anti-solipsism](../arguments/deutsch-anti-solipsism.md).

Reference:
- Deutsch, D. (2011). *The beginning of infinity: Explanations that transform the world*. Viking.
