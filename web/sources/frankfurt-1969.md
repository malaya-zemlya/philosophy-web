Introduces the counterexample style now called the Frankfurt case — a counterfactual intervener who
never intervenes — against the principle that moral responsibility requires the ability to do
otherwise. Grounds [frankfurt-cases](../concepts/frankfurt-cases.md) and, through it, the novel correspondence
[frankfurt-dualizes-to-ganzfeld](../claims/frankfurt-dualizes-to-ganzfeld.md) in the consciousness–free-will duality dictionary.

Reference: Frankfurt, H. G. (1969). Alternate possibilities and moral responsibility. *The Journal
of Philosophy*, *66*(23), 829–839. https://doi.org/10.2307/2023833
