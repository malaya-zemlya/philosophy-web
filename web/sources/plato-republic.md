Book IV divides the soul into reason, spirit, and appetite, arguing from cases of inner conflict —
wanting to drink and refusing to drink at once — that one thing cannot simultaneously do opposites
in the same respect. The founding text for the disunity of agency; cited in
[unity-dualizes-to-unity-of-agency](../claims/unity-dualizes-to-unity-of-agency.md) as the output-side partner of binding failures on the
input side.

Reference: Plato. (1992). *Republic* (G. M. A. Grube, Trans.; C. D. C. Reeve, Rev.). Hackett
Publishing. (Original work published ca. 375 BCE)
