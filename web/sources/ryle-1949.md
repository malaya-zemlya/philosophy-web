relevance (content): Gilbert Ryle's attack on Cartesian dualism as the dogma of the **"ghost in the
machine"** — the charge that dualism rests on a *category mistake*, miscasting mental concepts (which, he
argues, are really about behavioural dispositions) as descriptions of inner, non-physical episodes. The
founding statement of *logical behaviourism* and a landmark in the deflationary wing of the
[mind-body problem](../concepts/mind-body-problem.md). The phrase "the ghost in the machine" is Ryle's coinage.

Reference:
- Ryle, G. (1949). *The concept of mind*. Hutchinson.
