relevance (content): the book that turned the clinical data on **pain asymbolia** and pain
*without* unpleasantness into a sharp philosophical distinction between *feeling pain* (the sensory
discrimination of a noxious state, which asymbolics retain) and *being in pain* (the
affective-motivational state that makes pain matter, which they lack). Supplies the conceptual
framing for [pain-asymbolia](../concepts/pain-asymbolia.md) and the standard reference for the claim that pain's sensory
and affective dimensions are dissociable.

Reference:
- Grahek, N. (2007). *Feeling pain and being in pain* (2nd ed.). MIT Press.
