relevance (content): Ruth Millikan's foundational statement of *teleosemantics*: intentional
content is fixed by *proper functions* — what a representation-producing or -consuming mechanism
was selected for doing — so meaning is a biological category on a par with a heart's function of
pumping blood. The account's signature strength is a principled treatment of misrepresentation (a
state means what it was selected to indicate, even on occasions when it misfires). Grounds
[teleosemantics](../positions/teleosemantics.md) and [intentional-content-from-biological-function](../claims/intentional-content-from-biological-function.md).

Reference:
- Millikan, R. G. (1984). *Language, thought, and other biological categories: New foundations for
  realism*. MIT Press.
