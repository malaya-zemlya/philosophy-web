The standard modern statement of incompatibilism and of the **Consequence Argument**: if
determinism is true, our acts are consequences of the laws and the remote past, and neither is up
to us, so nothing is up to us. Grounds [alternatives-genuinely-open](../claims/alternatives-genuinely-open.md) as the leeway
incompatibilist's central commitment, and is cited in
[agent-causation-certificate](../arguments/agent-causation-certificate.md) for the caveat that the conceivability-certificate
transfer does *not* extend to inference-rule arguments of this kind.

Reference: van Inwagen, P. (1983). *An essay on free will*. Clarendon Press.
