The canonical statement of the **[disappearing-agent objection](../arguments/disappearing-agent.md)**
(ch. 2): on event-causal
libertarianism nothing *settles* which decision occurs — the agent, as a source of resolution,
disappears from the causal story. Grounds [exclusion-dualizes-to-disappearing-agent](../claims/exclusion-dualizes-to-disappearing-agent.md), where
the objection is identified as the arrow-reversed image of Kim's causal-exclusion argument, and
supplies the modern form of hard incompatibilism discussed under [hard-determinism](../positions/hard-determinism.md).

Reference: Pereboom, D. (2014). *Free will, agency, and meaning in life*. Oxford University Press.
