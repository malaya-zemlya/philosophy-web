Introduces linear logic, whose resource-sensitive connectives split conjunction and disjunction
each into two (⊗ and &; ⊕ and ⅋) and sort them by *polarity* into passive (input-like) and active
(committing) connectives. Cited in [graded-polarity](../arguments/graded-polarity.md) as the refined formal setting for
the graded version of the product/coproduct assignment in the
[consciousness-freewill-duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md), where the crude two-connective picture idealizes both
perception and action.

Reference: Girard, J.-Y. (1987). Linear logic. *Theoretical Computer Science*, *50*(1), 1–101.
https://doi.org/10.1016/0304-3975(87)90045-4
