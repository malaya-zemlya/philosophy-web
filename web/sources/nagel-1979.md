relevance (content): Nagel's measured statement that panpsychism — the view that the basic physical
constituents of the universe have mental properties whether or not they are parts of organisms —
follows from a small set of otherwise attractive premises (the reality of experience, no radical
emergence, the non-reducibility of the mental, and the denial of non-physical souls). A canonical
articulation of the *continuity* and *anti-emergence* motivations behind
[panpsychism](../concepts/panpsychism.md).

Reference:
- Nagel, T. (1979). Panpsychism. In *Mortal questions* (pp. 181–195). Cambridge University Press.
