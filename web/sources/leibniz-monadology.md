relevance (content): §17 is the *mill argument*, an early ancestor of the hard problem and the
explanatory gap — imagine a thinking machine enlarged so you could walk inside it "as into a mill";
inspecting it you find only parts pushing one another, never anything that explains a perception.
Cited in [hard-problem-conceived-de-novo-by-humans](../claims/hard-problem-conceived-de-novo-by-humans.md) as the head of the human lineage that
originated hard-problem thinking with no prior template.

Reference:
- Leibniz, G. W. (1989). The principles of philosophy, or, the monadology (R. Ariew & D. Garber,
  Trans.). In *Philosophical essays* (pp. 213–225). Hackett. (Original work published 1714)
