relevance (content): the book in which Searle names and develops *biological naturalism* — the view
that consciousness is a real, higher-level biological feature caused by and realised in
neurobiological processes, ontologically irreducible (first-personal) yet nothing "over and above"
the brain's concrete causal powers, with the digestion analogy. Grounds
[biological-naturalism](../positions/biological-naturalism.md) and [consciousness-is-biological-feature](../claims/consciousness-is-biological-feature.md).

Reference:
- Searle, J. R. (1992). *The rediscovery of the mind*. MIT Press.
