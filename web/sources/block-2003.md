relevance (content): Ned Block's generalization objection to Kim's causal exclusion argument: if
supervening properties are always causally pre-empted by their subvening bases, causal power drains
downward past chemistry and biology to fundamental physics — and if there is no fundamental level,
it drains away entirely. Either the exclusion principle overreaches, or the sciences above physics
lose their causal subject matter. Grounds [exclusion-generalizes](../arguments/exclusion-generalizes.md).

Reference:
- Block, N. (2003). Do causal powers drain away? *Philosophy and Phenomenological Research*,
  *67*(1), 133–150.
