relevance (content): supplies the counter-analogy against the "integration composes a subject"
inference — a densely information-integrated group agent (the population of the United States)
that satisfies the functional/integration conditions yet whose status as a felt subject most
treat as a reductio. Cited by [conscious-usa-counter-analogy](../arguments/conscious-usa-counter-analogy.md)
against [subjects-can-combine](../arguments/subjects-can-combine.md). Note: Schwitzgebel himself
runs the case as a *modus ponens* (materialism implies the USA is probably conscious, and he does
not reject the consequent); the objection borrows the widely shared intuitive verdict rather than
Schwitzgebel's own conclusion.

Reference:
- Schwitzgebel, E. (2015). If materialism is true, the United States is probably conscious.
  *Philosophical Studies*, *172*(7), 1697–1721. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11098-014-0387-8
