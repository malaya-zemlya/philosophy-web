relevance (content): the classic statement of Popper's *critical rationalism* — that knowledge is not
built up by justification from secure foundations but grows by bold conjecture and severe attempted
refutation, so all knowledge is fallible and corrigible. Grounds [all-knowledge-is-conjectural](../claims/all-knowledge-is-conjectural.md)
and [fallibilism](../positions/fallibilism.md), and supplies the anti-justificationist backdrop to
[munchhausen-trilemma](../concepts/munchhausen-trilemma.md).

Reference:
- Popper, K. R. (1963). *Conjectures and refutations: The growth of scientific knowledge*. Routledge & Kegan Paul.
