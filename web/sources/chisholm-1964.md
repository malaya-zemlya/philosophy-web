The classic statement of agent-causal libertarianism: when we act freely we are unmoved movers —
each of us, in Chisholm's phrase, "a prime mover unmoved" — causing events without being caused to
cause them by prior events. Grounds [agent-causation](../concepts/agent-causation.md) and the libertarian pole of the
free-will dispute ([libertarianism](../positions/libertarianism.md)).

Reference: Chisholm, R. M. (1964). *Human freedom and the self* (The Lindley Lecture). Department
of Philosophy, University of Kansas.
