relevance (content): Eric Olson's book-length defence of *animalism*: we are human animals, and our
persistence conditions are those of living organisms — biological, not psychological, continuity.
Home of the "thinking-animal" (too-many-thinkers) argument: the animal in your chair thinks your
thoughts, so unless you are that animal there are two thinkers where common sense finds one.
Grounds [animalism](../positions/animalism.md), [identity-is-biological-continuity](../claims/identity-is-biological-continuity.md), and
[too-many-thinkers](../arguments/too-many-thinkers.md).

Reference:
- Olson, E. T. (1997). *The human animal: Personal identity without psychology*. Oxford University
  Press.
