relevance (content): Laurence Nemirow's defence of the ability analysis of knowing what an
experience is like, developed independently of and alongside David Lewis. Nemirow holds that
becoming acquainted with an experience confers practical abilities rather than propositional
knowledge, so Mary acquires know-how and no new fact. (His "acquaintance" is this practical
know-how, not the rival acquaintance analysis of [mary-gains-acquaintance](../claims/mary-gains-acquaintance.md), on which Mary
gains a non-factual *knowledge of* the experience.) A co-source, with [lewis-1988](lewis-1988.md), for
[ability-hypothesis](../arguments/ability-hypothesis.md).

key passage:
- Nemirow, Laurence (1990), "Physicalism and the Cognitive Role of Acquaintance," in W. G. Lycan
  (ed.), *Mind and Cognition* (Blackwell, 1990).
