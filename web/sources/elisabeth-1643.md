relevance (content): the source of the **interaction problem** for substance dualism. In her 1643 letters,
Princess Elisabeth of Bohemia pressed Descartes on how an immaterial, unextended mind could move the body
at all, given that causation seemed to require contact and extension — the objection that has dogged
[dualism](../positions/dualism.md) ever since and that frames the *problem of mental causation* in the
[mind-body problem](../concepts/mind-body-problem.md). Descartes's replies (the union of mind and body as a
"primitive notion") are widely regarded as unsatisfying.

Reference:
- Shapiro, L. (Ed. & Trans.). (2007). *The correspondence between Princess Elisabeth of Bohemia and René Descartes*. University of Chicago Press. (Original letters 1643)
