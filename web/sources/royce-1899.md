relevance (illustration): the *self-representative map* — Royce's image of a perfectly exact map of
England drawn within England, which must therefore contain a map of itself, which must contain a map
of that map, and so on to infinity. It is the canonical picture of infinite self-nesting invoked for
the regress form of the finite self-model bound in [finite-self-model-incomplete](../arguments/finite-self-model-incomplete.md); Royce
introduces it to model self-consciousness as a system one of whose parts perfectly represents the
whole.

Reference:
- Royce, J. (1899). *The world and the individual, first series: The four historical conceptions of
  being*. Macmillan. (The self-representative map appears in the Supplementary Essay, "The One, the
  Many, and the Infinite.")
