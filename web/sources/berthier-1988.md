relevance (content): the clinical study that characterised **pain asymbolia** as a *sensory-limbic
disconnection syndrome*. Across six patients with lesions around the insula, the somatosensory
detection of noxious stimuli was preserved — patients could feel, localise, and describe the pain —
yet the normal aversive, emotional, and withdrawal responses were absent or inadequate, including to
threatening gestures. Grounds [pain-asymbolia](../concepts/pain-asymbolia.md) on the neurological side; the philosophical
framing is supplied by [grahek-2007](grahek-2007.md).

Reference:
- Berthier, M., Starkstein, S., & Leiguarda, R. (1988). Asymbolia for pain: A sensory-limbic
  disconnection syndrome. *Annals of Neurology*, *24*(1), 41–49.
  https://doi.org/10.1002/ana.410240109
