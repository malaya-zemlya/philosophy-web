relevance (content): Davidson's APA presidential address, the origin of the *Swampman* case — a
molecule-for-molecule duplicate of a person assembled by chance from a lightning-struck swamp, with
no causal or selection history. Davidson himself judges that Swampman lacks thoughts and reference,
biting the bullet his externalism invites; deployed by others, the case is the standard objection
to history-based theories of content. Grounds [swampman](../arguments/swampman.md).

Reference:
- Davidson, D. (1987). Knowing one's own mind. *Proceedings and Addresses of the American
  Philosophical Association*, *60*(3), 441–458.
