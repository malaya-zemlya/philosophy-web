relevance (content): the fullest statement of Roger Penrose's anti-mechanist view that human mathematical
understanding is *non-algorithmic* and that the brain must therefore exploit non-computable physical
processes (for Penrose, in an objective-collapse quantum gravity). It grounds
[consciousness-noncomputable](../claims/consciousness-noncomputable.md) and [noncomputable-consciousness](../positions/noncomputable-consciousness.md), and it is the position
located at the **D2** horn of [unwarrantable-zombie-premise](../arguments/unwarrantable-zombie-premise.md) — the cognitive
hypercomputationalist who could possess a consistency-certifying faculty, at the cost of denying the
physical Church–Turing thesis *of the reasoning faculty itself*.

Reference:
- Penrose, R. (1994). *Shadows of the mind: A search for the missing science of consciousness*. Oxford University Press.
