relevance (content): Thomas Reid's mature statement of the philosophy of *common sense* — that the
existence of the external world (along with other minds, the self, and the past) is a **first principle**,
not a conclusion reached by argument; the skeptic's reasonings cannot dislodge it, and it is the skeptic,
not the plain believer, who bears the burden. Grounds [commonsense-realism](../positions/commonsense-realism.md).

Reference:
- Reid, T. (1785). *Essays on the intellectual powers of man*. John Bell.
