relevance (content): Parfit's Part Three (Personal Identity) develops the *Physical*,
*Psychological*, and *Combined Spectrum* cases — connected series of small changes from one person
toward another — and argues that personal identity is not what matters and can be a matter of degree
or indeterminate. Grounds the personal-identity instance of the
[continuity-argument-schema](../concepts/continuity-argument-schema.md): [personal-identity-admits-of-degree](../claims/personal-identity-admits-of-degree.md) (the degree
thesis), [psychological-rewiring-tolerance](../claims/psychological-rewiring-tolerance.md) (the tolerance premise across a rewiring
spectrum), and [parfit-rewiring](../arguments/parfit-rewiring.md) (the argument that selects the degree horn). The
degree/indeterminacy theses are argued chiefly in chapters 10–13.

Reference:
- Parfit, D. (1984). *Reasons and persons*. Oxford University Press.
