relevance (content): the modern source of the **phenomenal-concept strategy** and, applied to Mary,
the *old-fact / new-guise* reply to the knowledge argument. Loar argues that phenomenal concepts are
first-person, recognitional concepts of inner states, cognitively isolated from the theoretical
concepts of the same states, so Mary acquires a new *concept* of an already-known physical fact rather
than a new fact. Grounds [old-fact-new-guise](../arguments/old-fact-new-guise.md) and its conclusion
[mary-gains-old-fact-new-guise](../claims/mary-gains-old-fact-new-guise.md), and underwrites the general phenomenal-concept machinery in
[gap-deflated-by-phenomenal-concepts](../arguments/gap-deflated-by-phenomenal-concepts.md).

Reference:
- Loar, B. (1990). Phenomenal states. *Philosophical Perspectives*, *4*, 81–108. (A revised "second
  version" appears in N. Block, O. Flanagan, & G. Güzeldere (Eds.), *The nature of consciousness:
  Philosophical debates*, pp. 597–616, MIT Press, 1997.)
