relevance (content): Dennett's case that intuitions about thought experiments must honour the case as
*stipulated* rather than the imaginable surrogate one actually pictures — the methodological point
behind the debunking of the Mary intuition in [mary-misimagined](../arguments/mary-misimagined.md), and behind the hard-line
reply that complete physical knowledge would leave Mary nothing to learn ([mary-learns-nothing-new](../claims/mary-learns-nothing-new.md)).
Distinct from [dennett-1988](dennett-1988.md) (*Quining Qualia*), which targets the thick notion of qualia, and
from [dennett-2005](dennett-2005.md), which develops the RoboMary case.

Reference:
- Dennett, D. C. (1991). *Consciousness explained*. Little, Brown and Company.
