relevance (content): empirical evidence bearing on the Chinese Room's axiom A3. Using *unsupervised*
Gromov–Wasserstein optimal transport — alignment that does not rely on any pre-given correspondence between
labels — the authors compare the colour-similarity structure of humans with that of GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 over
93 colours. GPT-4's structure aligns remarkably well with that of colour-neurotypical participants
(GPT-3.5 less so). Because the alignment is unsupervised, it is evidence that the *internal relational
structure* of the model's colour representations matches the human one, not merely that labels correlate.
Grounds [llm-text-yields-human-aligned-representations](../claims/llm-text-yields-human-aligned-representations.md) and the
[LLM-representations reply](../arguments/llm-representations-reply.md).

Reference:
- Kawakita, G., Zeleznikow-Johnston, A., Tsuchiya, N., & Oizumi, M. (2024). Gromov–Wasserstein unsupervised alignment reveals structural correspondences between the color similarity structures of humans and large language models. *Scientific Reports*, *14*, 15917. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-65604-1
