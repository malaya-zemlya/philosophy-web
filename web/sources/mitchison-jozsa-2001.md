relevance (content): the theory of *counterfactual computation* — protocols by which the outcome of
a quantum computation can be learned from runs in which, in the standard sense, the computer never
ran. Grounds the claim in [olympia-quantum-counterfactuals](../arguments/olympia-quantum-counterfactuals.md) that the line between an
*exercised* and an *unexercised* computation, on which Maudlin's Olympia argument leans, is not
physically fundamental.

Reference: Mitchison, G., & Jozsa, R. (2001). Counterfactual computation. *Proceedings of the Royal
Society of London. Series A: Mathematical, Physical and Engineering Sciences*, *457*(2009),
1175–1193. https://doi.org/10.1098/rspa.2000.0714
