relevance (content): grounds the form/meaning distinction behind the trained-mimicry rival —
that a system trained only on linguistic *form* (the distribution of tokens) has no access to
*meaning* (the relation of form to communicative intent and world), so mastery of form is a sign
that underdetermines the presence of the underlying states. Cited by
[trained-mimicry-rival](../arguments/trained-mimicry-rival.md) and its premise
[llm-mimicry-rival](../claims/llm-mimicry-rival.md), against
[behavioral-parity](../arguments/behavioral-parity.md). The paper's "octopus test" thought
experiment dramatises a learner with perfect form-statistics and no grounding.

Reference:
- Bender, E. M., & Koller, A. (2020). Climbing towards NLU: On meaning, form, and understanding
  in the age of data. In *Proceedings of the 58th Annual Meeting of the Association for
  Computational Linguistics* (pp. 5185–5198). Association for Computational Linguistics.
  https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/2020.acl-main.463
