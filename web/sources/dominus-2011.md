relevance (content): long-form reporting on the craniopagus twins Krista and Tatiana Hogan, joined at
the head by a "thalamic bridge." The twins appear to share some sensory experience across their two
bodies — one reportedly seeing through the other's eyes, or reacting to what the other tastes — a
putative real-world case of partial experiential sharing between two persons. A case datum for
[subjects-actually-merge-and-divide](../claims/subjects-actually-merge-and-divide.md). (Journalism, not a controlled study; cited as a reported
phenomenon, not established fact.)

Reference:
- Dominus, S. (2011, May 29). Could conjoined twins share a mind? *The New York Times Magazine*. https://www.nytimes.com/2011/05/29/magazine/could-conjoined-twins-share-a-mind.html
