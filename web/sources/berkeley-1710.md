relevance (content): the founding statement of subjective idealism — Berkeley's *immaterialism*,
on which to be is to be perceived (*esse est percipi*) and physical objects exist only as ideas
in perceiving minds. Grounds the Berkeleian variant of [idealism](../positions/idealism.md) and the claim
[reality-is-fundamentally-mental](../claims/reality-is-fundamentally-mental.md).

Reference:
- Berkeley, G. (1982). *A treatise concerning the principles of human knowledge* (K. P. Winkler,
  Ed.). Hackett. (Original work published 1710)
