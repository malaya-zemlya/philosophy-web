relevance (content): the classic statement of dual-aspect monism — one substance (God-or-Nature)
that we conceive under the *attribute* of thought as well as under the attribute of extension,
mind and matter as two expressions of a single reality. Grounds [dual-aspect-monism](../positions/dual-aspect-monism.md)
and [reality-two-aspects-mental-physical](../claims/reality-two-aspects-mental-physical.md).

Reference:
- Spinoza, B. de. (1994). *A Spinoza reader: The Ethics and other works* (E. Curley, Ed. &
  Trans.). Princeton University Press. (Original work published 1677)
