relevance (content): Earl Conee's *acquaintance hypothesis* reply to the knowledge argument. Conee
argues that knowledge by acquaintance is a third epistemic category, irreducible to knowledge of facts
or to know-how; before release Mary knew every fact about the experience of red but was not acquainted
with it, and on release she becomes acquainted — a change of relation, not a gain of information.
Primary source for [acquaintance-hypothesis](../arguments/acquaintance-hypothesis.md) and its conclusion
[mary-gains-acquaintance](../claims/mary-gains-acquaintance.md).

Reference:
- Conee, E. (1994). Phenomenal knowledge. *Australasian Journal of Philosophy*, *72*(2), 136–150.
  https://doi.org/10.1080/00048409412345971
