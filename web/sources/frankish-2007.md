relevance (content): grounds the anti-zombie parity objection — that an "anti-zombie" (a physical
duplicate whose physical nature necessitates consciousness) is as conceivable as a zombie, so
running conceivability-to-possibility on both yields contradictory modal conclusions unless one
intuition is privileged by assumption. Cited by [anti-zombie-parity](../arguments/anti-zombie-parity.md)
and its premise [anti-zombie-conceivable](../claims/anti-zombie-conceivable.md), against the bridge
premise of [zombie-conceivability](../arguments/zombie-conceivability.md).

Reference:
- Frankish, K. (2007). The anti-zombie argument. *The Philosophical Quarterly*, *57*(229),
  650–666. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-9213.2007.510.x
