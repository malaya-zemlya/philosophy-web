relevance (content): Paul and Patricia Churchland's reply to Searle, published alongside his
["Is the Brain's Mind a Computer Program?"](searle-1990.md). They grant that *classical, rule-based*
symbol manipulation may not suffice for mind but argue that Searle's third axiom is not self-evident, and
that a *connectionist* (massively parallel, brain-like) architecture is the live candidate for genuine
cognition — pressing the [connectionist/parallel reply](../arguments/connectionist-reply.md) against the Chinese
Room. Grounds that reply.

Reference:
- Churchland, P. M., & Churchland, P. S. (1990). Could a machine think? *Scientific American*, *262*(1), 32–37.
