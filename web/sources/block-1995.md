relevance (content): the paper that draws the distinction between *access consciousness* (a state's
content being available for reasoning, report, and the control of behaviour) and *phenomenal
consciousness* (there being something it is like to be in the state), and argues that conflating the
two underlies much confusion in the science of consciousness. The source for
[access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness](../concepts/access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness.md), cited in [consciousness](../concepts/consciousness.md).

Reference:
- Block, N. (1995). On a confusion about a function of consciousness. *Behavioral and Brain Sciences,
  18*(2), 227–247.
