relevance (content): Searle's APA Pacific Division Presidential Address, arguing that "syntax is not
intrinsic to physics" and that computational/functional individuation is observer-relative — there is
no fact of the matter, fixed by the physics alone, about which computation a system performs. Grounds
[functional-decomposition-relative](../claims/functional-decomposition-relative.md) and the observer-relativist denial cited in
[counterfactual-structure-objective](../claims/counterfactual-structure-objective.md). Distinct from [searle-1990](searle-1990.md) ("Is the Brain's
Mind a Computer Program?", *Scientific American*), which popularises the Chinese Room.

Reference:
- Searle, J. R. (1990). Is the brain a digital computer? *Proceedings and Addresses of the American
  Philosophical Association, 64*(3), 21–37. https://doi.org/10.2307/3130074
