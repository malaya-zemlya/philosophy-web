relevance (content): the dedicated statement of Chalmers's argument for the **Principle of Organizational
Invariance** — that any two systems with the same fine-grained functional organization have qualitatively
identical experiences — by way of the *fading qualia* and *dancing qualia* thought experiments. Grounds
[organizational-invariance](../claims/organizational-invariance.md), [fading-qualia](../arguments/fading-qualia.md), and [dancing-qualia](../arguments/dancing-qualia.md). The same
material appears as ch. 7 of *The Conscious Mind* ([chalmers-1996](chalmers-1996.md)). Distinct from
["Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness"](chalmers-1995.md), the *other* 1995 paper, which coined
"the hard problem."

Reference:
- Chalmers, D. J. (1995). Absent qualia, fading qualia, dancing qualia. In T. Metzinger (Ed.), *Conscious experience* (pp. 309–328). Imprint Academic.
