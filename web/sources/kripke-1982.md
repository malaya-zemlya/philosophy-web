relevance (content): Kripke's reconstruction of Wittgenstein's rule-following considerations as a
*skeptical paradox about meaning* — the "plus/quus" argument that no fact about a speaker constitutes
their meaning one thing rather than another — together with the "skeptical solution" that grounds meaning
in communal assertibility conditions rather than truth-conditional facts. The source for
[kripke-rule-following](../arguments/kripke-rule-following.md) and [no-constitutive-fact-of-meaning](../claims/no-constitutive-fact-of-meaning.md). The reading is Kripke's
own ("Kripke's Wittgenstein," or "Kripkenstein"), an interpretation of [wittgenstein-1953](wittgenstein-1953.md).

Reference:
- Kripke, S. A. (1982). *Wittgenstein on rules and private language: An elementary exposition*. Harvard
  University Press.
