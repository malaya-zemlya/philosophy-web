relevance (content): James's popularisation and extension of Peirce's pragmatic maxim — the doctrine
that the cash value of an idea is the practical difference its truth would make. Paired with
[peirce-1878](peirce-1878.md) as the "Peirce–James pragmatic maxim" invoked in
[unobservables-judged-by-coherence-and-usefulness](../claims/unobservables-judged-by-coherence-and-usefulness.md) and [unobservability-in-principle](../concepts/unobservability-in-principle.md).

Reference:
- James, W. (1907). *Pragmatism: A new name for some old ways of thinking*. Longmans, Green, and Co.
