relevance (content): the work that names and presses the **Münchhausen trilemma** — Hans Albert's
sharpening of the ancient Agrippan regress, on which every demand for the full justification of a belief
ends in infinite regress, logical circularity, or a dogmatic break-off. Albert, a critical rationalist in
Popper's line, uses it to reject *justificationism* itself. Grounds [munchhausen-trilemma](../concepts/munchhausen-trilemma.md),
[justification-terminates-in-trilemma](../claims/justification-terminates-in-trilemma.md), and [munchhausen-trilemma](../arguments/munchhausen-trilemma.md).

Reference:
- Albert, H. (1985). *Treatise on critical reason* (M. V. Rorty, Trans.). Princeton University Press. (Original work published 1968)
