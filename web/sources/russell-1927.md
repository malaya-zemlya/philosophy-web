relevance (content): the historical root of *Russellian monism*. Russell argues that physics gives us
only the abstract structural-causal pattern of the world and is silent on the intrinsic ("categorical")
nature of whatever realises that structure — leaving room for consciousness to be, or to ground, that
intrinsic nature. Grounds [russellian-monism](../positions/russellian-monism.md) and the claim
[functional-description-structural-dynamical-only](../claims/functional-description-structural-dynamical-only.md).

Reference:
- Russell, B. (1927). *The analysis of matter*. Kegan Paul, Trench, Trübner & Co.
