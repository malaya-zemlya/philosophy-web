relevance (content): the paper in which Searle recasts the Chinese Room as a formal **derivation from
axioms** — A1 (programs are syntactic), A2 (minds have semantics), A3 (syntax is neither constitutive of
nor sufficient for semantics) ⊢ C1 (programs are neither constitutive of nor sufficient for minds), with a
further axiom A4 (brains cause minds) yielding the causal-powers conclusions. Grounds
[chinese-room-derivation](../arguments/chinese-room-derivation.md) and its axiom claims, and canvasses the standard replies with Searle's
rejoinders. Appeared paired with the Churchlands' rebuttal ([churchland-1990](churchland-1990.md)).

Reference:
- Searle, J. R. (1990). Is the brain's mind a computer program? *Scientific American*, *262*(1), 26–31.
