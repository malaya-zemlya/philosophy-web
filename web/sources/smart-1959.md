relevance (content): the classic statement and defence of the type-identity theory of mind. Smart
argues that sensations are *contingently identical* to brain processes — the identity is empirical,
not analytic — and replies systematically to the standard objections (most famously the
"nation of philosophers" / Occam's-razor and the topic-neutral-translation replies). With
[place-1956](place-1956.md) it is the canonical source for [type-identity](../positions/type-identity.md) and
[mental-states-are-brain-state-types](../claims/mental-states-are-brain-state-types.md).

Reference:
- Smart, J. J. C. (1959). Sensations and brain processes. *The Philosophical Review, 68*(2),
  141–156. https://doi.org/10.2307/2182164
