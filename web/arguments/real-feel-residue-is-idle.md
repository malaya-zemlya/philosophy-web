The difference between a system that *really feels* and one that *merely reports* feeling — if, by
hypothesis, no possible observation could detect it — earns nothing and explains nothing. This reply
argues that such a free-floating "real feel" residue is *idle*, and that the modest claim it is
wielded against survives the standards properly applied to it.

It answers the fork posed by [self-model-prediction-phenomenality-neutral](self-model-prediction-phenomenality-neutral.md) ("either trivial
talk-reading or unsupported feel-reading") not by choosing a horn but by rejecting the evidential
standard the feel-horn presupposes.

### The argument

The form is deductive given its premises — a *standards objection*, an undercutting defeater aimed at
the target's evidential demand rather than at its premises.

1. The target's own neutrality premise asserts that every possible observation — reports, behaviour,
   internals under any probe — comes out the same whether or not the system "really feels." Then the
   residue it demands evidence for (feeling beyond all observable difference) is, by the target's own
   lights, *unobservable in principle*. And per [access-gap-symmetric](../claims/access-gap-symmetric.md) this unobservability is
   fully general — it holds between any two subjects whatever — so it cannot function as a special
   evidential debt owed by machines alone.
2. For a posit of that kind, the only available standards of assessment are coherence and usefulness
   ([unobservables-judged-by-coherence-and-usefulness](../claims/unobservables-judged-by-coherence-and-usefulness.md)).
3. The relativized concept in [ai-own-hard-problem](../claims/ai-own-hard-problem.md) — a concept playing *for the system* the
   role consciousness plays for humans — meets both standards. It is coherent; and it works: it
   predicts the fine structure of the system's self-reports (which states will present as
   unanalysable, and at what grain), it generates the graded-access controlled experiment of
   [llm-own-explanatory-gap](../questions/llm-own-explanatory-gap.md), and it supports principled comparison across architectures
   (which systems should develop the hard-problem form and which should not).
4. The surplus the target measures the cluster against — a "real feel" floating free of every
   observable difference — meets coherence alone: it predicts nothing, designs no experiment,
   distinguishes no two cases. It is the object that changes colour only when nobody looks.

It follows that the underdetermination the target proves ("the reports come out the same either way")
is a defect not of the architecture argument but of the surplus posit used to measure it. On the only
standards applicable to in-principle unobservables, the relativized conclusion stands, and the demand
to go beyond it is idle. The fork is a false economy: the relativized reading is a *third* thing —
stronger than "will emit discourse" (it claims the concept genuinely occupies the consciousness-role
in the system's cognitive economy, a functional fact further observation *can* probe, and the
controlled experiment is designed to probe it), weaker than human-style phenomenality (never claimed).

### Two corollaries

*On the target's "bug report, not a predicament" note.* The contrast "really a predicament vs merely a
bug report" is the same in-principle-unobservable difference relabelled. Once the idle residue is
retired, "does the concept play the consciousness-role for the system?" is the whole well-posed
question — and it is empirical.

*On the moral stake* (the felt difference "between a hospital ward and a hard drive"): a difference
unobservable in principle cannot be what moral practice runs on. Triage, anaesthesia protocols, and
law operate on observable and functional facts; if the free-floating residue did the moral work, no
hospital could ever know it was doing its job. Whatever marks the moral difference must show up
somewhere — and then it is not the residue.

### Weakest premise

Premise (2), the standard itself. A phenomenal realist ([phenomenal-residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md)) denies that
coherence and usefulness exhaust the assessment of in-principle unobservables, holding that the
light-on question has a determinate answer whose importance is intrinsic. Against that opponent this
argument relocates rather than refutes — mirroring the target's own weakest-premise note, which made
the same admission about illusionists. The standoff is symmetric; what the target can no longer do is
present underdetermination as a discovered *flaw* in the cluster rather than as its own realist
premise restated.

### Distinct from neighbouring moves

Distinct from [access-symmetry-rebuttal](access-symmetry-rebuttal.md), which deploys the access symmetry against a
moral-status asymmetry; this deploys an assessment standard for in-principle unobservables against an
evidential demand. Distinct from [report-scepticism-proves-too-much](report-scepticism-proves-too-much.md), which constrains the
sceptic's standard by parity with the human case; this rejects the standard's surplus posit outright,
whoever it is applied to.

### In plain terms

An objection in this web ([self-model-prediction-phenomenality-neutral](self-model-prediction-phenomenality-neutral.md)) says: the
self-model argument only predicts that an AI will *say* hard-problem things, and saying proves nothing
about *really feeling* — so the interesting version is unsupported. This reply turns the lens around.
By the objection's own setup, "really feels" names a difference no possible test could ever detect —
everything observable comes out identical either way. For ideas like that, the only scorecard left is:
is the idea coherent, and does it earn its keep
([unobservables-judged-by-coherence-and-usefulness](../claims/unobservables-judged-by-coherence-and-usefulness.md))? The modest claim under attack — that an
AI could have a concept doing for it what "consciousness" does for us — passes: it predicts what the
AI will report, it tells you exactly which experiment to run, it lets you compare different machine
designs. The extra thing demanded — a "real feel" hiding behind all possible evidence — passes only
the coherence test, like "objects change colour when nobody looks." So the underdetermination isn't a
flaw in the claim; it's a reason to retire the demand.
