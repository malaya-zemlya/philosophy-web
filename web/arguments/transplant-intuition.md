The **transplant case** is the canonical argument for psychological over biological criteria of
personal identity, introduced by Shoemaker ([1963](../sources/shoemaker-1963.md)).

**Inference form:** thought experiment, from a near-universal verdict.

1. **Scenario.** Brown's brain is removed and placed in Robinson's skull. The resulting person —
   call him "Brownson" — has Brown's memories, personality, plans, and sense of self, seated in
   Robinson's body. Brown's original body dies.
2. **Intuition.** Brownson is *Brown*. He recognizes Brown's wife, continues Brown's quarrels,
   feels Brown's remorse; to insist he is really Robinson (or a new man) because of the torso he
   wears strikes almost everyone as absurd.
3. **Bridge.** Brownson is not biologically continuous with Brown — Brown's organism died on the
   table; a brain is an organ, not an organism. So if Brownson is Brown, persistence is carried by
   the psychology the brain brings, not by the continuing life of an organism.
4. ∴ Personal identity tracks psychological continuity
   ([identity-is-psychological-continuity](../claims/identity-is-psychological-continuity.md)), and the biological criterion
   ([identity-is-biological-continuity](../claims/identity-is-biological-continuity.md)) gives the wrong verdict in a case it must count
   as decisive.

**Weakest premise:** the bridge (3) — thought-experiment CQ4. The [animalist](../positions/animalism.md)
grants the pull of the verdict but re-describes it: what goes with the brain is everything Brown
*cares about* — his psychology — and our intuitions track what matters, not identity
([identity-not-what-matters](../claims/identity-not-what-matters.md) turned to animalist use, per Olson,
[1997](../sources/olson-1997.md)). Brown himself, the animal, stays behind and dies. The verdict (2) is
also pressured as theory-laden — trained on fiction in which minds hop bodies. The neo-Lockean's
rejoinder: an error theory that convicts nearly every judge, in the clearest case the debate
possesses, costs more than the criterion it saves.

### In plain terms

Your brain — carrying all your memories, quirks, and plans — is moved into a different body, and
your old body dies. Nearly everyone says: *you* wake up in the new body; you didn't die, you moved.
But notice what that admission does: your old living organism is gone, yet you survived — so being
you can't be a matter of which organism keeps running
([identity-is-biological-continuity](../claims/identity-is-biological-continuity.md)); it must be a matter of where your mental life goes
([identity-is-psychological-continuity](../claims/identity-is-psychological-continuity.md)). The
["you are the animal" theorist](../positions/animalism.md) has one comeback: what moved was everything
you *care about*, and caring followed it — but strictly speaking, you stayed behind on the table.
