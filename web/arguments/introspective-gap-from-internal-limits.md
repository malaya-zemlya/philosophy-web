This argument explains how an *explanatory gap* — a system's sense that no account of its
machinery captures why its inner states appear as they do — could be a product of how the
system's self-access is built, not a sign that something is missing from the physical world. It
concludes that a system can be completely and correctly explained from the outside while
remaining unable, from the inside, to derive any explanation that would satisfy it.

### The inference

The form is an analogy that supplies the key premise, followed by a deductive composition of two
premises.

1. [A system's inside vantage is informationally poorer than
   an outside one](../claims/inside-vantage-access-limited.md): variables realised in its processing can be missing from the economy of
   states it can report on.
2. [An explanation satisfies a system only if
   the system can traverse it](../claims/explanation-must-be-internally-traversable.md): a bridge an external theorist can cross may be underivable from
   inside.

From these it follows that [a system can be
fully explicable from outside yet unable to derive a satisfying self-explanation from inside](../claims/self-explanation-gap-can-be-architectural.md) —
the gap arising from access architecture, not from a residue in the world.

### The analogy and what it borrows

The move models internal-versus-external explicability on *provability relative to a system*:
what is derivable depends on the resources available, so a truth transparent from a richer
standpoint can be underivable from a poorer one. The shared respects are (i) a fixed stock of
internal observables and operations, and (ii) derivability defined relative to that stock. The
analogy borrows only this structural point — not the contested conclusion of [Penrose](../sources/penrose-1994.md)
that minds outstrip machines.

### The weakest premise

Premise 2 is load-bearing and contestable. The risk is a slide from an *access* gap to a
*phenomenal* one. Showing that a system cannot traverse a self-explanation accounts for missing
intelligibility-from-inside, which on Block's [access/phenomenal
distinction](../concepts/access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness.md) need not touch why there is anything it is like to be the system at all — the
*ontological* member of the [varieties of introspective
gap](../concepts/introspective-gap-varieties.md). So the argument secures an architectural or conceptual gap; whether that exhausts the felt
gap is exactly what an opponent denies, which is why the conclusion is filed as contested. (The
[analogy pattern](../../patterns/analogy.md)'s *disanalogy* critical question presses here: an
opponent may argue that informal "intuitive explanation" disanalogises from formal provability in
just the respect that matters.)

### Relation to mysterianism

The conclusion is a mechanism-level cousin of McGinn's cognitive closure ([mysterianism](../positions/mysterianism.md),
[cognitive-closure-exists](../claims/cognitive-closure-exists.md), [mcginn-1989](../sources/mcginn-1989.md)): this argument is one way to *earn* a
closure-style verdict by construction rather than asserting it. It deliberately carries no
`attacks` or `supports` edge into the residue debate ([explanatory-gap](explanatory-gap.md),
[phenomenal-residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md)).

### In plain terms

Put two simple points together. First: a system often can't reach its own inner workings — the
facts are in it but not available to it (like not feeling your neurons fire). Second: an
explanation only *clicks* for a mind if that mind can actually walk the steps itself; a bridge an
outside expert can cross may have no inside path. Stack those and you get the conclusion: a system
can be completely explained from the outside yet, from the inside, never able to derive why its
own thoughts appear as they do — so the "gap" comes from how its self-access is built, not from
anything missing in the world. The shaky step is the second one: not being able to *reach* an
explanation isn't obviously the same as there being a leftover *feel* to explain — which is why
this is marked contested.
