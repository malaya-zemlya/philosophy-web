Raises thought-experiment **CQ-verdict** (and sharpens **CQ-begs-the-question**) against
[dancing-qualia](dancing-qualia.md): the "you would surely notice the flip" intuition that supplies the reductio's
absurdity is theory-laden, because the only thing that could *constitute* noticing — a cross-time comparison
whose earlier term is a memory of the prior quale — is a functionally fixed item, held constant by the very
construction.

### The objection

The dancing-qualia reductio needs two things of the subject at once: that their reports and judgements stay
fixed (so they say "nothing changed"), and that a real red→blue flip is the sort of thing they *could not*
fail to notice (so the silence is absurd). Examine what "noticing a change" requires. To notice that red has
become blue is to be in a state of *mismatch*: a representation "it was red" set against "it is blue now."
The "it was red" term is a **memory or expectation trace**. But in a functional isomorph that trace is fixed
by construction — and note it need not even be *replaced*: memory sits downstream of the switch, and because
the silicon circuit is a functional duplicate it feeds memory a signal functionally identical to the
biological one. So memory necessarily lays down "saw red" whatever the qualia did. The mismatch state that
would *be* the noticing is exactly the state the isomorphism forbids.

This forces a dilemma, and either horn dissolves the argument:

- **Noticing is functional** (memory, attention, the disposition to report) → it is held fixed → nothing
  registers the dance → and there is no absurdity, only a quale-flip that makes no difference to anyone. The
  "you'd surely notice" premise has no mechanism left to stand on.
- **Noticing is non-functional** — a direct acquaintance with the quale bypassing memory → then it *tracks*
  the quale, so when the quale flips the noticing flips too, the subject *does* notice, and there is no
  dancing-*without*-detection to be absurd about.

∴ The reductio's air of absurdity is generated by requiring a noticer that is *both* functionally fixed
*and* quale-sensitive — which is functionalism about introspective/memorial access asserted, not shown. This
`supports` [dancing-qualia-presupposes-functional-introspection](../claims/dancing-qualia-presupposes-functional-introspection.md) by exhibiting the cleanest case of
the smuggled premise: memory, which no one mistakes for a store of raw qualia.

### Chalmers' reply, and why it does not escape

Chalmers can deny that the relevant noticing is a memory comparison at all: a red→blue flip is *occurrently*
salient — a phenomenal jolt in the specious present that grabs you *now*, before memory is consulted — and
it is the failure of *that* immediate salience that is absurd. But the dilemma survives the move untouched:
occurrent salience is itself either functional (a disposition to attend, startle, report — held fixed, so no
jolt registers) or a non-functional acquaintance (which tracks the quale, so the subject notices). Memory is
merely the most obvious thing any noticing must lean on; the point is that *every* candidate noticer falls on
one side or the other of the functional / non-functional line, and Chalmers needs one that falls on neither.

### Connection to the paradox of phenomenal judgement

The objection is the [paradox of phenomenal judgement](../concepts/paradox-of-phenomenal-judgment.md) turned
against its intended remedy. On any view where qualia are not fixed by functional organization, a subject's
judgements and *memories* about their qualia are caused by the functional organization, not by the qualia —
so "I have always seen red" is guaranteed regardless of what the qualia did. Dancing qualia is Chalmers's
attempt to *escape* that paradox, by arguing qualia cannot float free. The memory point shows the escape is
circular: the only thing that makes the floating-free look *absurd* is the assumption that memory and
judgement must track qualia — which is the denial of floating-free, assumed rather than earned. The paradox
he means to avoid is doing the work meant to refute it.

### What it does and does not show

It does **not** show that dancing qualia are actual, that inverted qualia are possible, or that introspection
is non-functional. It shows only that the reductio's *absurdity* is not a neutral datum: it dissolves once
one declines functionalism about memory and introspective access — exactly the entitlement of a
[phenomenal-residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md) theorist. The conclusion (organizational invariance) may
still be true; this argument denies that *dancing qualia establishes it*.

Distinct from [dancing-qualia-begs-the-question](dancing-qualia-begs-the-question.md) / [dancing-qualia-presupposes-functional-introspection](../claims/dancing-qualia-presupposes-functional-introspection.md),
which diagnose the smuggled premise at the level of introspective *judgement* in general: this argument
routes specifically through **memory and cross-time comparison** — the mechanics of "noticing a change" — and
ties the circularity to the paradox of phenomenal judgement.

### In plain terms

Chalmers says: if the switch flipped your colours, you'd *surely* notice — yet by design you say nothing, and
that's absurd. But ask what "noticing" a colour-flip would take. You'd have to feel "it *was* red, now it's
blue" — and the "it was red" part is a *memory*. In a functional copy, memory is pinned: it sits past the
switch and gets the very same signal either way, so it always records "saw red," no matter what the colours
did. The one mental state that would count as catching the change is the one the setup rules out. So of
course you don't notice — not absurdly, but inevitably. Calling that absurd just assumes your memories must
follow your qualia, which is the very thing in dispute. (Chalmers can say the flip would jolt you on the
spot, before memory — but a "jolt" is either another functional reaction, also pinned, or a direct meeting
with the quale, which *would* let you notice. Neither gives him the oblivious-yet-jolted subject he needs.)
