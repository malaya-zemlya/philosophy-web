The [unwarrantable-premise objection](unwarrantable-zombie-premise.md) to the zombie
argument, carried across the [consciousness–free-will
duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md) to the output side — the one dictionary entry that transfers *machinery* rather than
analogy, since the transferred premises are the very same claims, not dualized counterparts.

The libertarian who offers [agent-causation](../concepts/agent-causation.md) as a genuine metaphysical
possibility on armchair grounds — "the picture is coherent, so it is at least possible" — is making
a conceivability argument, and every step of the certificate objection applies verbatim.

**Inference form:** deductive, governed by a dialectical constraint on cogency.

1. ([cogency-requires-accessible-noncircular-warrant](../claims/cogency-requires-accessible-noncircular-warrant.md)) An argument moves its opponent only
   if its premises admit accessible, non-question-begging warrant.
2. ([ideal-conceivability-is-apriori-consistency](../claims/ideal-conceivability-is-apriori-consistency.md)) The claim that agent-causation is
   ideally conceivable is the consistency claim: the complete causal facts conjoined with genuine
   agent-origination harbor no contradiction that any amount of rational reflection could uncover.
3. ([consistency-certification-is-oracle-hard](../claims/consistency-certification-is-oracle-hard.md)) Reliably certifying the consistency of a
   scenario that rich is at least as hard as the halting problem, hence beyond any effective
   faculty.
4. The same trilemma that meets the zombie-conceiver meets the agent-causal theorist: a computable
   reasoner can report only "no contradiction found so far" (the prima facie notion the possibility
   claim disavows); a non-computable certifying faculty either rests on contested empirical physics
   unavailable to an a priori argument, or presupposes a non-physical mind — importing a
   metaphysics at least as strong as the agent-causal conclusion itself.

∴ The ideal conceivability of libertarian agent-causation is a consistency claim no computable
reasoner can certify, and the a priori possibility argument for agent-causation is not cogent
([agent-causation-conceivability-uncertified](../claims/agent-causation-conceivability-uncertified.md)).

**Scope.** The transfer is specific to *conceivability* arguments. It does not extend to van
Inwagen's Consequence Argument ([van-inwagen-1983](../sources/van-inwagen-1983.md)), an inference-rule argument about the
transfer of powerlessness with entirely different failure modes; nor does it show agent-causation
impossible — it attacks the warrant, not the truth.

**Weakest premise:** as with its zombie-side twin, the warrant-trichotomy behind step 4 — the
modal rationalist will press for a fourth source of modal insight, fallible but positive, that is
neither exhaustive search nor question-begging model-construction. Whether such a faculty exists is
the same open crux on which [unwarrantable-zombie-premise](unwarrantable-zombie-premise.md) ultimately rests; the two
arguments stand or fall together, which is precisely what makes the pair the duality's strongest
evidence ([consciousness-freewill-duality](consciousness-freewill-duality.md)).

### In plain terms

On the consciousness side, there's a known objection to the famous zombie argument: nobody can
honestly certify that the zombie scenario hides no contradiction, because certifying "no hidden
contradiction anywhere" in a physics-rich scenario is provably beyond step-by-step reasoning
([unwarrantable-zombie-premise](unwarrantable-zombie-premise.md)). This argument points the same objection at free will:
the claim that an "uncaused chooser" is clearly coherent, hence possible, needs exactly the same
impossible certificate. Whatever the armchair can honestly deliver — "seems fine so far" — is too
weak to carry either argument.
