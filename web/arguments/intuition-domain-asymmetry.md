The *intuition-domain asymmetry* is a methodological argument about how much trust to place in two
competing intuitions about machine minds. It grants that our gut verdict "nobody is home" in a giant
behaviour-matching [lookup-table mind](../concepts/lookup-table-mind.md) should be discounted as an
unreliable extrapolation — and argues that this discount does *not* carry over to the intuition that
a brain-scale silicon duplicate would be conscious. The two cases sit at very different distances
from the everyday minds our intuitions were trained on, so distrusting the one need not poison the
other.

### The inference

The form is a defeasible *subsumption under a principle*: apply one principle about intuitive warrant
to two cases and read off a difference.

1. [An intuitive verdict's evidential weight decays](../claims/intuition-warrant-tracks-trained-domain.md)
   with the case's dissimilarity — in scale and structure — to the exemplars the intuition was formed
   on (actual brains and agents).
2. [The behaviour-matching lookup table](../claims/lookup-table-physically-unrealisable.md) is of cosmic
   scale (on the order of $10^{9600}$ rows) with no brain-like internal structure — maximally
   dissimilar to any paradigm mind on both axes.
3. A brain-scale silicon isomorph that preserves the brain's causal organisation (its
   [grain](../concepts/grain.md)) differs from a paradigm mind only in substrate — a near neighbour in both
   scale and structure.

∴ [the warrant-withdrawal that hits the "no mind"
intuition about the table does not hit the intuition about the isomorph](../claims/blockhead-deflation-targeted-not-global.md). Horn 2 of
[the blockhead dilemma](blockhead-dilemma.md) is therefore *targeted*, not global, and the
dilemma does not prove too much.

### Why this is not question-begging

The asymmetry is read off *distance from exemplars* — an epistemic fact about how intuitions are
formed — not off any prior ruling that organisation is what constitutes a mind. Premise (3) invokes
organisation only as a *similarity metric* to paradigm cases, a measure available to friend and foe
of functionalism alike; it does not assert that organisation suffices for mind.

### Weakest premise

The weakest point is Block's option of relocating the verdict from *scale* to *kind*
([block](../sources/block.md)). He may insist that the "no mind" verdict targets brute *storage as a kind* —
unreliable at any size — so that a scale-extrapolation reply misses it. The answer, now its own node,
is that [pure storage is not a stable kind at mind-matching scale](storage-not-stable-kind.md):
addressing and retrieving over $10^{9600}$ entries is itself a vast organised causal system, so the
cosmic "table" is not the same kind as the surveyable phone-book the intuition was trained on. That
reply does dialectical work beyond this argument's scale point, and whether it fully answers Block is
the live crux.

The argument is distinct from [the blockhead dilemma](blockhead-dilemma.md) it supports, and
from [the cosmic-size reply](lookup-table-physical-impossibility.md) that establishes premise
(2): it is concerned solely with the *epistemology of the intuition*, bounding the reach of horn 2.

### In plain terms

This defends a tricky move — distrusting our "nobody home" hunch about the giant lookup-table mind
because it is a wild imaginary case ([the blockhead dilemma](blockhead-dilemma.md)) — from a
"you can't have it both ways" complaint. The worry: if we throw out that hunch on the grounds that
gut feelings about bizarre imaginary cases aren't reliable, don't we then also have to throw out the
gut feeling on our *own* side, that a silicon copy of a brain would be conscious? The answer is no,
and here's the principle behind it: a hunch is trustworthy roughly to the degree the case resembles
the everyday examples that trained it. The cosmic lookup table is unimaginably huge and utterly
unlike any real brain — a wild extrapolation, so the hunch about it is weak. A brain-scale silicon
twin that copies the brain's actual wiring is a near-twin of the real thing — so the hunch about
*it* stays strong. Same principle, different distances, so distrusting the one needn't poison the
other. (And no cheating: "similarity" is measured by closeness to real minds, not by secretly
assuming up front that organization is what makes a mind.)
