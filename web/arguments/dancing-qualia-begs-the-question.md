The *dancing-* and *fading-qualia* arguments are a celebrated reductio, due to David Chalmers
([chalmers-1996](../sources/chalmers-1996.md)), against the view that conscious experience could come apart from
functional organisation. Imagine replacing a brain neuron by neuron with functionally identical
silicon parts. If the felt qualities (the *qualia*) drifted during the swap — red gradually coming to
look the way blue once did — while every functional disposition stayed constant, the subject would by
hypothesis go on judging and reporting that nothing had changed: a person radically deceived about
their own present experience. That seems absurd, so (the argument concludes) qualia cannot drift while
function holds; experience supervenes on functional organisation.

This entry argues that the reductio *begs the question*. Its apparent absurdity is manufactured by a
premise that already assumes functionalism about introspection — the very thesis in dispute. It is
aimed at Prong B (the supervenience reductio) of [gap-deflated-by-phenomenal-concepts](gap-deflated-by-phenomenal-concepts.md).

### The diagnosis

The form is twofold: a diagnosis of a question-begging premise, plus a demonstration that the
supposedly impossible world can in fact be described coherently (discharging the functionalist's
demand to tell the dancing-qualia story without incoherence).

1. Prong B's work is carried by the phrase *"introspective judgement is held fixed by construction."*
   That assumes introspective judgement is *itself* a purely functional item — that to judge "my
   experience just changed" is exhausted by occupying a transition-node, so that fixing the functional
   profile fixes the judgement.
2. But that is [the phenomenal residue thesis](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md) *denied*, applied to
   introspection and inserted as a neutral-looking setup condition — the diagnostic point recorded in
   [dancing-qualia-presupposes-functional-introspection](../claims/dancing-qualia-presupposes-functional-introspection.md). The reductio thereby assumes
   functionalism about introspection in order to derive a contradiction from the *denial* of
   functionalism about experience. It is the conclusion smuggled into the stage directions.

### Telling the world coherently

Distinguish the *phenomenal* fact from the *judgemental* fact, and take introspection to be an
*acquaintance* relation — a way a phenomenal state makes itself known — rather than a free-floating
functional tic ([functionalism](../concepts/functionalism.md)). Now run the replacement series; two cases exhaust the
options.

- *Acquaintance survives the duplication.* Then where the quale changes the judgement changes with it
  (the judgement was reading off the experience), so there are no self-deceived introspectors and the
  construction simply fails to deliver the advertised dissociation. At most it shows that genuine
  introspection is not fixed by the *coarse* transition-profile — a thesis the residue theorist gladly
  owns.
- *The duplication fixes the judgement-disposition by brute construction while the quale drifts.* Then
  you do get a dissociation, but only because you *stipulated a functional zombie-introspector* — a
  sub-system emitting "nothing has changed" with no acquaintance behind it. The "self-deception" is
  then the direct readout of having built an introspector that does not introspect, not an absurdity
  the residue theorist is forced to swallow.

The reductio's sting requires holding both at once: introspection functional enough to be fixed by the
construction, yet authoritative enough that its divergence from experience counts as *deception*
rather than mere malfunction. But a broken gauge is not a liar; an authoritative introspection is
acquaintance, and acquaintance is not what the series holds fixed.

It follows that the dancing-qualia world is not incoherent; its apparent incoherence was supplied
entirely by an assumed functionalism about introspection — the conclusion, not a neutral premise.
Prong B is therefore question-begging against [the phenomenal residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md) and
gives no conceivability-free positive support for supervenience.

### Weakest premise

The fork-management in the coherence demonstration. An opponent may insist on a *third* construal, on
which introspection is partly functional and partly acquaintance such that the dissociation is both
genuine and deceptive without equivocation. Producing a notion of "introspective judgement held fixed"
that does not already presuppose the residue false would be the move that retracts this thrust.

### Schema role

In the terms of [continuity-argument-schema](../concepts/continuity-argument-schema.md), the fading-qualia reductio tries to force the
*f-constant* horn for the carbon-to-silicon series (function held constant all the way down). This
thrust contests whether it has *earned* that horn: the "introspection held fixed" stipulation is what
*makes* the path look continuous, so the *discontinuity* and *disconnection* horns (some replacement
does flip qualia; biology is a non-negligible step) were assumed shut, not shown to be.

### In plain terms

This is a rebuttal to a clever argument on the other side (the "fading qualia" half of
[gap-deflated-by-phenomenal-concepts](gap-deflated-by-phenomenal-concepts.md)), so first that argument. It tries to prove that
feelings *must* be fixed by function. Picture slowly swapping someone's brain, piece by piece, for a
machine that behaves identically. If their inner feels secretly drifted along the way — red starting
to look the way blue used to — while every function stayed the same, they'd never notice or say a word
about it. They'd be bizarrely blind to a change in their own experience, which seems absurd. So, the
argument concludes, the feels *can't* drift: function locks feeling in place.

This node's reply: that argument quietly assumes that "noticing your own experience" is itself just
another function. But that's exactly what's in dispute. If noticing is instead a direct awareness *of*
the feel, then one of two things happens — either the noticing changes right along with the feel (so
there's no undetected switch, and the spooky scenario never gets off the ground), or you've
deliberately built a "noticer" that announces "all normal" with no real awareness behind it (a broken
gauge, not a genuine person being fooled). Either way, the apparent absurdity was slipped in through
the setup, not discovered. And a broken gauge isn't a liar.
