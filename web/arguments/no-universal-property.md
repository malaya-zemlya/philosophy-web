Categorical duality earns its transfer-power from *universal properties*: a theorem about limits in
$C$ becomes a theorem about colimits in $C^{op}$ because limit and colimit are defined by unique
factorization. The [strict duality thesis](../claims/consciousness-freewill-strict-duality.md) borrows
that authority without paying for it — no universal property has been located on either pole.

**Inference form:** deductive, from the transfer conditions of
[categorical duality](../concepts/categorical-duality.md) itself.

1. Theorem transfer across a duality fires only once a construction is located *with* its universal
   property: something must factor uniquely through conscious integration and co-uniquely through
   choice. Fan-in/fan-out is the *shape* of a universal property, not one.
2. No completed universal property for either pole is currently on the table. The partial payments
   fall short in instructive ways: the pullback reading of binding
   ([binding-is-obstruction-to-product](../claims/binding-is-obstruction-to-product.md)) *is* a universal property, but what is missing is
   the empirical claim that neural integration satisfies it; linear-logic polarity is
   theorem-bearing (focalization) but lives in linear, not cartesian, settings
   ([graded-polarity](graded-polarity.md)); active inference (Friston, [2010](../sources/friston-2010.md)) makes
   perception and action dual minimizations of one variational functional — the closest existing
   formalization of the whole thesis — but supplies an optimization symmetry, not a factorization.
3. The structure transferred must moreover be **non-temporal**: if the morphisms are just causal
   flow, "consciousness and free will are duals" reduces to "input and output run in opposite
   temporal directions," which is nearly definitional. Only argument-shape and universal property
   earn transfer, and only the former is in hand.

∴ Arrow-reversal between the domains yields a heuristic dictionary requiring per-entry
verification, not a transfer theorem ([duality-dictionary-not-theorem](../claims/duality-dictionary-not-theorem.md)) — and the strict
duality thesis, read as licensing more, is unsupported.

**Weakest premise:** step 1's implicit exclusivity — that nothing short of a full universal
property licenses any transfer. Weaker structure (an adjunction, a mere functor, the active-inference
functional) might license *partial* transfer, in which case the gap between dictionary and theorem
is a spectrum rather than a wall; the strict thesis would still overclaim, but by less.

### In plain terms

In mathematics, flipping the arrows gives you new truths for free only when the things flipped are
pinned down by an exact specification — a "universal property." The duality between consciousness
(many streams in) and free will (one action out) has the right *silhouette* but, so far, no such
specification on either side. So the flip generates good leads, not guaranteed results: every
translated claim must still be argued on its own merits. This is the main reason the framework's
own [mirror thesis](../claims/consciousness-freewill-mirror.md) is stated as a claim about matching
dialectics rather than a mathematical theorem.
