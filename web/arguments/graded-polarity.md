An objection to the two structural premises of the
[consciousness–free-will duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md): the assignment of pure
product structure to experience and pure coproduct structure to action idealizes both sides.

**Inference form:** deductive — counterexamples to each premise's universality.

1. Against [conscious-content-is-product-like](../claims/conscious-content-is-product-like.md): attention makes experience partially
   *exclusive* — attending to the conversation mutes the music; contents compete for the field
   rather than co-inhabiting it symmetrically.
2. Against [action-content-is-coproduct-like](../claims/action-content-is-coproduct-like.md): behavior is partially *conjunctive* — one
   walks while talking. The motor system takes products of compatible actions and coproducts only
   of incompatible ones.
3. So neither side instantiates its assigned structure purely; each mixes conjunction and
   exclusion.

∴ The crude ∏/∐ assignment is false as stated.

**The refined picture the objection points to.** Linear logic (Girard,
[1987](../sources/girard-1987.md)) splits conjunction and disjunction each in two — ⊗ (co-presence) and &
(external choice); ⊕ (internal choice) and ⅋ — and sorts the connectives by *polarity* into
negative (passive, input-like) and positive (active, committing). Game semantics runs on exactly
this division: the environment moves in negative phases, the player commits in positive ones. The
graded picture is thus not the duality's refutation but its sharpening — the thesis becomes close
to a restatement of polarization — with the caveat that the clean self-dual version lives in
linear, not cartesian, settings, so the upgrade has a real mathematical price
([no-universal-property](no-universal-property.md)). The duality also *predicts* where exclusivity enters
experience: exactly at the input-side selector, attention
([attention-is-dual-of-will](../claims/attention-is-dual-of-will.md)).

**Weakest premise:** the tacit step from "not purely" to "false as stated." The structural claims
concern the characteristic combinatorial shape of each side — conjunctive co-presence versus
exclusive commitment — and a defender can read them as claims about the dominant structure, with
attention and motor-compatibility as the graded fringe the polarity refinement absorbs. The
objection then demotes the premises from exact to approximate rather than refuting them.

### In plain terms

The tidy story says experience takes everything *together* while action picks exactly *one*. Real
life is messier: focus on one voice and the rest of the room fades (experience excludes), and you
can walk, talk, and chew gum at once (action combines). A more careful logic — one with two kinds
of "and" and two kinds of "or" — captures the mess: some combining is passive taking-in, some
choosing is active committing. The mirror between perceiving and acting survives, but in this finer
vocabulary rather than the tidy either/or.
