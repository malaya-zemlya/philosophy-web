The **confabulation argument** supports the representation view of introspection with an inductive
generalization from experimental psychology, canonically the studies surveyed by Nisbett and Wilson
([1977](../sources/nisbett-wilson-1977.md)).

**Inference form:** inductive (argument from example).

1. **Instances.** In study after study, subjects confidently report on their own mental processes
   and get them wrong. Shoppers asked to compare identical stockings prefer the rightmost pair and
   cite its (nonexistent) superior quality, never the position effect that actually drove the
   choice; subjects whose behaviour is demonstrably shaped by a planted cue deny the cue mattered
   and produce fluent alternative reasons; split-brain patients invent explanations for actions
   initiated by the hemisphere their speech centre cannot see.
2. **Representativeness.** These cases are not exotic breakdowns: they involve ordinary choices,
   judgments, and motives, made under ordinary conditions, and the effect survives across domains
   and decades of replication.
3. ∴ Introspective reports are, in general, generated the way the errors reveal: not by inner
   observation of the processes themselves, but by a rapid, unconscious *inference* from
   plausible-cause theories — a construction, not a reading
   ([introspection-is-fallible-representation](../claims/introspection-is-fallible-representation.md)).

**Weakest premise:** the *representativeness* step (pattern-example CQ2) — and it is exactly where
the acquaintance theorist pushes back. Nisbett and Wilson's cases concern access to mental
*processes* and *causes* (why one chose, what influenced a judgment); they show little or nothing
about access to current phenomenal *states* (the ache as felt now). If the datum theorist restricts
direct acquaintance to occurrent experience ([introspective-acquaintance-is-datum](../claims/introspective-acquaintance-is-datum.md)), the
inductive base may not reach the disputed class at all. The reply: the burden then falls on the
datum theorist to explain why a reporting system demonstrably confabulatory about everything else
becomes infallible precisely where checking is impossible.

### In plain terms

Psychologists have caught the inner witness lying — repeatedly. People pick the right-hand pair of
identical stockings and praise its finer weave; people nudged by a hidden cue swear the cue meant
nothing and offer polished reasons of their own. In each case the person isn't lying to us — they
are confidently reporting inner workings they simply cannot see, filling the gap with a plausible
story. If self-reports are manufactured that way when we *can* check them, the argument goes, why
trust them as direct readouts when we can't? The comeback: all these studies catch people wrong
about *why* they did things — not wrong about how a present pain feels, which is where defenders of
[direct acquaintance](../claims/introspective-acquaintance-is-datum.md) make their stand.
