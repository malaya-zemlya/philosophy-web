The *Chinese nation argument* (also the "China brain"), due to Ned Block ([block-1978](../sources/block-1978.md)),
is an *absent-qualia* objection to [functionalism](../concepts/functionalism.md): it describes a system
that realises a mind's full functional organisation yet, intuitively, has no felt experience, and
concludes that organisation alone is not sufficient for consciousness.

### The thought experiment

Suppose the people of a whole nation each simulate one neuron, signalling to one another — by
radio, say — on exactly the conditions under which neurons fire. The population then jointly
instantiates the same state-to-state organisation a brain does: not merely its outward behaviour
but its full internal causal structure ([chinese-nation-realises-organisation](../claims/chinese-nation-realises-organisation.md)). On
functionalism, which individuates mental states by causal role, this signalling nation should
have a mind, qualia and all. Yet it seems there is *nothing it is like* to be the nation — the
machinery runs, but no unified subject hovers above the crowd actually feeling anything
([chinese-nation-lacks-qualia](../claims/chinese-nation-lacks-qualia.md)).

### The inference

The form is a counterexample, an absent-qualia *intuition pump*: a single case that realises the
functional organisation while (intuitively) lacking phenomenal consciousness refutes "functional
organisation ⟹ phenomenal consciousness."

1. The nation realises a mind's full functional-causal organisation, not merely its behaviour
   ([chinese-nation-realises-organisation](../claims/chinese-nation-realises-organisation.md)).
2. Yet there is nothing it is like to be the nation ([chinese-nation-lacks-qualia](../claims/chinese-nation-lacks-qualia.md)).

Therefore realising the functional organisation is not sufficient for phenomenal consciousness.
This gives positive reason for [the phenomenal-residue thesis](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md) (role
under-determines the phenomenal) and is a counterexample to the uniform, phenomenal-covering
reading of [substrate independence](../claims/substrate-independence.md).

### Why it is sharper than the lookup table

The [lookup-table objection](lookup-table-objection.md) uses a system that lacks
organisation, and so threatens only behaviourism; an organisational functionalist excludes it
cheaply. The present case *grants* the organisation, so it cannot be dismissed that way. It bears
on functionalism proper — about *qualia* rather than *behaviour-as-evidence*. The two Block cases
attack different sufficiency claims and meet different replies.

### Weakest premise and replies

The *weakest premise* is premise 2 ([chinese-nation-lacks-qualia](../claims/chinese-nation-lacks-qualia.md)). It rests on an
absent-qualia intuition that the *systems reply* (Lycan, Dennett) rejects — holding that the nation
as a whole is a subject though no citizen is — and that a functionalist would deflate as a mere
artefact of our concepts ([deflation by phenomenal
concepts](gap-deflated-by-phenomenal-concepts.md)). That systems-reply rebuttal is the natural next move.

### Relation to neighbouring cases

Distinct from the [dense China-brain regress](dense-china-brain-regress.md), which takes the
case one level up — against the organisational-grain reply
([the grain reply](organisational-grain-dissolves-dilemma.md)) — to argue that the
[grain of role individuation](../concepts/grain.md) faces a liberal/chauvinist dilemma
([organisational-grain-faces-own-grain-dilemma](../claims/organisational-grain-faces-own-grain-dilemma.md)). The present node is the prior, classical
absent-qualia objection: organisation present, qualia absent, therefore organisation insufficient.
Distinct too from the [explanatory gap](explanatory-gap.md), which runs via conceivability
and zombies rather than a realised case.

### In plain terms

Suppose an entire nation of people, each passing signals by raising flags, together act out the
exact inner wiring of a single brain — not just its outward behavior, but every internal
cause-and-effect step a brain goes through. On the "it's the pattern that counts" view, that
flag-waving nation should then have a mind, feelings and all. But — the argument urges — surely
there's no extra *someone* hovering above the crowd actually *feeling* anything; the machinery
runs, yet nobody's home. If that's right, then running the right pattern of cause-and-effect isn't
enough to produce a felt experience. This is a sharper case than the cheat-sheet machine
([lookup-table mind](../concepts/lookup-table-mind.md)): the nation really does carry out the full inner
organization, so you can't wave it away by saying "it has no inner structure." The contested part
is the gut feeling that the nation feels nothing — some reply that the nation *as a whole* might
be a genuine subject even though no single citizen is.
