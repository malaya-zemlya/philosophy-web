A standing objection to any attempt to reduce the hard problem of consciousness
([hard-problem](../concepts/hard-problem.md)) to a problem about self-access. It grants that a system may be barred from
reaching its own inner workings, but denies that removing those barriers would remove the *felt* gap:
the two come apart.

### The inference

The form is an objection *by distinction* — in pattern terms it raises the *disanalogy* critical
question of the [analogy pattern](../../patterns/analogy.md) (CQ-disanalogy) against
[the internal-limits argument](introspective-gap-from-internal-limits.md).

1. Removing a system's access or architectural barriers to self-explanation need not remove the
   phenomenal explanatory gap; the two come apart ([closing-access-gap-leaves-phenomenal-open](../claims/closing-access-gap-leaves-phenomenal-open.md),
   turning on the distinction in [access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness](../concepts/access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness.md)).

Therefore an account showing only that a self-explanatory gap *can be* architectural
([self-explanation-gap-can-be-architectural](../claims/self-explanation-gap-can-be-architectural.md)) does not show that it *is* — still less that it
exhausts the felt gap. The architectural thesis secures the *architectural-access* and
*conceptual-translation* members of the [introspective-gap-varieties](../concepts/introspective-gap-varieties.md) while leaving the
*ontological* member untouched, which is the one the hard problem is about. So the thesis under-reaches
its target.

### What it does and does not establish

It does *not* assert a phenomenal residue or an ontological gap ([phenomenal-residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md)); it only
blocks the inference from "a gap can be architectural" to "the hard-problem-shaped gap is thereby
explained." The disanalogy it presses on the internal-limits argument: an informal, intuitive
explanation of *experience* may differ from formal provability precisely in that no amount of internal
access or derivation yields the phenomenal — the respect that matters.

### Weakest premise

Premise (1), denied by illusionists and strong functionalists for whom there is no phenomenal
explanandum beyond the access facts. On their view, closing the access gap leaves nothing further, so
the architectural thesis is not under-reaching but complete.

### In plain terms

This is the main objection to the "it's just an access problem" idea. Grant that a system can't reach
its own inner workings — fix that, give it perfect access to everything inside, and the original
puzzle could still sit there: "why is any of this accompanied by an inner feel at all?" That question
is about *feeling*, not *information access*, and the two are different things. So explaining why a
system can't *reach* an explanation isn't the same as explaining away the *felt* mystery — the
architectural story answers a real but different question, and leaves the hard problem standing. (The
reply, from the other side: maybe there's no extra "feel" to explain once all the access facts are in
— in which case there was nothing left over to begin with.)
