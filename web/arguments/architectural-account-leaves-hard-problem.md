inference form: objection by distinction (raises the [analogy pattern](../../patterns/analogy.md)'s *disanalogy* critical question, CQ-disanalogy, against
[introspective-gap-from-internal-limits](introspective-gap-from-internal-limits.md)).

1. ([closing-access-gap-leaves-phenomenal-open](../claims/closing-access-gap-leaves-phenomenal-open.md)) Removing a system's access/architectural
   barriers to self-explanation need not remove the phenomenal explanatory gap; the two come apart
   ([access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness](../concepts/access-vs-phenomenal-consciousness.md)).
∴ An account showing only that a self-explanatory gap *can be architectural*
   ([self-explanation-gap-can-be-architectural](../claims/self-explanation-gap-can-be-architectural.md)) does not show it *is* — still less that it
   exhausts the felt gap. The architectural thesis secures the *architectural-access* /
   *conceptual-translation* members of [introspective-gap-varieties](../concepts/introspective-gap-varieties.md) while leaving the
   *ontological* member untouched, which is the one the hard problem is about. So the thesis
   under-reaches its target.

what it does and does not establish: it does NOT assert a phenomenal residue or an ontological gap
([phenomenal-residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md)); it only blocks the inference from "a gap can be architectural" to "the
hard-problem-shaped gap is thereby explained." The disanalogy it presses on
[introspective-gap-from-internal-limits](introspective-gap-from-internal-limits.md): informal "intuitive explanation" of *experience*
may differ from formal provability precisely in that no amount of internal access/derivation yields
the phenomenal — the respect that matters.

weakest premise: (1), denied by illusionists/strong functionalists for whom there is no phenomenal
explanandum beyond the access facts — in which case closing the access gap leaves nothing further, and
the architectural thesis is not under-reaching but complete.

### In plain terms

This is the main objection to the "it's just an access problem" idea. Grant that a system can't reach
its own inner workings — fix that, give it perfect access to everything inside, and the original
puzzle could still sit there: "why is any of this accompanied by an inner feel at all?" That question
is about *feeling*, not *information access*, and the two are different things. So explaining why a
system can't *reach* an explanation isn't the same as explaining away the *felt* mystery — the
architectural story answers a real but different question, and leaves the hard problem standing. (The
reply, from the other side: maybe there's no extra "feel" to explain once all the access facts are in
— in which case there was nothing left over to begin with.)
