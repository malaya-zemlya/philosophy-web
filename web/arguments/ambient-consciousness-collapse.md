The deepest disanalogy pressed against the [strict
duality thesis](../claims/consciousness-freewill-strict-duality.md): the two poles are not symmetric, because one of them contains the other. The
phenomenology of agency — what it is like to choose — is itself consciousness; there is no
output-side analog of phenomenality unless "origination" plays that role, and origination is
exactly what output-side deflationists treat as confabulated (Wegner,
[2002](../sources/wegner-2002.md)). Consciousness is less one pole of the duality than the ambient medium
in which both poles appear.

**Inference form:** deductive — from an asymmetry the strict thesis cannot absorb.

1. A categorical duality relates two constructions standing in mirror-image relation within (or
   across) categories; neither side is a constituent of the other.
2. Conscious experience is not confined to the input pole: deliberating, choosing, and initiating
   are *experienced* — the will's operation is itself a conscious content.
3. The point has a concrete theoretical face: integrated information theory reads consciousness as
   integration, fan-*in* ([consciousness-is-integrated-information](../claims/consciousness-is-integrated-information.md)), while global
   workspace theory reads it as broadcast — availability to all consumer subsystems, fan-*out*
   ([consciousness-is-global-broadcast](../claims/consciousness-is-global-broadcast.md)). If consciousness is both sink and source, it
   already occupies both poles.
4. The clean opposition holds only of the perception→consciousness→will→action pipeline, which is
   itself a folk-psychological idealization: endogenous imagery and inner speech are consciousness
   without input; deliberation is will saturated with input.

∴ Consciousness and the will are not related as dual poles; the strict thesis mislocates
consciousness in its own diagram.

**Weakest premise:** premise 1's assumption that a duality requires disjoint relata. The
framework's finer reading already concedes the coarse pairing and relocates the duality at the
level of *operations* — attention dual to volition ([attention-is-dual-of-will](../claims/attention-is-dual-of-will.md)), both
occurring within the conscious medium — so the collapse refutes the coarse thesis while leaving the
refined one standing. The objection is decisive against "consciousness is the product-pole," not
against "input-side selection mirrors output-side selection."

### In plain terms

The proposal treats consciousness as the receiving end and the will as the sending end. But
choosing doesn't happen in the dark — you *experience* your own deciding, which means the
"receiving end" is present inside the "sending end" too. Even the leading theories of consciousness
disagree about its direction: one says consciousness is information flowing *together*, another
says it is information being *broadcast out*. If consciousness sits on both ends at once, it can't
be one end of a mirror — though the finer pairing, attention versus willing as two selectors
([attention-is-dual-of-will](../claims/attention-is-dual-of-will.md)), survives this objection.
