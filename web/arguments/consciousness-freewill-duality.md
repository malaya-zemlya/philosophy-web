The master argument of the [consciousness–free-will
duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md): the dictionary that arrow-reversal generates keeps landing on positions and problems
that already exist independently in the other literature, and the best explanation of that
regularity is that the two debates really are one dialectic mirror-imaged along the
perception–action axis.

**Inference form:** abductive — an argument from sign. Each independently rediscovered
correspondence is a *sign*; the mirror is the state of affairs the signs indicate.

1. Conscious content is product-like ([conscious-content-is-product-like](../claims/conscious-content-is-product-like.md)) and choice
   content is coproduct-like ([action-content-is-coproduct-like](../claims/action-content-is-coproduct-like.md)) — so the two domains
   occupy the poles of an exact mathematical duality
   ([categorical-duality](../concepts/categorical-duality.md)), refined at the level of maps by
   [attention-is-dual-of-will](../claims/attention-is-dual-of-will.md).
2. Applying arrow-reversal to standard consciousness arguments yields, without free parameters,
   moves the free-will literature already owns under its own names: exclusion becomes the
   disappearing agent ([exclusion-dualizes-to-disappearing-agent](../claims/exclusion-dualizes-to-disappearing-agent.md)); the zombie becomes the
   volitional zombie, deflationist reply and all
   ([zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie](../claims/zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie.md)); the unity of consciousness becomes the unity
   of agency ([unity-dualizes-to-unity-of-agency](../claims/unity-dualizes-to-unity-of-agency.md)); the combination problem becomes the
   composition of will ([combination-dualizes-to-composition-of-will](../claims/combination-dualizes-to-composition-of-will.md)).
3. In at least one case the dictionary transfers *machinery*, not analogy: the Π₁
   conceivability-certificate objection applies verbatim to libertarian agent-causation
   ([agent-causation-conceivability-uncertified](../claims/agent-causation-conceivability-uncertified.md), via
   [agent-causation-certificate](agent-causation-certificate.md)).
4. Independent rediscovery on this scale is unlikely if the mirroring tracked nothing; it is
   expected if the two debates share structure along the perception–action axis.

∴ The consciousness and free-will debates are one dialectic mirror-imaged along the
perception–action axis ([consciousness-freewill-mirror](../claims/consciousness-freewill-mirror.md)).

**Weakest premise:** step 4, the correlation premise of the sign pattern. The correspondences could
reflect not a mirror specific to perception and action but the *generic* shape of any
realist-versus-deflationist stalemate — every hard philosophical dispute splits into a further-fact
camp and a deflationary camp, so translated entries may land on existing positions merely because
the target literature, like all literatures, has both camps (the sign pattern's *alternatives*
critical question: the sign may indicate a more general state than the one inferred). The
framework's reply is the specificity of the entries — postdiction pairing with Libet, Block's
distinction pairing with Fischer and Ravizza's, and one verbatim transfer of formal machinery — and
its concession is recorded separately: even granting the conclusion, the duality yields a
dictionary requiring per-entry verification, not a theorem
([duality-dictionary-not-theorem](../claims/duality-dictionary-not-theorem.md)).

### In plain terms

Take a famous puzzle about consciousness, flip "input" to "output" and "receiving" to "initiating,"
and check the free-will literature: surprisingly often, the flipped puzzle is already there, with
its own name and its own history. Do this several times and it stops looking like coincidence —
the two debates seem to be the same argument running in opposite directions. That regularity is
this argument's whole case; the honest fine print is that each translated puzzle still has to be
checked by hand ([duality-dictionary-not-theorem](../claims/duality-dictionary-not-theorem.md)).
