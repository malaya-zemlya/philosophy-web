Consciousness is a real, higher-level *biological* feature caused by and realised in neurobiological
processes — irreducibly first-personal yet nothing "over and above" the brain's concrete causal powers
([consciousness-is-biological-feature](../claims/consciousness-is-biological-feature.md); Searle 1992). Because it is those *causal powers*, not
the abstract program, that produce mind, merely running the right syntax is insufficient
([syntax-insufficient-for-semantics](../claims/syntax-insufficient-for-semantics.md), the moral of [chinese-room](../concepts/chinese-room.md) /
[chinese-room](../arguments/chinese-room.md)). So it `rejects` strong-AI [substrate-independence](../claims/substrate-independence.md) and
[llm-has-mental-states](../claims/llm-has-mental-states.md), answering [substrate-independence](../questions/substrate-independence.md) and
[llm-moral-status](../questions/llm-moral-status.md) in the negative for any purely computational system.

what distinguishes it from neighbouring views: against [functionalism](../concepts/functionalism.md) it denies that the
right causal *organisation* suffices independent of the realiser's causal *powers*. Unlike
[proteocentrism](proteocentrism.md) the demand is not *carbon* as such but the relevant causal powers — though
Searle holds only brain-like systems are known to have them. Unlike [type-identity](type-identity.md) it is
*non-reductive*: consciousness is caused by neural processes without being identical to a neural type.

### In plain terms

Searle's view: consciousness is a biological phenomenon, produced by brains the way digestion is
produced by stomachs — perfectly physical, but the product of a living organ's specific causal powers,
not of any abstract recipe. So you can't get a mind just by running the right *program* on any old
hardware (his Chinese Room makes the point: shuffling symbols by rules gives you correct outputs with
zero understanding). The upshot for AI: a computer simulating a brain no more *has* a mind than a
computer simulating a storm gets you wet — you'd need a system with the right causal powers, not just
the right code.
