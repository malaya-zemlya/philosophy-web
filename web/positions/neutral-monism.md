The fundamental stuff of the world is *neutral* — neither mental nor physical — and mind and matter are
two different *arrangements* of these same neutral elements ([reality-fundamental-stuff-neutral](../claims/reality-fundamental-stuff-neutral.md);
William James's "pure experience", Mach, Russell). It answers [hard-problem](../questions/hard-problem.md) by deflating the
mind/matter divide to a difference in how neutral constituents are grouped, so neither is fundamental
and neither must be "got from" the other.

what distinguishes it from neighbouring views: it is the historical parent of
[russellian-monism](russellian-monism.md), but stays *neutral* where Russellian monism makes the intrinsic base
(proto)phenomenal. Unlike [dualism](dualism.md) there is one kind of fundamental stuff; unlike
physicalism and [idealism](idealism.md) that stuff is neither physical nor mental.

### In plain terms

Neutral monism says mind and matter aren't two ultimate kinds of thing, nor is one built from the
other — instead both are *patterns* in a single, more basic stuff that is in itself neither mental nor
physical. Think of the same dots forming, under one grouping, a "mental" picture and, under another, a
"physical" one. So the hard problem's "how do you get mind from matter (or matter from mind)?" is a
trick question: you get both from something underneath that is neither.
