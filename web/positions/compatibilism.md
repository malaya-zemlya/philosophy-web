*Compatibilism* is the view that free will and determinism can both be true: an action is free when
it comes about in the right way — flowing from the agent's own deliberation, values, and
responsiveness to reasons, without compulsion, coercion, or manipulation — regardless of whether
the laws of nature and the past fixed that it would occur
([freedom-compatible-with-determinism](../claims/freedom-compatible-with-determinism.md)). The freedom worth wanting, on this view, is a
matter of *how you act*, not of whether the universe left the future open.

The tradition runs from Hobbes and Hume, who located freedom in the absence of external impediment,
to contemporary actual-sequence accounts on which what matters is *guidance control* — ownership of
a reasons-responsive mechanism — rather than *regulative control*, the power to do otherwise
(Fischer & Ravizza, [1998](../sources/fischer-ravizza-1998.md)). [Frankfurt
cases](../concepts/frankfurt-cases.md) supply the classic pressure toward that reorientation: agents seem fully responsible even
when, unbeknownst to them, no alternative was open. What distinguishes compatibilism from its
neighbors is what it denies: against [libertarianism](libertarianism.md) it denies that
freedom needs indeterministic openness or [agent causation](../concepts/agent-causation.md); against
[hard determinism](hard-determinism.md) it denies that determinism would unseat freedom in
the first place. Within the [consciousness–free-will
duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md), compatibilism occupies the deflationary pole of the free-will debate — the mirror image
of deflationism about phenomenal consciousness
([deflationism-entails-compatibilism](../claims/deflationism-entails-compatibilism.md)).

### In plain terms

Suppose a complete physics of your brain could have predicted your choice of career. Does that mean
you never really chose? Compatibilists say no: what makes a choice yours is that *you* weighed the
reasons and nothing forced your hand — not that the outcome was cosmically unfixed. Freedom is
about acting from your own mind, not about escaping cause and effect.
