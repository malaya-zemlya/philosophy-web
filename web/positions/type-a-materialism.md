**Type-A materialism** is the most deflationary wing of physicalism: it denies that there is any
*epistemic gap* between the physical truths and the truths about consciousness. Once you have explained
all the functions — the discrimination, the reportability, the control of behaviour, the access of
information to reasoning — there is, on this view, simply nothing further left to explain. The felt,
"what-it's-like" residue that the [hard-problem](../concepts/hard-problem.md) points at is not a hard remainder but a
non-existent one, or at most an artefact of how we introspect. Accordingly a type-A theorist holds that
a [philosophical-zombie](../concepts/philosophical-zombie.md) is not really conceivable on reflection ([zombie-conceivable](../claims/zombie-conceivable.md) is
denied) and that Mary ([knowledge-argument](../concepts/knowledge-argument.md)), leaving her black-and-white room, learns no new
*fact* — at most a new ability ([mary-gains-abilities-not-facts](../claims/mary-gains-abilities-not-facts.md)).
It commits to the core physicalist thesis that the mental just is the physical ([mental-is-physical](../claims/mental-is-physical.md))
and rejects any leftover phenomenal fact beyond the functional story ([phenomenal-residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md)). It is
**type-A** in Chalmers' taxonomy ([materialism-types](../concepts/materialism-types.md); [chalmers-2003](../sources/chalmers-2003.md)).

**What distinguishes it from neighbouring views.** Type-A is a *family*, not a single doctrine: analytic
functionalism, logical behaviourism, [eliminative-materialism](eliminative-materialism.md), and [illusionism](illusionism.md) are
all type-A, because each in its own way refuses to grant the explanatory gap any genuine purchase. What
unites them and sets them apart from the rest of physicalism is the denial that the gap is even real. This
is exactly the point on which it splits from [type-b-materialism](type-b-materialism.md), which *concedes* that the gap
is real (the zombie is conceivable, Mary's surprise genuine) and tries instead to show it is merely
epistemic; and from [type-c-materialism](type-c-materialism.md), which concedes the gap looks unbridgeable for now but
bets it will close. Against all of them stand the realist anti-physicalisms ([dualism](dualism.md),
[russellian-monism](russellian-monism.md)) that take the gap to mark a real division in nature.

### In plain terms

The hard-nosed "there's nothing to explain" camp. When people insist that even after brain science is
finished there's still a left-over mystery — the *feel* of pain, the *redness* of red — a type-A
materialist answers: no, there isn't. Explain everything the brain *does* and the job is done; the
sense that something extra has been skipped is itself just a trick of how we look inward. So on this view
a perfect zombie (physically just like you but with the lights off inside) isn't really imaginable once
you think it through, and the famous colour-scientist Mary learns a new *skill*, not a new *fact*, when
she first sees red. It is the most demanding line a physicalist can take, because it asks you to give up
the very intuition the other camps try to keep. Contrast the [type-b-materialism](type-b-materialism.md) camp, which
admits the puzzle feels real and then explains it away.
