*Libertarianism* (no relation to the political doctrine) holds that free will exists and is
incompatible with determinism — so determinism is false, at least where free agents act. When you
choose freely, more than one future is genuinely open ([alternatives-genuinely-open](../claims/alternatives-genuinely-open.md)), and
you are the difference: not the laws, not the past, not chance, but the agent settles which
alternative becomes actual.

The position's burden is to say how an undetermined decision can still be the agent's *doing*
rather than a lucky accident — the *luck objection* that shadows every version. Event-causal
libertarians answer with indeterministic causation among the agent's own states; agent-causal
libertarians (Chisholm, [1964](../sources/chisholm-1964.md)) posit [agent
causation](../concepts/agent-causation.md), the agent as substance originating action without being caused to; non-causal versions
drop the causal picture altogether. The Consequence Argument (van Inwagen,
[1983](../sources/van-inwagen-1983.md)) supplies the position's negative half — that determinism excludes
the openness freedom needs — against [compatibilism](compatibilism.md); the
[disappearing-agent objection](../arguments/disappearing-agent.md) (Pereboom,
[2014](../sources/pereboom-2014.md)) is the sharpest current attack on its positive
half. Within the [consciousness–free-will duality](../concepts/consciousness-freewill-duality.md),
libertarianism occupies the further-fact pole of the free-will debate — the mirror image of
phenomenal realism about consciousness ([zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie](../claims/zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie.md)) — and its
reliance on the armchair coherence of agent-causation is challenged by
[agent-causation-certificate](../arguments/agent-causation-certificate.md).

### In plain terms

Libertarians about free will think that when you stood at a real fork in life, both roads were
truly open — the universe itself hadn't settled it — and *you* did the settling. The hard part is
explaining how "not settled by anything prior" differs from "random": if nothing made you choose
left over right, was the choice yours or a coin flip's? Every version of the view is an attempt to
answer that question.
