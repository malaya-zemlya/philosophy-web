*Teleosemantics* is the naturalist programme that explains mental content by appeal to biological
*teleology*: an inner state represents what the mechanisms producing and consuming it were selected
to track ([intentional-content-from-biological-function](../claims/intentional-content-from-biological-function.md)). Its classic statements are Ruth
Millikan's theory of proper functions ([1984](../sources/millikan-1984.md)) and Fred Dretske's
indicator-function account ([1995](../sources/dretske-1995.md)). The view answers the
[problem of intentionality](../questions/intentionality.md) head-on: original aboutness
([intrinsic-derived-intentionality](../concepts/intrinsic-derived-intentionality.md)) is not primitive, not conferred by interpreters, and
not a mystery — it is what you get when evolution builds detectors and hooks their outputs to
behaviour-guiding consumers.

### What distinguishes it

Against simple causal or informational theories, teleosemantics has a principled account of
*misrepresentation*: since a mechanism can malfunction, or operate off its selected conditions,
what a state *means* (its function-fixed content) can come apart from what merely *triggers* it —
the frog's fly-detector still means *fly* when it fires at a passing bead
([teleosemantics-explains-misrepresentation](../arguments/teleosemantics-explains-misrepresentation.md)). Against
[interpretivism](intentional-stance.md), content here is fully observer-independent: there
were facts about what bee dances represented before anyone theorized about bees. The standing
objections are the [Swampman case](../arguments/swampman.md) — an atom-for-atom duplicate with no
selection history, hence, on this view, no content — and indeterminacy worries about *which*
function selection actually conferred (does the frog's detector mean *fly*, or *small dark moving
thing*?).

### In plain terms

This is the "meaning comes from evolution" theory of how thoughts get to be about things. A heart
is *for* pumping blood because pumping is what hearts were kept around for; likewise, says this
view, a brain state is *about* danger because warning of danger is what that kind of state was kept
around for. No inner ghost reads the brain's symbols — their meaning just is their biological job.
The famous stress test: imagine lightning assembling, by pure chance, a perfect atom-for-atom copy
of you in a swamp ([swampman](../arguments/swampman.md)). It would talk and act just like you, but its states have
no evolutionary job — so this theory must say they mean nothing, which many find hard to swallow.
