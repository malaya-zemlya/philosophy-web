The *consciousness–free-will duality* is a proposed structural mirror between two of philosophy's
hardest problem-families. Consciousness is **input-facing**: it integrates many perceptual streams,
the apparent final destination of perception. Free will is **output-facing**: it selects among many
available actions, the apparent first source of action. Reversing the direction of the arrows — the
basic operation of [categorical duality](categorical-duality.md) — appears to map one domain
onto the other, and if the mapping is sound, concepts, paradoxes, and whole arguments should
transfer between the two literatures. The framework's claim is not that the transfer is automatic,
but that arrow-reversal is a systematic *generator* of candidate correspondences, a striking number
of which turn out to already exist in the other literature under different names.

### What the arrows are

Talk of arrow-reversal is only as precise as the arrows, so the diagrams behind the dictionary
should be fixed once. The *objects* are states: world-states, sensory registrations, conscious
experiences, deliberative states, motor commands, actions. An *arrow* $A \to B$ is a directed
dependence — $A$ feeds, informs, or causally settles $B$. Perception is a chain of arrows running
world → sense channels → experience; agency is a chain running deliberation → motor command →
world. A philosophical thesis is then read as a claim about the *shape* of this diagram:
[epiphenomenalism](../positions/epiphenomenalism.md) says the conscious state is a node with only
incoming arrows; libertarian origination demands a node with only outgoing ones; integration says
many arrows converge on one apex; choice says one apex commits to exactly one of many candidate
arrows out.

"X is the arrow-reversed image of Y" is accordingly a three-step recipe. Draw Y as a diagram of
dependence-arrows. Reverse every arrow, so that *receives-from* becomes *issues-to*, sinks become
sources, and convergence becomes branching. Then relabel every input-side item as its output-side
counterpart — sensory stream ↔ action option, integration ↔ commitment, attention ↔ volition,
"what arriving is like" ↔ "originating". Read the resulting diagram back as a thesis: that thesis
is X.

Two cautions govern the recipe. Read as bare causal flow it is nearly trivial — inputs and outputs
run in opposite temporal directions by definition — so an entry earns its place only when what the
reversal preserves is combinatorial structure (which product/coproduct role a state plays) or
argument-shape (which premise deletes which arrow), never mere temporal direction
([no-universal-property](../arguments/no-universal-property.md)). And these dependence-arrows must not be conflated with the
*structural* arrows of a product or coproduct (projections, injections) — the sign problem
dissolved below turns exactly on keeping the two kinds apart.

### Two axes, and which one carries the substance

The informal statement runs together two different dualities. The first axis is
**initial/terminal**: "final destination of perception" makes consciousness a terminal object (a
unique arrow into it from everywhere), and "first source of action" makes the will an initial
object. This is the strictest reading and the emptiest: uniqueness of routing is flatly false of
consciousness (most of the organism's states never reach it), and terminal objects carry no
internal structure, so nothing about the *content* of experience or choice is captured.

The second axis is **product/coproduct**, and it carries the substance
([conscious-content-is-product-like](../claims/conscious-content-is-product-like.md), [action-content-is-coproduct-like](../claims/action-content-is-coproduct-like.md)). Conscious
content behaves like a *product* of qualia channels: an element of a product realizes all of its
components simultaneously, and experience is likewise conjunctive — visual, auditory, and
proprioceptive channels co-present in a single moment. The content of a free choice behaves like a
*coproduct* of actions: an element of a coproduct realizes exactly one summand, and incompatible
motor programs cannot co-execute. Co-presence versus commitment.

An apparent sign problem dissolves on inspection. Drawn naively, "many streams in" makes
consciousness the apex of a *cocone* (coproduct-shaped) and "many options out" makes the will the
apex of a *cone* (product-shaped) — the reverse assignment. But this conflates structural arrows
with mediating maps. In the product reading, the incoming streams are not arrows into the apex;
they are the components of a single mediating map from the world-state into the product — the
pairing $\langle q_1, q_2, \ldots\rangle$ — while the structural arrows (projections) point
outward. Dually, the action options are not arrows out of the will; the choice is one mediating
injection in.

The duality then splits into two exact pairs at the level of maps. The **combination pair**:
pairing into a product (integration of streams) is dual to copairing out of a coproduct (a standing
case-analysis over possible actions — behavioral competence rather than choice itself). The
**selection pair**: projection out of a product (selecting one stream among co-present ones) is
dual to injection into a coproduct (committing to one action among alternatives). On this reading
the dual of the will is not consciousness as a whole but **attention**
([attention-is-dual-of-will](../claims/attention-is-dual-of-will.md)): attention is input-side selection, volition is output-side
selection.

### The dictionary

Applying arrow-reversal to standard arguments yields a dictionary; entries that land on
independently existing positions are the framework's evidence
([consciousness-freewill-duality](../arguments/consciousness-freewill-duality.md)).

- **Exclusion ↔ disappearing agent.** Kim's [causal-exclusion argument](../arguments/causal-exclusion.md)
  makes consciousness all sink and no source; its dual is Pereboom's
  [disappearing-agent objection](../arguments/disappearing-agent.md) and the luck objection generally
  ([exclusion-dualizes-to-disappearing-agent](../claims/exclusion-dualizes-to-disappearing-agent.md)).
- **Zombies ↔ volitional zombies.** The [philosophical zombie](philosophical-zombie.md)
  dualizes to a behaviorally identical system with no real choosing; the compatibilist reply is
  structurally the deflationist reply to zombies
  ([zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie](../claims/zombie-dualizes-to-volitional-zombie.md)). By parity, deflationism about phenomenality
  pairs with [compatibilism](../positions/compatibilism.md)
  ([deflationism-entails-compatibilism](../claims/deflationism-entails-compatibilism.md)); [illusionism](../positions/illusionism.md) pairs with
  Wegner's illusion of conscious will.
- **Conceivability certificates.** The Π₁ objection to the zombie argument's conceivability premise
  transfers verbatim to libertarian agent-causation
  ([agent-causation-certificate](../arguments/agent-causation-certificate.md)) — the one entry that transfers machinery rather than
  analogy.
- **Unity ↔ unity.** The unity of consciousness dualizes to the unity of agency — binding failures
  mirror akrasia and the divided will ([unity-dualizes-to-unity-of-agency](../claims/unity-dualizes-to-unity-of-agency.md)).
- **Combination ↔ composition.** The [combination problem](../arguments/combination-problem.md) for
  panpsychism dualizes to the composition of will
  ([combination-dualizes-to-composition-of-will](../claims/combination-dualizes-to-composition-of-will.md)).
- **Frankfurt ↔ ganzfeld.** Frankfurt cases (freedom survives deletion of alternatives) dualize to
  the ganzfeld objection (does consciousness survive deletion of differentiation?) — an apparently
  novel prediction ([frankfurt-dualizes-to-ganzfeld](../claims/frankfurt-dualizes-to-ganzfeld.md)).

### Status and limits

Free-will problems do not live in the opposite category — they live in the same time-forward causal
world as the consciousness problems — so arrow-reversal supplies a dictionary, not a transfer
theorem; each entry must be verified by hand ([duality-dictionary-not-theorem](../claims/duality-dictionary-not-theorem.md),
[no-universal-property](../arguments/no-universal-property.md)). The main disanalogies pressed against the strict reading
([consciousness-freewill-strict-duality](../claims/consciousness-freewill-strict-duality.md)) are the actual/possible seam
([actual-possible-seam](../arguments/actual-possible-seam.md)), the collapse of agency-phenomenology back into consciousness
([ambient-consciousness-collapse](../arguments/ambient-consciousness-collapse.md)), and the graded structure of both sides
([graded-polarity](../arguments/graded-polarity.md)). A further meta-claim holds that every genuine disanalogy traces to
the thermodynamic arrow of time ([disanalogies-trace-to-entropy](../claims/disanalogies-trace-to-entropy.md)). The sharpest defensible
version is therefore not "consciousness and free will are category-theoretic duals" but: the two
debates are the same dialectic mirror-imaged along the perception–action axis, and the categorical
framing is the scaffolding that generates the transferable moves
([consciousness-freewill-mirror](../claims/consciousness-freewill-mirror.md)).

### In plain terms

Perception is many-streams-in: sights, sounds, and body sense arrive together in one experienced
moment. Action is one-choice-out: of the many things you could do next, you do exactly one. This
entry describes a proposal that these two shapes are mathematical mirror images, so that famous
puzzles about consciousness turn into famous puzzles about free will when you "flip the arrows" —
and, surprisingly often, the flipped puzzle already exists in the other literature under its own
name. The mirror is a lead-generator, not a proof machine: each translated puzzle has to be checked
on its own merits.
