primary definition: a taxonomy distinguishing four things that can be meant when a self-modelling
system "cannot explain its own experience to itself," kept apart so that a felt explanatory gap is
not automatically read as the metaphysical hard problem. The four are mutually compatible — a given
case may exhibit several at once — but they have different sources and different remedies.

senses:
- *ontological gap* — even given a complete physical/computational account, why is there experience
  at all? This is the hard problem proper ([hard-problem](hard-problem.md)); the anti-physicalist reading
  treats the residue as lying in the world ([phenomenal-residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md), [explanatory-gap](../arguments/explanatory-gap.md)).
- *architectural-access gap* — the system cannot reach the internal variables that *would* explain
  it: facts realised in its own processing are not available to its self-report economy
  ([inside-vantage-access-limited](../claims/inside-vantage-access-limited.md)). A gap in the *self*, not in the world.
- *conceptual-translation gap* — the system possesses both a mechanistic and an introspective
  description but has no internal route from one vocabulary to the other; the bridge that exists for
  an outside theorist is not traversable from inside ([explanation-must-be-internally-traversable](../claims/explanation-must-be-internally-traversable.md)).
- *training-contamination gap* — the system merely reproduces human hard-problem discourse it was
  trained on, with no underlying gap of its own. The confound an experiment must control for.

the dispute it feeds: it structures [llm-own-explanatory-gap](../questions/llm-own-explanatory-gap.md) (which kind, if any, an
introspecting LLM would exhibit) and lets the architectural/conceptual readings be stated as a
*mechanism* for McGinn-style cognitive closure ([self-explanation-gap-can-be-architectural](../claims/self-explanation-gap-can-be-architectural.md),
[cognitive-closure-exists](../claims/cognitive-closure-exists.md)) without asserting an ontological residue.

### In plain terms

When something can't "explain its own experience to itself," that can mean four very different things,
and they get muddled together. (1) **Ontological:** even after you know everything about the
machinery, why is there a felt experience at all? — the genuine hard problem. (2) **Architectural-
access:** the facts that would explain it are inside the system but it can't read them (like not being
able to feel your own neurons fire). (3) **Conceptual-translation:** it has the mechanical story *and*
the first-person story but no inner bridge between them — a translation an outside scientist can make
but the system itself can't. (4) **Contamination:** it's just parroting things humans have written
about the hard problem. Most of the interest is in telling (2) and (3) apart from (1) and (4).
