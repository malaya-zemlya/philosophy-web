*Grain* is the level of resolution at which a functional or causal role is specified: how much
detail two systems must share before they count as realising the *same* role, and so — on
functionalism — as having the same mental state. The metaphor is photographic. A coarse-grained
description draws few distinctions and lumps many systems together; a fine-grained one draws many
and separates them. Because [functionalism](functionalism.md) individuates mental states by
role yet leaves the grain of "role" unspecified, the grain is the free parameter on which most
substrate-independence verdicts actually turn: fix the grain and you have decided who counts as
having a mind.

### Settings along the dial

The dial runs from coarse to fine.

- *Coarse / input–output / behavioural.* The role is just the system's input–output profile, its
  Turing-test-passing behaviour. This is the most permissive setting, satisfied by a mere
  behavioural duplicate, which is why it is charged with being "too liberal": it would admit a
  [lookup-table mind](lookup-table-mind.md), a system that produces the right outputs from a
  giant stored table. This is horn 1 of the [grain dilemma](../arguments/role-grain-dilemma.md).
- *Organisational / functional-causal.* The role is the system's internal state-to-state
  transition structure — which states cause which, under which counterfactuals — abstracted from
  the physical medium. This is the proposed principled middle
  ([organisational role-grain](../claims/role-grain-is-organisational.md)): coarse enough to be realised
  in silicon, fine enough to exclude the lookup table.
- *Fine / microphysical / biological.* The role includes spike timing, neurochemistry, and the
  physical realiser itself. This is the most restrictive setting, charged with being "too
  chauvinistic," since it collapses toward [proteocentrism](../positions/proteocentrism.md), on which
  carbon biochemistry does real individuative work. This is horn 2 of the grain dilemma.

### Where to set the dial

The concept earns its keep because a single dispute keeps recurring at it.

- The [grain dilemma](../arguments/role-grain-dilemma.md) argues that no setting works: coarse is too
  liberal, fine too chauvinistic.
- The reply of [organisational role-grain](../claims/role-grain-is-organisational.md) and
  [the organisational-grain middle](../arguments/organisational-grain-dissolves-dilemma.md) is that the
  organisational setting is a principled, independently motivated middle — the grain at which
  cognitive science actually quantifies.
- The counter-reply, [the dense China-brain regress](../arguments/dense-china-brain-regress.md) and its
  conclusion [the organisational grain's own
  dilemma](../claims/organisational-grain-faces-own-grain-dilemma.md), presses that even the middle fails: a dense [Chinese Nation](chinese-nation.md)
  matches the organisation yet intuitively lacks a mind, so the grain either readmits the absurd or
  smuggles sub-organisational detail back in.

### Grain for thought versus grain for feeling

A distinction the debate sometimes blurs: grain is a knob on *functional description*. It bears on
two separate questions. First, the individuation of *cognitive* and *intentional* states — belief,
inference — where the organisational setting is strong and even critics tend to concede it. Second,
whether *any* setting suffices for *phenomenal* states, the what-it-is-like of experience. The
phenomenal-residue worry ([phenomenal residue](../claims/phenomenal-states-not-exhausted-by-role.md),
[china-nation-absent-qualia](../arguments/china-nation-absent-qualia.md), [explanatory-gap](../arguments/explanatory-gap.md)) is precisely the claim that
no setting of this knob captures qualia at all — a different complaint from "the dial is set wrong."

### In plain terms

Suppose you agree a mind is defined by what it *does* rather than what it's made of. There's still
a catch: *how exactly* do you describe what it does? Very roughly ("takes in questions, gives
sensible answers")? Or in fine detail ("these exact signals trigger those, with this precise
timing")? That choice of detail-level is the "grain" — like the resolution on a screen, coarse or
fine. It matters enormously, because it quietly decides who counts as having a mind. Describe a
mind too coarsely and almost anything qualifies — even a giant cheat-sheet that just looks up
canned answers. Describe it too finely and only a biological brain can match it, so no computer
ever could. A lot of the fight over whether machines can think comes down to one question: where do
you set this dial — and can you justify *that* setting without just tuning it to get the answer you
already wanted?
